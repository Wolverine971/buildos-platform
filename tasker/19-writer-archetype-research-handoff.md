## Required Research Questions

### 1. Where Writers Hang Out

Find the high-signal online places where writers discuss process, pain, publishing, critique, marketing, rejection, and finishing.

Start with:

- Reddit: `r/writing`, `r/writers`, `r/selfpublish`, `r/PubTips`, `r/fantasywriters`, `r/DestructiveReaders`, `r/Screenwriting`, genre-specific writing subs.
- Forums/communities: Absolute Write Water Cooler, KBoards Writers' Cafe, Scribophile, Critique Circle, NaNoWriMo forums, QueryTracker forums/comments, Substack writer communities, Discords if publicly indexed.
- Professional/authority sites: Jane Friedman, Writer Beware, Reedsy, The Creative Penn, QueryTracker, Manuscript Wish List, Publishers Marketplace where accessible, Alliance of Independent Authors, Authors Guild.
- Craft/media sources: Writing Excuses, Brandon Sanderson lectures, Mary Robinette Kowal, Ursula K. Le Guin essays/interviews, George Saunders, Anne Lamott, Stephen King's `On Writing`, Save the Cat Writes a Novel, Story Grid, Helping Writers Become Authors.

For each source/community, note:

- Who it is for.
- What language they use.
- What problems repeat.
- Whether it is usable for observation, outreach, or only background.
- Any community rules around self-promotion, surveys, AI, critique, or market research.

### 2. Native Terms And Concepts

Find terms writers already use and terms BuildOS might redefine.

Look for:

- Craft terms: draft, scene, chapter, beat, arc, premise, theme, voice, POV, stakes, conflict, outline, pantser, plotter, plantser, revision, line edit, developmental edit, copyedit, proofread, beta reader, critique partner, sensitivity reader, synopsis, query, comps, manuscript, WIP, trunk novel, worldbuilding bible.
- Emotional terms: writer's block, impostor syndrome, shiny new idea, inner critic, messy middle, sagging middle, second-book syndrome, rejection, query trenches, trunked manuscript, abandoned draft, burnout, shame spiral.
- Publishing terms: query letter, agent, slush pile, partial/full request, revise and resubmit, offer of representation, submission, acquisitions, advance, royalties, ISBN, ARC, launch team, newsletter, backlist, KDP, KU, wide, trad pub, hybrid, vanity press.
- Workflow terms: Scrivener, Google Docs sprawl, Notion boards, index cards, corkboard, character sheets, timeline, revision pass, manuscript tracker, word-count spreadsheet.

Create a table of:

- Term.
- What writers currently mean by it.
- What pain is hiding inside it.
- How BuildOS might reframe it without sounding like an outsider.

## Citation And Source Standards

- Browse current sources. Do not rely only on memory.
- Cite sources inline with Markdown links.
- Prefer primary sources where possible:
    - Official community rules.
    - Authoritative blogs.
    - Publisher/agent resources.
    - Public forum threads.
    - Official NaNoWriMo/QueryTracker/Reedsy/Writer Beware pages.
- Reddit/forum citations can be paraphrased. Avoid long verbatim quotes unless needed.
- Separate source-backed claims from interpretation.
- Mark any blocked/inaccessible source as blocked instead of pretending it was read.

## Research Method

Do three passes:

1. **Broad scan** — gather the source map and repeated language.
2. **Deeper language/pain scan** — pull out names, terms, pain cycles, comeback arcs, cast/settings.
3. **BuildOS synthesis** — convert research into naming, redefinitions, product surfaces, and later archetype rewrite opportunities.

Keep noise out:

- Generic "just write every day" advice.
- AI-content-mill advice.
- Naive publishing advice that ignores scams, rights, agents, or market realities.
- Motivational quotes without workflow insight.
- Productivity advice that does not understand manuscripts.
