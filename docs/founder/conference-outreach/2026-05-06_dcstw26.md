## Why this draft

- **Bridge in sentence 2**, not a mission line. "Agent and context-engineering work in the DMV" is the concrete factual overlap with the AI track — local + exact subject matter.
- **BuildOS leads, Curri supports, Marine + DoD anchor DMV credibility.** The "USMC Scout Sniper Team Leader" line earns its place at a DC conference where it wouldn't at a generic startup. Different audience, different credential to surface.
- **Talk thesis paragraph is the actual pitch.** Has tension ("agents don't need better prompts, they need a shared workspace") so they can imagine the room. Without it, this is just a resume — they need a hook.
- **Honest gap reframed as positioning, not apology.** "Engineer-who-actually-shipped-it version" makes the gap a feature.
- **Soft CTA + acknowledgment of their process.** "Happy to fill out the application — just wanted to put a face on it first" respects their workflow without asking them to skip it.
- **No bullets, no bold, no headers in the message body.** Prose only, per voice rules.

## Considerations

- **Who to address.** Both Rachel and Darius signed the email. Darius is Director of Programs and Community per the RealLIST mention — likely the right person for speakers specifically. Could swap to "Hey Darius" alone if biasing toward the actual decision-maker.
- **Alternative honest-gap line** (if "$5M ARR" reads too rehearsed):
    - _"Heads up — I'm an engineer first, not a stage founder. The talk would be the practitioner version."_
    - _"Heads up — this'd be the practitioner version, not the VC pitch version."_
- **Talk durability.** Conference is October 2026 (~5 months out). The agentic landscape will move significantly. The "shared context layer between humans and agents" thesis survives that drift; specific tool / model references would not. Keep the talk pitched at the thesis level.
- **Anti-AI marketing strategy doesn't apply here.** This is a technical conference audience — leading with "agentic" and "context engineering" is appropriate. The "thinking environment" public framing is for end users, not engineers.

## Talk outline (draft, not in the email)

Working title options:

- _Agents Don't Need Better Prompts. They Need a Workspace._
- _The Shared Context Layer: Where Humans and Agents Actually Meet_
- _Building an Agent Harness in Production: What I Learned Solo Shipping BuildOS_

Possible structure:

1. **The chatbot dead-end.** Why "talk to the AI" is the wrong primitive for real work.
2. **Context engineering, in practice.** What actually breaks in prod — multi-LLM routing, tool surfaces, JSON-mode failures, schema drift.
3. **Harness design.** The difference between an agent that works once and one that works reliably. Tool selection, context budgets, retries, fallbacks.
4. **The shared-surface thesis.** Humans and agents reading and writing the same project state. Why this is the real unlock.
5. **Where this is going.** MCP, agent-native apps, what teams should be building toward.
