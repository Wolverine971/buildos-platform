# Red Rover K-12 — Product & Market Research

Prepped for CTO interview on 2026-05-26. Source: company site, EdTech press, PitchBook/Crunchbase, ProTeacher forums, EdTech Digest, GovTech.

## 1. What Red Rover does

Red Rover is a **K-12 Human Capital Management (HCM) suite** — five tightly-integrated modules sold to school districts:

1. **Absence Management** — the founding product. Substitute placement engine that texts qualified subs, fills openings automatically, supports partial-day and support-staff absences. Direct AESOP / Frontline replacement.
2. **Time Tracking** — employee time + extra-duty comp.
3. **Hiring** — modern ATS for K-12 recruiting.
4. **Professional Development Management** — launched Oct 2025. CEU tracking, compliance, teacher growth.
5. **Records** — launched March 2026. Centralized employee documents/data, replacing paper forms, Google Forms, JotForm patchworks.

Positioning is **"You Deserve Better"** — explicitly anti-do-it-all-platform. They sell focused modules with a clean UX and a 13-second average support response time as a differentiator. Native cross-module integration (one login, one record across absence/time/hiring/PD/records) is the moat versus point solutions.

## 2. Who buys it

- **US public school districts**, K-12. **1,900+ districts across 49 states** as of mid-2026 (was 400+ in late 2023, 1,300+ in 2024 — roughly 2-3x growth per year).
- Sizes range from small rural (New Holstein WI, Vashon Island WA) to mid/large districts (Buncombe County NC, Leander ISD TX, Lake County FL, Adlai E. Stevenson HS District 125 IL, CNUSD CA).
- They've also disclosed a NYC Public Schools integration page — that's a top-5-district-in-the-country footprint, though scope is unclear.
- **Buyer personas**: HR Director, HR Coordinator, Payroll Assistant, Director of Operations. Procurement is district-level, not building-level.
- Appears **US-only** — no international footprint signaled.

## 3. Market position & competitors

The K-12 absence-management market is a **Frontline Education near-monopoly being actively eroded**.

- **Frontline Education** — 80,000 schools / 7,000+ districts. Their Absence Management product (rebranded from AESOP, the historical category leader) covers 4,000+ districts. PE-owned, considered legacy/clunky by customers.
- **SmartFind Express** (PowerSchool / Eduspire) — the other incumbent. Often bundled with SIS deals.
- **SubAssistant**, ESS, Kelly Education (services not software) — smaller niche players.

**Red Rover's wedge**: modern UX, fast support, mobile-first sub app, transparent pricing posture, and a "rip-and-replace Frontline" sales motion. ProTeacher forum threads literally titled "Switching from Frontline to Red Rover" — that's the migration story they're winning on. Subs describe the experience as comparable functionally but with better push notifications and a cleaner mobile flow. Districts cite up to **40% cost reduction** vs incumbents and claim near-100% daily fill rates.

EdTech Digest finalist for **Best Administrative Solution, EdTech Awards 2025**. Earned coverage in GovTech, EdTech Digest, HRTechEdge, MyChesCo.

## 4. Pricing model

Not publicly listed — **"contact sales" custom quotes**. The mobile app is free for subs and employees. Industry pattern (and what Frontline does) is **annual contract, per-district, scaled by employee count / FTE**, often with per-module add-ons. Red Rover's "transparent pricing" marketing language is positioning against Frontline's opaque enterprise bundles — but they still don't publish a number, so it's a sales-led motion, not self-serve.

Net: **admins love it, subs tolerate it** — which is the right side of the buying equation.

## 8. Where they sit in the K-12 SaaS landscape

- **Vertical SaaS, HCM layer.** Sibling categories: SIS (PowerSchool, Infinite Campus), LMS (Canvas, Schoology), Finance/ERP (Tyler, Skyward), Comms (ParentSquare, Remind). Red Rover doesn't touch students — it's **operations infrastructure for the adults running the building**.
- **Mid-market sweet spot**: small/mid districts (500-15K students) where Frontline feels overweight and SmartFind feels stale. Pushing up-market via PD/Records + Principal Advisor hires — clearly trying to land large districts now that the suite is broader.
- **Tailwinds**: post-COVID substitute shortage made absence/sub-fill a board-level metric. Teacher retention crisis makes PD + Hiring + Records a defensible suite story. K-12 IT budgets are sticky and federal ESSER replacement spending is still rolling.
- **Risks**: Frontline can cut price; PowerSchool can bundle SmartFind into SIS renewals; districts are slow-moving, long sales cycles, summer-driven implementation windows.

## Talking-points cheat sheet for the CTO call

- **They're a vertical HCM scale-up, not a startup** — 189 people, BPC-backed, ~1,900 districts. Engineering work is about scaling a multi-tenant SaaS across districts with hard data-isolation, SSO/SIS integrations, mobile sub apps, and growing module surface area.
- **The CTO (Mike Sheldon) is a founder** — he built the absence-management engine. Likely cares about: schedule-optimization algorithms, notification reliability, integration plumbing (SIS, payroll, SSO), mobile performance, data model unification across 5 modules.
- **Their moat is execution + UX + support**, not novel tech. Talk about reliability, integration discipline, and shipping cleanly — not LLM moonshots.
- **DJ's angle**: logistics ops at Curri = matching supply (subs) to demand (absences) in real time with mobile notifications. That's literally the Red Rover core loop. The parallel sells itself.
