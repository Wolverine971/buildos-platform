# Red Rover K-12 — Funding, Leadership, and Momentum

**Prepared:** 2026-05-25 (interview 2026-05-26 with CTO Mike Sheldon)
**Referral:** Jon Chua
**Company:** Red Rover Technologies — K-12 human capital management SaaS (absence management, time tracking, recruiting/hiring, professional development)
**HQ:** Exton, PA (559 W Uwchlan Ave, Suite 200) — remote-first
**Founded:** 2020
**Scale claims:** 1,700–1,900 school districts across 45–49 states; ~189–203 employees (LinkedIn / ZoomInfo)

## 1. Funding history

Red Rover K-12 is **lightly capitalized for its scale**. The only publicly confirmed institutional financing event is:

- **Brighton Park Capital growth equity investment — 2024.** Amount not disclosed. Brighton Park is a Greenwich, CT growth-equity firm founded 2019, focused on software/healthcare/tech-enabled services. Sam Kentor (Partner) is the named deal lead. Source: [bpc.com/companies/red-rover](https://www.bpc.com/companies/red-rover).
- **No seed, Series A, or Series B publicly recorded** under the K-12 entity in Crunchbase / EdSurge / press releases.
- **Important disambiguation:** Tracxn/PitchBook references showing a "$50M / $35M Series B / Oppenheimer 2022" are for a **different company — Red Rover Moving and Storage** (PRNewswire confirms Oppenheimer's $15M into the moving company in 2021). A Crunchbase "$14.8M Series A April 2024" hit is **Red Rover Interactive** (Oslo/UK game studio). Neither is the K-12 company.
- **No PE roll-up. No public acquisitions** by Red Rover, and no signs Red Rover has been acquired or shopped. Brighton Park is growth equity, not a Vista/Thoma Bravo-style roll-up vehicle — they take meaningful minority/structured positions in profitable, growing software businesses.
- **Revenue / ARR:** Not public. Implied scale: 1,700+ districts × typical K-12 HR SaaS ACV ($15–40k blended) suggests ARR in the $25–60M range, but this is inferred, not sourced. Brighton Park-stage businesses are usually $20M+ ARR with 80%+ gross margins and clean unit economics.

Bottom line: founder-led 2020 startup that grew to 1,300+ districts largely on revenue, then took a single growth round in 2024 to accelerate. Not venture-treadmill; not PE-owned.

## 4. K-12 SaaS market context

- **Market is cold for venture, hot for M&A.** EdTech VC funding fell to $2.4B in 2024 (lowest in a decade, -88% from 2021 peak); Q1 2025 was $410M vs $580M Q1 2024.
- **But strategic M&A is at record highs.** **Bain Capital closed PowerSchool acquisition at $5.6B (late 2024).** **Roper acquired Frontline from Thoma Bravo for $3.725B (2022).** Curriculum Associates, Instructure, Renaissance all actively rolling up.
- **Red Rover sits in the absence-management category where Frontline (now Roper) is the incumbent** — Frontline's Aesop has ~4,000+ of ~14,000 US districts. Red Rover is the modern challenger, eating Frontline's lunch by being purpose-built rather than acquired-stack.
- **Typical exit path:** Either a Roper/PowerSchool strategic at 15–20x EBITDA, or a PE roll-up sponsor (Vista, Thoma Bravo, Bain). Brighton Park is positioned to either ride to that exit or take a control round later.

## What this means for the conversation

You're walking into a **founder-led, growth-equity-backed, profitable K-12 SaaS** — not a venture rocket and not a PE roll-up. Brighton Park backs disciplined operators, which means Sheldon almost certainly cares about **shipping velocity against a clear product roadmap, customer-driven feature work, and a maintainable architecture his ~25–40 engineers can run** — not moonshots or AI theater. Sheldon's pedigree is **Frontline → Propel → Red Rover**, so he has watched the incumbent (Frontline) get rolled up twice and is deliberately building the modern, unified replacement; expect him to value engineers who think in terms of platform cohesion, intuitive UX, and durable systems over hype-driven stacks. The right posture: pragmatic senior engineer who ships, owns outcomes, and understands K-12 customers are real humans with real workflows.
