## The Central Premise

Saarinen explores why quality remains elusive despite technological advancement. He traces this to a recurring cycle: craft flourishes initially, technology enables faster creation, speed becomes prioritized over care, and quality eventually gets missed — triggering a revival of craftsmanship.

## The Craft Cycle

The essay identifies a pattern repeating throughout history. As Saarinen notes, even in 1927, writer Earnest Elmo Calkins observed how new technologies enabled "the bad design in incredible quantities." The cycle shows craft → technology → speed-focus → quality decline → renewed longing for craft.

## AI's Impact

Unlike previous tools, AI represents a fundamental shift. Rather than just accelerating labor, it attempts to outsource judgment itself. The core tension: "Technology makes it faster to build, but harder to care."

## Quality as Business Strategy

Contrary to conventional wisdom, Saarinen argues quality _is_ scalable and profitable. Linear's trajectory demonstrates this — reaching profitability by year two and 10,000+ paying customers with minimal marketing through organic advocacy powered by product excellence.

## Linear's Quality Framework

The company operationalizes quality through:

- Quality as north star (not just speed metrics)
- Intuition and user feedback over pure data
- Hiring for demonstrated craft
- Small teams with judgment authority
- No assembly-line handoffs
- Internal-only MVPs
- Zero-bug policies (7-day fix window)

## The Fundamental Message

Quality ultimately reflects a choice — one individuals and teams make daily, regardless of market pressures toward speed and cost-cutting.
