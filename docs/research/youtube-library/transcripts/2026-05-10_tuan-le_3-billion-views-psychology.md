# What Getting 3 Billion Views Taught Me About Human Psychology

## Six Principles (Outline)

1. **Your brain decides before you do** — mere exposure effect; format steal beats originality.
2. **The curiosity trap** — open a knowledge gap in the first 2s; never lead with the product.
3. **People buy identities, not products** — means-end chain theory; speak to layer 3 (psychological values), not layer 1 (attributes).
4. **The credential shortcut** — authority bypasses critical thinking in the first 2s.
5. **People share what makes them look good** — design for the sharer's reputation, not the viewer's enjoyment.
6. **Hijack the brain with stories** — Hook → Problem → Story → Payoff; cut anything not delivering new info; captions mandatory.

## Case Receipts

- **Bulldock (Korean instant ramen)**: 300K → 1.8M followers, 900M views in 12 months. Stole spicy-challenge / reaction formats; product unchanged.
- **Stan (creator platform)**: First video 1M views TikTok / 5M LinkedIn. Opened with "How much do you pay for rent?" office tour. 20M views in months for the CEO's personal brand.
- **Japanese restaurant**: 200–300 views → 1.8M, 2M back-to-back. 300K followers in 3 months. Format change: cinematic shots → "hey chef, can you make me something gourmet?" The chef's outfit + pro kitchen _was_ the credential.
