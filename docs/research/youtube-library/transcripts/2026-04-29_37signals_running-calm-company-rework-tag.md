## Episode Index (Calm Company tag)

### 0112 — A Dose of Empathy (2021-03-02)

With Marcy Capron Vermillion and Maia Reed. Equilibria's CBD dosage specialists do one-on-one consultations with customers. During the pandemic, this team has taken on an unprecedented amount of customer support — bearing witness to the heightened stress and anxiety that their customers face.

### 0103 — Take Some Time Off, We Mean It! (2020-11-10)

With Dan Jimenez. Unlimited paid time off is a common perk in the tech industry, but an open-ended vacation policy led to confusion and even burnout. Dan Jimenez of Chatbooks discusses how they shifted from unlimited to mandatory PTO, and how they're recalibrating.

### 0097 — Something's Broken (2020-09-22)

With Jeremy Daer and Troy Toman. Basecamp recently suffered three outages in a week. Programming lead Jeremy Daer and director of operations Troy Toman discuss their approach to customer communication around these incidents, including public accountability, mental health, and the human side of crisis management.

### 0075 — Work, Rest, and What You Will (2020-03-10)

With Emily Twarog. Labor historian Emily Twarog explains the origins of the 8-hour work day and why it was so short-lived in the U.S., exploring the history behind workplace standards that Basecamp advocates for today.

### 0070 — The Road Out Of Startupland (2020-02-04)

With Sahil Lavingia. Sahil Lavingia reflects on his journey through Silicon Valley — from unicorn aspirations and $8 million in venture capital to a "very different outcome" — offering perspective on life outside startup culture.

### 0062 — Calm in the Political Storm (2019-11-26)

With Marty Santalucia. Workplace cultures in politics and tech share similarities where "overwork is glorified; long hours are the norm; employees are expected to respond instantly." Marty Santalucia explores alternatives to these practices.

### 0050 — The Cult of Overwork (2019-09-03)

With Ty Fujimura. Ty Fujimura, president of web design firm Cantilever, discusses how he escaped the Cult of Overwork and rethinks the relationship between hours worked and actual productivity.

### 0046 — Hire When it Hurts (2019-07-16)

With Jason Fried, David Heinemeier Hansson, Andrea LaRowe, Kristin Aardsma, and Jeremy Daer. Basecamp explores its hiring practices while adding five new employees and receiving over 4,000 applications, diving deep into how the company knew it was time to hire and why.

### 0038 — Did You Order the Code Red? (2019-03-26)

With Jim Mackenzie and Jeremy Daer. Jeremy from Basecamp's Security, Infrastructure, and Performance team discusses handling a mass-login attack, exploring the statement that "passwords just aren't cutting it online."

### 0037 — Hiring Is Not Hazing (2019-03-12)

With Aja Hammerly. Aja Hammerly, a developer advocate at Google, addresses drawbacks of common tech interview techniques like whiteboard coding and trivia questions, sharing tips for better hiring practices.

### 0035 — No Hard Feelings (2019-02-12)

With Liz Fosslien and Mollie West Duffy. Co-authors of _No Hard Feelings: The Secret Power of Embracing Emotions At Work_ discuss how the future of work is emotional and how organizations can foster healthier workplace environments.

### 0033 — Big Integer (2019-01-15)

With David Heinemeier Hansson, Taylor Weibley, Kristin Aardsma, and Jeremy Daer. On November 8, Basecamp 3 went down for almost five hours — the worst outage in a decade. This episode goes inside the company to examine practices around internal communication, customer support, and calm work.

### 0032 — Happy Pacifists (2018-12-18)

With David Wood, Joshua Guble, and Elizabeth Gramm. Two professors share research on how business rhetoric rife with "the language of war" affects human behavior and workplace culture, advocating for alternative approaches.

### 0031 — Sometimes It's Crazy At Work (2018-12-04)

With Jason Fried, David Heinemeier Hansson, and Adam Stoddard. Following the launch of _It Doesn't Have to be Crazy at Work_, this episode explores what happened when the book's successful launch faced unexpected complications.

### 0027.5 — It Doesn't Have to be Crazy at Work — Part 2 (2018-10-16)

With David Heinemeier Hansson. DHH discusses taking a calm approach to writing and marketing the new book on managing workplace culture.

### 0027 — It Doesn't Have to be Crazy at Work — Part 1 (2018-10-09)

With David Heinemeier Hansson. Jason Fried and David Heinemeier Hansson's new book pushes back against "the toxic culture of overwork and unhealthy ambitions" driving modern workplaces.

### 0024 — Take a Break (2018-07-24)

With Jason Fried, Adeline Koh, and Rachel Winard. Three business owners discuss sabbaticals, including Adeline Koh of Sabbatical Beauty and Jason Fried, sharing how extended leave shapes entrepreneurial thinking.

### 0016 — How to Say You're Sorry (2018-03-20)

With David Heinemeier Hansson, Susan McCarthy, Robin Geall, and Robert Rawlins. Exploring why saying sorry is difficult for businesses, this episode features apology experts examining what's changed in public accountability practices.

### 0014 — Meetings Are Toxic (2018-02-20)

With Dan Kim and Dan Miller. This episode collects stories about terrible meetings, examining how they're "held too frequently, run too long, and involve more people than necessary."

### 0013 — Interruption is Not Collaboration (2018-02-06)

With Thomas Fritz, Dave Shepherd, Noah Lorang, and Bryan Miles. Academic researchers, Basecamp's head data analyst, and a remote company CEO discuss workplace interruptions and building healthier communication cultures.

### 0002 — Workaholics Aren't Heroes (2017-08-29)

With Josh Tsui, Chase Clemons, and Sylvia Maldonado. Challenging the glorification of exhaustion, this episode features a video game industry veteran and others discussing why "being tired isn't a badge of honor."
