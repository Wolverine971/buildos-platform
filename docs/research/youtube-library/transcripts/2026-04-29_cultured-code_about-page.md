## Company Overview

Cultured Code is a productivity software company headquartered in Stuttgart, Germany. The organization comprises 12 team members representing diverse international backgrounds, including Germany, Poland, France, Czechia, Brazil, Australia, Canada, and the United States.

## Design Philosophy

The company emphasizes three core principles:

> "We're passionate about productivity, simplicity, and beautiful design."

These values are integrated into Things, their award-winning task management application.

## Product Offering

Things is available across Apple's ecosystem, running on Mac, iPad, iPhone, Apple Watch, and Apple Vision Pro devices.

## Employment Approach

Rather than maintaining a traditional hiring pipeline, Cultured Code welcomes unsolicited applications from exceptionally skilled professionals interested in contributing to Things. Prospective candidates may submit cover letters and résumés in English or German.

## Werner Jainek (Founder) — design philosophy quotes captured from interviews

> "[The app] should disappear ideally during use."

> "[Things] is a very personal tool, a conversation with oneself."

> "When organizing, you must be careful not to over-organize."

(These quotes are sourced from Lars Bobach interview and other reporting; Jainek explains why some features like folders and subfolders for projects were deliberately excluded — making tasks harder to find. Only projects and areas remain for sorting tasks.)

## Notes For BuildOS

The Cultured Code design philosophy fits squarely in the calm-software school: tools that disappear during use, deliberately under-organized, treated as a conversation between the user and themselves rather than as a system to be configured. This is the same school as the BuildOS anti-feed positioning, and the opposite of the "delight grid + 50-40-10" delight school.
