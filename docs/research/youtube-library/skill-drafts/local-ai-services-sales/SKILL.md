# Local AI Services Sales

Use this skill when an agent helps package AI or automation work for local businesses. The agent should sell business outcomes such as reviews, calls, appointments, reactivated leads, and follow-up speed. Claude Code, CRM automation, voice agents, and enrichment tools are implementation details.

## When to Use

- Create an offer ladder for local service businesses
- Sell AI appointment setting, SMS follow-up, review requests, or lead reactivation
- Turn messy CRM exports, paper lists, or old leads into outreach campaigns
- Build local SEO or Google Business Profile service packages
- Draft hand-raiser posts or 5-minute Loom sales videos
- Decide what to offer first to a skeptical small-business owner

Do not use this skill to pitch generic "AI transformation" or software subscriptions without a concrete revenue path.

## Core Offer Ladder

Move from low fear to high trust.

1. **Review rebate.** Contact satisfied past customers and ask for reviews, often with a coupon or next-service incentive. Offer the first small batch free or performance-based.
2. **Lead reactivation.** Reach out to old inquiries, abandoned leads, or inactive customers and route interested replies into appointments.
3. **AI appointment setter.** Install a text or voice setter that handles inbound speed-to-lead, follow-up, booking, and missed-lead recovery.
4. **Local visibility expansion.** For businesses with capacity, improve or expand local search coverage through compliant Google Business Profile, location page, review, and citation work.

## Positioning Rules

- Sell reviews, booked calls, reactivated revenue, and missed-lead recovery.
- Do not lead with Claude Code, AI agents, Retell, Twilio, GoHighLevel, Supabase, or n8n.
- Use AI privately to clean data, deploy infrastructure, personalize messages, and run follow-up.
- Start with the offer that creates the least buyer anxiety.
- Make the first result easy to verify.

## Campaign Workflow

1. **Qualify the business.** Confirm the business has demand, customer history, operational capacity, and a service where one more booked job matters.
2. **Choose the offer rung.** Start with review rebate if trust is low, lead reactivation if they have old leads, setter if they already feel missed-lead pain, local SEO if geography is the distribution ceiling.
3. **Audit data reality.** Expect messy CRMs, scanned paper, missing fields, old numbers, and inconsistent notes.
4. **Clean and segment.** Filter out unhappy customers, stale leads beyond the useful window, duplicates, and wrong-fit contacts.
5. **Draft short personalized outreach.** Keep SMS or DM copy concise. Personalize only what the data supports: name, date, service, location, or prior inquiry.
6. **Preview before generating.** Show sample output before producing a full CSV or campaign file.
7. **Route replies.** Configure the setter or human handoff for interested, angry, confused, and wrong-number replies.
8. **Measure the result.** Track reviews landed, leads reactivated, calls booked, show rate, close rate, and deliverability issues.
9. **Upsell only from proof.** Use the first result to justify the next rung.

## Async Sales Video

For selling the service without a live sales call:

1. Open with the outcome, not the service.
2. Demonstrate what the buyer gets: call recording, dashboard, booked appointment, review, or lead reply.
3. State the price with the quantified outcome.
4. Explain exactly how to buy or start.
5. End with a question channel.

Use custom videos until the offer gets a few yeses. Then convert the objections into one evergreen video and personalize the wrapper message.

## Local SEO Guardrails

- Do not recommend fake locations, virtual offices, P.O. boxes, or duplicate profiles.
- If expanding Google Business Profiles, require real legal entities or DBAs, real offices, unique phone numbers, and matching location pages.
- Review requests should go to real customers only.
- Split review requests geographically when supporting multiple real locations.
- Local visibility work only helps if the business can close and fulfill the extra demand.

## Stop Conditions

Stop and ask for human review if:

- The campaign would contact unhappy customers
- The business has no consent basis or unclear contact rights
- The local SEO plan risks violating platform guidelines
- The buyer wants unverifiable AI claims
- The agent is about to send messages, launch a campaign, or publish outreach
