# Investor, Founder, PR, and Specialty Outreach

Read this when the user is not running a standard B2B sales campaign: investor fundraising, founder-to-founder notes, recruiting, podcast guest pitches, PR, partnerships, or customer research.

## Investor Fundraising

Governing source: Michael Seibel / Y Combinator, with support from YC fundraising material, NFX storytelling, and local fundraising analyses.

Goal: start a back-and-forth. Do not force a meeting before explaining the company.

Payload:

- Problem.
- Solution.
- Launch status.
- Traction or growth, if any.
- Market size or why it can be large.
- Cofounders and technical ability.
- Contrarian insight.
- Optional standard startup deck.

Rules:

- Readable in under 60 seconds.
- Plain language.
- No long origin story.
- No jargon.
- No hidden company description.
- No meeting-first ask.
- Named founder sender at company domain.
- Slow follow-up; do not hammer after confirmed opens.

CTA examples:

- "Does this fit what you like to see?"
- "Happy to send more if useful."
- "Would it be worth sending the deck?"
- "Open to feedback if this is in your lane."

## Founder-to-Founder

Use when a founder is writing to another founder, creator, operator, or peer.

Best frame:

- Direct.
- Specific.
- Low ego.
- Proof of real attention.
- Small ask.

Good CTAs:

- "Want me to send the note?"
- "Mind if I share the draft?"
- "Open to a quick gut check?"
- "Worth sending over?"

Founder-led outreach can use more personality than SDR outreach, but it still needs a real bridge. Do not hide a sales pitch behind fake peer warmth.

## Warm-via-Content

Use Justin Welsh-style warm-up when cold email is not urgent:

- Publish useful content for the target audience.
- Reply and engage before asking.
- Use owned audience or visible expertise as trust support.
- Email only after the recipient has a reason to see the sender as relevant.

This is not a replacement for cold outreach. It is a pre-warming layer for founder-led markets.

## Recruiting

Candidate outreach is not sales copy. It should feel candidate-centered.

Include:

- Why this specific person.
- Why the role is meaningfully relevant to their trajectory.
- One concrete role detail.
- Honest compensation/location/level constraints when available.
- Small CTA: "open to seeing the role note?"

Avoid:

- Generic "impressive background."
- Hiding role details.
- Over-selling company mission before relevance.
- Calendar link first.

## Podcast / PR Pitch

Recipient psychology: they are protecting their audience.

Include:

- Specific recent episode/article/beat.
- Why your angle fits that audience now.
- 2-3 concrete topic angles.
- Proof you will not waste the host/editor's time.
- Small CTA: "want the angle list?"

Avoid:

- "I love your show" without a specific reference.
- Long bio first.
- Generic founder story.
- Hidden sales motive.

## Partnerships and Customer Research

Partnership outreach:

- Anchor in complementary audience, channel, product, or event.
- Offer one clear collaboration artifact.
- Make the first yes exploratory and small.

Customer research:

- Be honest that it is research.
- Offer a useful counter-gift: summary, benchmark, early access, donation, or artifact.
- Do not disguise research as a sales call.

## Specialty Mode Guardrails

- Investor: no meeting-first ask.
- Recruiting: no fake flattery.
- PR/podcast: audience fit before sender credentials.
- Founder-to-founder: direct and specific beats performative warmth.
- Research: disclose research intent.
- Partnership: one collaboration idea, not a menu of possibilities.
