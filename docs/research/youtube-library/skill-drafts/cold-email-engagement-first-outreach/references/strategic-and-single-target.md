# Strategic and Single-Target Outreach

Read this when the recipient is high-value, named, senior, or relationship-sensitive. This reference governs founder-to-founder outreach, executive sales, partnership asks, recruiting a named candidate, podcast/PR pitches, and customer research asks.

## Governing Sources

- Sam McKenna: Show Me You Know Me, executive research, authenticity.
- Connor Murray: three-part strategic body, assumptive language, 4-touch cadence.
- Florin Tatulea / 30MPC: packaging, proof, artifact CTA, AI-assisted research.
- Sam McKenna Apollo analysis: hyper-specific subject, preview, objection preemption, respectful CTA.

## Research Bar

Minimum shippable anchor is Level 3:

- Level 3: specific post, article, hire, initiative, public trigger, or named detail.
- Level 4: quote from podcast/talk/interview or detailed buyer language.
- Level 5: real mutual contact or insider context.

Do not ship Level 0-2 anchors for strategic outreach. Re-dig instead.

## Research Workflow

1. Find the public or relationship anchor.
2. Verify it is real and recent enough to matter.
3. Identify why it matters to the recipient.
4. Connect it to the reason for outreach.
5. Draft the bridge before writing the pitch.

Useful research surfaces:

- LinkedIn profile and posts.
- Personal site or founder letter.
- Recent podcast or webinar.
- Company news and product launches.
- Recent hires and open roles.
- Earnings call, 10-K, shareholder letter.
- Last 10 articles or episodes for creators/podcasters.
- Portfolio/thesis for investors.

## Packaging

Subject plus preview is the first conversion point.

Subject styles:

- Internal-looking noun: `enterprise ramp`
- Direct-report names: `Mark and Larry`
- Initiative or trigger: `north america expansion`
- Hyper-specific SMYKM hook: only when the recipient will recognize it instantly.

Preview:

- Extend the subject.
- Mention the anchor early.
- Do not repeat the subject.

Reject:

- "Quick question"
- "Loved your post"
- "[Company] x [Company]"
- Generic trigger everyone else will use.
- Personal hook with no business bridge.

## Body Anatomy

Use this order:

1. Anchor: specific, real, recent.
2. Bridge: why that anchor makes this email relevant.
3. Problem/opportunity: what risk, cost, or desire this creates.
4. Proof: peer, customer, artifact, or "seen this before."
5. Offer/artifact: what you can send or do.
6. CTA: smallest useful yes.

For strategic B2B, the artifact should be useful enough for a Mobilizer to forward internally:

- Diagnostic.
- Risk map.
- Tradeoff memo.
- Benchmark cut.
- Buying-committee question set.
- Internal narrative draft.

For a more formal enterprise note, use Connor Murray's three-part body:

1. Who I am.
2. Why I am relevant.
3. What I want.

## Authenticity Bridge

Audit every personal or public-context hook:

```text
If this hook were removed, would the reason for outreach still make sense?
```

If yes, the hook may be decorative. Rewrite until the anchor causes the outreach.

Bad:

```text
Saw you like Rush. We sell sales training.
```

Better:

```text
Saw your interview where you described sales onboarding as "getting the whole band in time." That is why I am writing: we found two onboarding gaps that usually show up when teams scale past 20 reps.
```

## Proof Slot

Use proof only when credible:

- Similar buyer role.
- Peer company in the same situation.
- Specific artifact you already pulled.
- Named customer result with permission/evidence.
- Real mutual contact.

Do not use vague "teams like yours" or unsupported outcomes.

## CTA Menu

Pick the smallest useful yes:

- "Want me to send the note?"
- "Mind if I send the teardown?"
- "Can I send the 3 signals?"
- "Worth sending the example?"
- "Open to 15 min if I send the summary first?"
- For strong-fit strategic sales: "What does availability look like next week?"

Avoid calendar links in first touch unless the user explicitly accepts the risk.

## Cadence

Strategic:

- Day 0: initial.
- Day +2: benefit-of-the-doubt follow-up.
- Day +4: ask for thoughts.
- Day +6: close-loop or "better next month?"

Single-target:

- 4 touches max.
- Keep the original thread.
- Follow-ups point back to the original context.
- Do not write a fresh pitch every time.
- For senior executives, test Thursday/Friday and weekend sends carefully.

## Objection Preemption

When the likely objection is obvious, preempt one inside the first email.

Use:

- "You may already have [X], but the gap I usually see is [Y]."
- "This may not be a priority this quarter; if so, I can send the benchmark and close the loop."

Do not preempt three objections. One is enough.

## Buyer-Choice Bridge

Use the Dunford/Moesta frame when the offer is more than a quick artifact:

```text
When teams hit [struggling moment], they usually choose between [alternative A] and [alternative B].
The tradeoff I noticed is [specific tradeoff].
I can send the short note if useful.
```

This keeps the outreach buyer-centered. Avoid feature walkthroughs, full pitch decks, and "we help" claims before the buyer's current choice is clear.
