## Use In Cold Email Architecture

Adds a current media-relations perspective beyond pitch templates. Use for PR/podcast mode standards and audience protection.

## Source Notes

- Publisher: PR News Online.
- Date shown on page: 2025-06-18.
- Trust level: useful secondary interpretation of Muck Rack journalism survey data.

## Cleaned Extract

- Many journalists are operating under time pressure, role expansion, layoffs, and audience fragmentation.
- Pitches should be clear, brief, and respectful of time.
- Pre-vetted experts, ready-to-use visuals, quotes, data points, and summaries can help journalists move faster.
- Relationships matter before the ask. Useful engagement when nothing is being requested can increase trust.
- A pitch should inspire a story rather than demand a transaction.
- Social DMs should be avoided unless the journalist has invited that channel.

## Cold Email Implications

- PR/podcast mode should ask: "does this help the recipient serve their audience?"
- The offer can be a source packet, expert availability, angle list, data point, visual asset, or concise summary.
- Follow-up should be minimal and additive; do not pressure or guilt a journalist/host.
- If the pitch includes gated or paid resources, include a clean summary or accessible attachment only when appropriate.

## What Should Enter The Skill

- Audience-first pitch standard.
- Time-respectful media pitch structure.
- Relationship-before-ask rule for recurring media relationships.
- "Inspire a story" framing.

## What Should Not Enter The Skill

- Mass PR blasts.
- Social DM follow-up by default.
- Founder ego as the story.
