# Muck Rack State of Journalism 2025

Source URL: <https://muckrack.com/research/state-of-journalism>

Source type: WEB cleaned source card.

Raw artifact policy: no raw HTML stored; this card preserves the useful extraction and source URL.

## Use In Cold Email Architecture

Strengthens PR/podcast mode by grounding outreach in journalist constraints and current media-workload context.

## Source Notes

- Source owner: Muck Rack.
- Survey field dates: April 4-30, 2025.
- Sample: 1,890 journalists, according to the research page.
- Trust level: useful for media-relations context; still treat as vendor research and triangulate with non-vendor sources.

## Cleaned Extract

- Journalists are adapting to AI, platform changes, rising workloads, and independent publishing.
- The report is about what shapes coverage and what PR people need to understand to earn attention.
- Because the full report is gated, use this card mainly for survey provenance and the broad direction: journalist outreach must respect workload, coverage focus, and trust.

## Cold Email Implications

- PR/podcast outreach should protect the recipient's audience first and the sender's story second.
- Pitches should be easier to evaluate: clear beat fit, real news, credible source, useful assets, and concise framing.
- Do not rely on generic "thought leadership" pitches without a specific audience reason.
- Mark vendor survey claims as directional unless supported by the full report or an independent source.

## What Should Enter The Skill

- Journalist workload and trust as constraints.
- Beat fit before pitch angle.
- Audience-protection standard for PR/podcast mode.

## What Should Not Enter The Skill

- Treating one survey as universal truth.
- Spray-and-pray media lists.
- AI-generated pitch personalization without manual verification.
