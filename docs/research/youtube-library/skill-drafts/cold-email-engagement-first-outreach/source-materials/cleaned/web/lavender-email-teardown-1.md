## Cleaned Extract

### Before (original email, Lavender score 89)

Subject: **"Congrats on funding"**

> Hi Will,
>
> Congrats on your recent round of funding! I figured it'd be an opportune time to reach out and learn how you're currently managing credit card spend at Lavender. [Company name] offers our own credit cards along with FREE spend management software to help you gain better control of recurring ads, vendors, software, supplies, and T&E spending, all while benefitting from up to 2% cashback. Given your funding, this would be an excellent opportunity to explore [company name]. Is it unreasonable to think that a quick conversation over the phone would be mutually beneficial?
>
> Best, [Redacted name]

### After (Lavender rewrite, score 93)

Subject: **"Managing Spend"**

> Hello Will,
>
> Lavender's seen quite substantial growth over the last six months. From onboarding marketing all-stars to quality engineering talent.
>
> One thing is for certain — they all have expenses.
>
> Teams going through similar growth use company credit cards and free spend management software to get more control over spend.
>
> How do you manage spend?
>
> PS. Congrats on the recent round.

### Critique points

1. **Subject:** "Congrats on funding" reads like marketing and risks spam-pattern matching; "Managing Spend" looks like an internal email.
2. **Them > you:** the original centers the sender's product ("[Company] offers our own credit cards… FREE spend management software… 2% cashback"); the rewrite opens with the recipient's situation (growth → hiring → expenses) before any offering appears.
3. **Readability/mobile:** rewrite uses short, one-line sentences shaped for a phone screen (article notes first reads are 8x more likely on mobile).
4. **Earned relevance:** the original spends its words on the offering instead of showing "why you're showing up in their specific inbox"; the rewrite derives the reason from observable facts about the recipient's company. The trigger (funding congrats) is demoted to a PS.
5. **CTA:** awkward negative-framed time-ask ("Is it unreasonable to think… mutually beneficial?") becomes a low-friction call-to-conversation: "How do you manage spend?"

### Lessons

- Tilt the word budget toward the recipient: a little more them > you.
- Neutral, internal-looking subject lines beat trigger-announcing ones.
- Put the trigger event in a PS, not the opener, when the body can earn relevance on its own.
- One idea per line; end on a question the prospect can answer from memory.
