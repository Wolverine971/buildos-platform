## Cleaned Extract

### Context numbers

- Average cold email reply rate: ~2%; standard range quoted as 1–5%. Lavender claims customers average "almost 40%" reply rates (vendor self-claim — do not use as a threshold).
- Over 150,000 emails are sent every minute; the average person receives ~121 emails a day.
- Readers scan an email for ~11 seconds; most decide whether to open from the preview text alone.
- First open is "8x more likely to happen on the phone" — write for a mobile screen.
- Follow-up context: Gartner cited at 66 activities to book one qualified meeting; Outreach cited at 18 touches; Lavender customers reportedly need ~4–5 touches.

### Body rules (the numeric core)

- **Length: 25–50 words ideal; keep it under 75 words.** "Emails that are short and mobile-optimized see 83% more replies."
- **Reading level: 3rd–5th grade gets +67% more replies.** 70% of cold emails are written at or beyond a 10th-grade reading level — most senders fail this.
- **White space:** break the email into one-line sentences or 1–2 sentence paragraphs.
- **Clarity:** buzzwords, jargon, adverbs, long words, and long sentences hurt scannability.
- **Tone:** friendly and casual, while professional and clear.
- **Deliverability hygiene:** avoid images, links, repetitive sends, more than ~50 cold emails daily, and bulk sends.
- **Clichés to eliminate:** "I hope this finds you well." / "Do you have 15 minutes?" / "I'm sure you're busy."

### Subject line guidance (summary; full data in `lavender-subject-line-tips.md`)

Short (1–3 words), title case, neutral tone, boring is best, descriptive of the email, sounds like an internal email. Avoid verbs, numbers, questions, first names, commands, superlatives, punctuation, clichés.

### Four-part email framework

1. **Observation** — lead with personalized research (1–2 sentences).
2. **Connection** — turn the research into a relevant hypothesis about _their_ problem; the observation must "neatly connect to the problem your product or service solves."
3. **Credibility** — context via social proof or a simple explanation.
4. **Question** — a **Call to Conversation (CTC)**, not a high-friction meeting request.

### Call to Conversation (CTC)

Principle: "The only goal of a cold email is to get a reply." Replace booking-demand CTAs with conversation starters: ask if it's worth learning more, clarify priorities, understand needs, uncover existing solutions, verify your hypothesis, identify a better contact, or offer a resource.

### Relevance framing

"Your buyer doesn't care about you" — write about their problems, not your features. Research sources: their website, Google, social profiles, company information, market activity.

### Persona-grade notes (from related benchmark-learnings posts on the same page)

A-grade emails to finance see ~79% reply-rate lift (largest persona lift); A-grade to operations ~58% lift; only 6.1% of emails to finance earned a Lavender "A" grade. Reported in adjacent Lavender benchmark-learnings posts; same directional-vendor status.
