SU CC E SS F U L P ITC H

Getting journalist responses is the most common challenge for

PR pros: If your pitch isn’t personalized, timely and thoughtful, it

will fall flat. Use this checklist to write a winning pitch.

Define your objectives: Outline what you hope to achieve with your

pitch, whether it’s brand awareness, a product launch, thought leadership,

Research and target your audience: Identify the media outlets and

reporters that align with your message and audience.

Read each reporter’s previous articles: Learn more about their point of

view, the kinds of stories they tell and organizations they cover.

WRI TI N G YOU R PITC H

Make sure your email is properly addressed: Double-check that you’re

greeting the correct person—and that you spelled their name correctly.

Personalize it: Reporters can see right through a generic copy and paste.

Include context on who you are and briefly mention any previous interactions

you’ve had with them. If you’re using an AI tool to help craft your message, it’s

extra important to add your personal touch.

Create a concise and compelling story: Your pitch should be engaging,

timely and include a compelling hook about why your story matters. Since most

reporters prefer pitches under 200 words, get to your point quickly.

Craft a clear subject line: A confusing subject line can negate all the hard work

you put into writing your pitch if the reporter doesn’t open your email. Make

sure your subject line is upfront and clear on what the pitch is about. Learn

more about how to write a great subject line.

Proofread: Beyond spelling and grammar, make sure you’re using an

up-to-date email address for your recipient (use a high-quality database to

confirm a journalists’ information). Always fact-check your pitch, especially

if you’re using an AI tool.

A FTER YOU H IT SE N D

Send a follow-up: 51% of reporters say the best time to send a follow-up is 3–7

days after the initial pitch, and about 45% say that one follow-up email is ideal.

Monitor and report on your coverage: Use a platform with a reliable media

monitoring tool to stay on top of media mentions. You should also have a

dashboard that updates automatically every time you get a mention that you

can export to showcase the value of your work to stakeholders.

Nurture relationships with reporters: Write a thank you note to reporters

who feature your story, and check in with them periodically even if you don’t

have a pitch. Media relations is all about relationship building; taking the time

to get to know reporters on a personal level will only help your future efforts.
