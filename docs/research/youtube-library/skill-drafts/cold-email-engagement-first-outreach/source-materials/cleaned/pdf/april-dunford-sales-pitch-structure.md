# April Dunford Sales Pitch Structure

Source URL: <https://businessofsoftware.org/wp-content/uploads/sites/11/2023/10/April-Dunford-_compressed.pdf>

Source type: PDF cleaned source card.

Raw artifact policy: no raw PDF stored; this card preserves the useful extraction and source URL.

## Use In Cold Email Architecture

Strengthens OfferLab and the outreach compiler by turning the offer into a buyer-choice story, not a feature walkthrough.

## Source Notes

- Creator: April Dunford.
- Venue/source: Business of Software presentation PDF.
- Trust level: high for B2B positioning and sales narrative; use as a framing source, not as a cold-email template.

## Cleaned Extract

- A sales pitch should help the buyer make a choice among alternatives. The cold email implication is that the offer should reduce confusion, not simply describe the sender's product.
- The useful sequence is market insight -> alternatives -> differentiated value -> proof -> objections -> ask. For cold email, only a compressed subset belongs in the first touch.
- Feature walkthroughs are weak because they start from the supply side. Better outreach starts from the buyer's context, the tradeoffs they face, and why one path may fit.
- A strong cold artifact can be a mini version of this pitch: name the market shift or tradeoff, show the alternative, offer a useful diagnostic or teardown, and ask permission to send it.
- Fit discipline matters. The pitch should help bad-fit prospects self-disqualify instead of pushing every recipient toward a meeting.

## Cold Email Implications

- OfferLab should ask: what buyer choice does this email help clarify?
- Compiler should reserve proof for the specific differentiated claim, not generic credibility.
- Taste review should reject feature tours, "we do X" copy, and pitches that do not name the alternative the buyer is using now.
- For strategic outreach, the "right offer" can be an artifact that helps the buyer compare options before speaking with sales.

## What Should Enter The Skill

- Buyer-choice framing.
- Alternatives and tradeoff language.
- Differentiated value plus proof as a compact pair.
- Fit-based refusal to sell to bad-fit recipients.

## What Should Not Enter The Skill

- Full deck structure in a first cold email.
- Hero's Journey-style sender narrative.
- Product walkthroughs masquerading as value.
