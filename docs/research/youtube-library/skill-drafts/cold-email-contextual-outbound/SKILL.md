# Cold Email Contextual Outbound

Use this skill to help an agent create cold outbound that earns attention through relevance, not volume alone. The agent should reason about deliverability, offer testing, buyer worldview, personalization, objections, and speed of reply.

## When to Use

- Draft a cold email campaign
- Test outbound offers or pain-point angles
- Personalize emails for senior buyers
- Build subject lines and first sentences from real research
- Preempt objections in the email body
- Plan reply handling, same-day call routing, or follow-up
- Diagnose low reply rates

Do not use this skill for newsletter writing, lifecycle email, support replies, or generic mass-blast copy.

## Required Inputs

- Target buyer and market
- Offer being tested
- Desired action after reply
- Source of prospect research
- Current deliverability constraints if sending at scale
- Known objections
- Proof, case studies, or call transcript language if available

## Core Workflow

1. **Confirm the goal.** Decide whether this is an offer test, booked-call campaign, intro-offer campaign, or relationship opener.
2. **Find the worldview.** Identify what the buyer already believes, fears, wants, or invests in. Good outbound feels like it understands the buyer's situation before pitching.
3. **Mine real language.** Pull phrases from sales calls, customer conversations, reviews, posts, interviews, or CRM notes. Prefer the buyer's own words.
4. **Write the offer as a test.** Use a simple 30-40 word statement: if we could help you get outcome X in situation Y, would that be worth exploring?
5. **Personalize the subject and opener.** Use research specific enough that the recipient can tell the email was meant for them.
6. **Preempt one objection.** Name the most likely objection and answer it before the buyer has to raise it.
7. **Use a respectful CTA.** Ask for time over the next week or two and offer to send the invite after they share availability. Avoid calendar-link dumping in first touch.
8. **Create variations.** Preserve the same offer while varying frame, subject, proof, and wording to avoid brittle sameness.
9. **Plan reply handling.** Route replies to a fast follow-up path. For high-intent replies, respond the same day and move toward a call.

## Subject Line Rules

- Make the subject specific to the recipient or a narrow shared context.
- Combine two or three real hooks when useful: company, post, event, hometown, alma mater, cause, customer, or niche signal.
- If the subject could work for thousands of people unchanged, it is probably too generic.
- Do not fake familiarity or imply a relationship that does not exist.

## Body Rules

- Lead with relevance before credentials.
- State the buyer challenge, not just what the sender sells.
- Keep the offer variable clean during testing. Do not mix in too many guarantees, bonuses, case studies, or CTAs until one angle works.
- Use plain speech and concrete sensory language when it helps the buyer visualize the problem.
- Longer can work only when every sentence earns its space through specificity.

## Testing Rules

- Test one major variable at a time.
- Hold CTA constant while testing offers.
- Treat positive reply rate as the main early signal.
- After a winner emerges, test frame, proof, and CTA around the winner.
- Port winning mechanisms into adjacent markets instead of inventing from scratch every time.

## Reply Handling

- Send reply alerts somewhere visible.
- Enrich phone number or account context on reply when allowed.
- Reply quickly while intent is fresh.
- Offer specific times after the buyer shows interest.
- Continue following up after verbal interest until the call is booked or the buyer declines.
- Use custom Looms or relevant proof for high-quality leads that need more context.

## Guardrails

- Do not fabricate personal research.
- Do not claim results without evidence.
- Do not send sensitive or regulated claims without review.
- Do not over-personalize into creepy territory.
- Do not optimize for volume before deliverability and offer quality are understood.
- Ask for confirmation before sending or submitting any outbound message.
