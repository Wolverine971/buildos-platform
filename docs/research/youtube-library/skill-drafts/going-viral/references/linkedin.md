# LinkedIn — Going Viral Reference

Read this file when working on LinkedIn specifically. The cross-platform `SKILL.md` covers the universal layer. This file covers what's _different_ on LinkedIn — which in 2025/2026 means a fundamentally rebuilt ranking system, dwell-time-over-engagement-velocity, a four-step content algorithm post creators must navigate, and a counterintuitive video-deprioritization that flips most "go viral" advice on its head.

## Read me first

LinkedIn underwent the **most aggressive algorithm overhaul of any major platform in 2025**. The old "post a story, get likes, win" playbook is dead. The new ranking system (360Brew, a foundation model) reads semantic relevance, profile-content alignment, dwell time, saves, and meaningful comments — not just velocity. Reach has dropped 50% year-over-year; engagement is down 25%; follower growth is down 59%. But the creators who adapted to the new physics are landing 5–10x more reach per follower than they were in 2023.

The four-step content gauntlet (Hala Taha): every post passes through (1) spam check, (2) "golden 90 minutes" engagement test, (3) extended distribution to first connections + lookalikes, (4) editorial-fit evaluation. Failing any step kills the post.

Counterintuitive: **video is currently de-prioritized vs. text + photo posts** for most creators (Lara Acosta data). LinkedIn is still optimizing its video ranking; until that stabilizes, text-and-photo posts outperform.

## Algorithm reality (2025/2026)

**The 360Brew shift (2025):**

- LinkedIn replaced its prior ranking system with **360Brew**, a large foundation model
- 360Brew evaluates: **semantic relevance, profile-content alignment, dwell time, saves, meaningful comments**
- This is NOT engagement velocity. A post with 50 thoughtful comments outranks one with 500 likes.

**The October 2025 reach drop (Richard van der Blom Algorithm Insights Report 2025/2026):**

- Average post reach: **8–12% of followers** (down from 15–20% a year ago)
- Organic reach down 50%; engagement down 25%; follower growth down 59% YoY
- **First 90 minutes determine ~70% of total reach** — early engagement signals tell the algorithm whether to keep distributing
- "Dwell time" (time spent reading without engaging) is the new silent boss metric

**What works:**

- **Text + photo posts** outperform video for most niches in 2025/2026 (Lara Acosta data, confirmed across multiple LI creators)
- **Saves > comments > likes** for ranking weight (saves indicate utility)
- **Meaningful comments** (10+ words) signal to the model; 1–3 word "Great post!" comments are now filtered
- **Carousels** still strong — they hold dwell time mechanically (swipe = engagement)
- **Personal stories with frameworks** (Lara Acosta SLAY) — story makes you read; framework makes you save

**What doesn't work:**

- **External links in the post body** — heavy reach penalty; put links in first comment instead
- **>5 hashtags** — flagged as spam by the spam-check stage
- **>5 @-tags** — also flagged as spam
- **Generic engagement bait** — "Agree? 👇" is now actively suppressed by the model
- **Reposts of your own content** within ~30 days — penalized
- **AI-generated content that smells AI-generated** — December 2025 raw-real-human shift hits LI too

## Hala Taha's four-step content algorithm

From _The 4-Step Formula to Go Viral on LinkedIn (2025 Masterclass)_ — the structural map every post must navigate.

### Step 1 — Spam check

When you submit a post, LinkedIn first scans for:

- Profanity, nudity, offensive keywords (instant kill)
- Tagging more than 5 people (spam flag)
- More than 5 hashtags (spam flag)
- External links in the post body (reach penalty)
- Excessive emojis (some signal but easy to overdo)

**Pass strategy:** Break text into one-sentence paragraphs (not "wall of text"); cap hashtags at 1–3; put any link in the FIRST COMMENT, not the post; tag at most 2–3 highly relevant people.

### Step 2 — The Golden 90 Minutes

After passing spam check, LinkedIn shows the post to a small initial audience — primarily your first connections + a sample of relevant non-followers — and watches engagement velocity for 30–90 minutes.

This is the most decisive window. If your post earns 20+ meaningful interactions in this window, LinkedIn boosts it to extended distribution. Miss this window and the post dies.

**Pass strategy:**

- Post when your audience is online (mid-week, US morning hours for most B2B; check your analytics)
- DM 3–5 close connections with a heads-up before posting; ask for a substantive comment, not a like
- Reply to every comment within the first 30 minutes — author replies count as engagement
- Don't edit the post in the first 90 minutes (some sources still flag edits as resets)

### Step 3 — Extended distribution

If Step 2 passes, the post extends to (a) all your first connections, (b) connections-of-commenters (powerful — every comment effectively "vouches"), (c) lookalike audiences modeled on your engaged readers.

**Pass strategy:** Reply to comments substantively. A reply that gets a reply from the original commenter is worth ~10x a like. Reply chains are how the post climbs the lookalike ladder.

### Step 4 — Editorial fit

The 360Brew model evaluates whether the content fits the platform's "professional editorial agenda." Posts that read as professional/educational/aspirational are favored; posts that read as vacation photos, political rants, or personal grievance are deprioritized.

**Pass strategy:** Write to your professional avatar. Even personal stories should connect to a professional lesson — Lara Acosta's framework: personal hook → professional insight → tactical takeaway.

## Lara Acosta — the SLAY framework + storytelling-first growth

Lara Acosta grew from 0 to 300K+ followers on LinkedIn in ~2 years using a storytelling-led approach. Key extracts (cross-referenced from Creator Science #241 surface-level summary; full transcript pending YouTube rate-limit clear):

**The first line is your YouTube thumbnail.** LinkedIn shows the first 1–2 lines (about 8–10 words on mobile) before the "...see more" cutoff. That's the entire hook. Make it:

- Punchy and indicative of the topic (echoes Kallaway _Master Storyteller_)
- Emotionally evocative — curiosity, frustration, surprise, or contrarian opening
- Mobile-optimized — 8–10 words, not a full sentence

**Counterintuitive — video is not the optimal growth strategy on LinkedIn (in 2025).** Despite LinkedIn's push for video, photo + text posts and carousels deliver higher engagement for most creators. Until LinkedIn's video ranking stabilizes, prioritize the formats with proven physics.

**SLAY framework** (extract from public summaries):

- **S**: Story — open with a specific, vivid, personal moment
- **L**: Lesson — extract the universal lesson from the moment
- **A**: Application — give the reader something to do
- **Y**: Yes-and — invite engagement with a question that earns a substantive reply (not "agree?")

**Networking at scale:** Acosta treats LinkedIn as "the biggest networking conference in the world that never sleeps." She replies to comments, DMs new connections, and engages on others' posts daily. The growth is not just publishing — it's the **engagement-on-others' posts** that compounds, because every meaningful comment surfaces her name to the OP's audience.

## Justin Welsh — the LinkedIn OS

Justin Welsh has been writing on LinkedIn daily since 2018. From his interviews and public framework summaries (Creator Science #109 + others):

**Daily atomic posts.** One idea per post. No multi-thesis pieces. The discipline is not in the writing; it's in choosing the one idea and not writing the others.

**Three post archetypes that compound:**

1. **Hard-won lesson** — "I spent X years failing at Y. Here's what finally worked." (Authority builder.)
2. **Specific framework** — "Here's the exact 5-step process I use to [outcome]." (Save-and-bookmark builder.)
3. **Contrarian observation** — "Everyone says [X]. They're wrong. Here's why." (Reach builder.)

Welsh rotates these across the week — never two of the same archetype back to back, because audience pattern-matching kills the surprise.

**The atomic essay structure (Welsh's LinkedIn-native version):**

```
[Hook — 8–10 words, specific or contrarian]

[Common belief — what most people think]

[Contrarian take — what you actually believe]

[Body — 3-5 short lines, often a numbered list]

[Close — one sentence + a question that earns substantive replies]
```

This is Kallaway's killer-script compressed for LinkedIn's 8-paragraph display optimum.

## Richard van der Blom — what the data actually says

Richard van der Blom's _Algorithm Insights Report 2025/2026_ (analyzed 1.8M LinkedIn posts):

**Confirmed signal weights:**

- Saves: heaviest weight (utility signal)
- Meaningful comments (10+ words): second-heaviest
- Reshares with commentary: high
- Reshares without commentary: low (algorithm reads as low-effort)
- Likes: lowest weight; functionally a noise floor

**Confirmed format performance (2025/2026 averages):**

- Carousels: highest engagement-per-impression
- Text + image: second
- Text-only: third
- Native video: fourth (de-prioritized currently — see Lara Acosta note)
- External-link posts: distant fifth (penalty)

**Posting timing:**

- Mid-week (Tue/Wed/Thu) outperforms Mon/Fri
- US morning (8–10am EST) for B2B audiences
- These windows produce ~20% higher reach but are NOT the largest lever — content quality is

**The reach drop:**

- Average post sees 8–12% of followers (down from 15–20%)
- Top 5% of posts now reach 40–60%+ of followers
- The middle 80% are starving — performance gap between hits and misses has widened

**Actionable implication:** Don't post mediocre content. The algorithm now punishes mid-tier work harder than it punishes outright bad work, because mid-tier work poisons the audience-match more efficiently than obvious failures.

## Carousel-specific structure

Carousels are LinkedIn's highest-engagement format in 2025/2026. They work because each swipe = engagement signal, and dwell time accumulates per slide.

**The 9-slide structure (battle-tested):**

```
Slide 1: Hook (the title-card: 4–6 words, large type, contrarian or specific)
Slide 2: The problem named (in audience language)
Slide 3: Common belief / wrong move
Slide 4: Contrarian take / right move
Slide 5: Step 1 / Component 1
Slide 6: Step 2 / Component 2
Slide 7: Step 3 / Component 3
Slide 8: Recap (numbered list of the 3 components)
Slide 9: CTA — single, soft, save-or-comment-driving
```

**Visual rules:**

- Square format (1:1) — works in feed and on mobile
- 1 idea per slide; if you can't, split into 2 slides
- High contrast, sans-serif type, generous white space
- Brand consistency (same color palette across all carousels) builds recognition

**Title-card test:** if Slide 1 is screenshotted standalone, would someone want to swipe? If no, the carousel doesn't earn the second slide.

## When this is the wrong platform

- **Direct-to-consumer products** — Instagram or TikTok have better physics
- **Live community / fandom** — Discord, X, or subreddits work better
- **Real-time news commentary** — X is faster
- **Heavily visual storytelling** — Instagram or TikTok
- **Pure performance ads** — wrong platform; LI ads have a different playbook

## LinkedIn-specific anti-patterns

- **External links in the post body.** Heavy reach penalty. Put the link in the first comment.
- **Wall-of-text posts.** Break into one-sentence paragraphs. The 8-paragraph mobile-display optimum exists for a reason.
- **>5 hashtags or >5 @-tags.** Spam-flag from Step 1 of Hala Taha's algorithm.
- **Daily reposts of the same thesis.** Audience-match collapses; subsequent posts dragged down.
- **Pre-edited posts that were AI-drafted and not voiced.** December 2025 "raw real human" shift suppresses identifiable AI prose.
- **Engagement bait** ("Agree? 👇" / "Like if you're with me") — actively suppressed by 360Brew.
- **Vacation / personal-life posts on a professional account.** Step 4 editorial-fit penalty.
- **Auto-DM sequences post-connect.** Banned + visible to recipients; brand damage > reach lift.
- **Posting native video right now.** Wait until LI's video ranking stabilizes (sometime in 2026); for now, text + photo + carousels.

## Cross-platform note

LinkedIn is the platform where **rented → owned ramps work fastest.** B2B audiences readily click through to newsletters, books, courses, and SaaS trials when the content earned the trust. A LinkedIn carousel → newsletter → product trial funnel converts at higher rates than the same funnel on any other platform.

Don't use LinkedIn for top-of-funnel attention only. Use it as the trust-building layer immediately above the offering.
