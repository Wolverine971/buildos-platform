# TikTok / Short-Form Vertical — Going Viral Reference

Read this file when working on TikTok specifically. Most short-form vertical guidance applies to **YouTube Shorts and Instagram Reels** as well — the underlying mechanics (sample-200 test, watch time + completion as #1 signal, the visual → audio → visual sandwich) are identical. Sibling platform-specific divergences are noted at the end.

The cross-platform `SKILL.md` covers the universal layer. The 13 Kallaway analyses in `youtube-library/analyses/` cover the granular hook + script + storytelling craft. **This file is the smallest of the four references because the `SKILL.md` is already short-form-pilled by default — Kallaway's entire content factory is TikTok / Reels native.**

## Read me first

TikTok rewards **completion rate above all else**. The completion-rate bar for virality crossed into ~70% in 2025. Hook in the first second, retain through retention beats every 3–5 seconds, payoff before the loop, and design for replay (last frame setting up first frame on rewatch). Native-looking content outperforms studio-polished content by ~47% — the algorithm punishes posts that read as ads.

The test: a stranger lands on your video mid-scroll. Can they parse the topic in **one second** without context? If no, you lose the test, and you lose the next 200 viewers.

## Algorithm reality (2025/2026)

**Core principle (TikTok creator portal + algorithm-research consensus):**

- Watch time + completion rate are the **#1 ranking signal** (~40–50% of weight)
- Completion rate ≥ ~70% required for virality at scale (was 50% in 2023)
- **Shares + saves > likes** — saves indicate utility, shares indicate share-payoff
- The algorithm tests new videos with **followers first**, then non-followers, in tiers of ~200 → 2K → 20K → 200K → 2M
- **Retention in the first 3 seconds matters more than follower count** — experienced creators consistently report this

**Format requirements (TikTok 2025 Creator Portal):**

- **9:16 aspect ratio strongly recommended** — non-vertical content gets reduced FYP distribution
- **Native-looking content outperforms studio-polished by 47%** (per TikTok creator portal data) — content that reads as a "traditional ad" with studio lighting + scripted talent is penalized
- Watermarks from other platforms (CapCut, Reels) trigger penalties — TikTok suppresses videos that look like recycled content

**2026 algorithm changes (post-acquisition):**

- US algorithm being retrained on US-only data under Oracle's cloud infrastructure
- This is the most significant US-side change since 2020 — playbooks may shift; verify against current data before treating any tactic as permanent

## Hook architecture (TikTok-specific)

The cross-platform `SKILL.md` covers the six hook archetypes. On TikTok, the additional constraint is **the one-second test**.

**Jenny Hoyos's 1-second hook doctrine** (averages 10M views per Short, 600M+ views in 2024):

- "I really do think you have one second to hook someone, especially on Shorts."
- Three-step formula: **shock → intrigue → satisfy**
    - **Shock** — break the scroll with an unexpected visual or claim
    - **Intrigue** — open the curiosity loop within 2–3 seconds
    - **Satisfy** — close the loop within the video; payoff lands before they swipe

## The visual → audio → visual sandwich (Kallaway)

How viewers actually consume the first 5 seconds of a vertical video:

1. **Eyes process visual + read text overlay** (~0–500ms)
2. **Ears parse the spoken hook** (~500–2000ms)
3. **Eyes return to the visual** seeking confirmation of what they just heard (~2000–3500ms)

> "Our eyes process 10 to 100 times more information per second than our ears — speed of light versus speed of sound." (Kallaway _100 Viral Hooks_)

**Therefore:** the **key visual** is load-bearing, not the words. Words exist to set up and confirm the visual. If your topic doesn't have a strong key visual, **kill the video**. (Kallaway is explicit about this — manufacture the visual or don't make the video.)

The four hook components (must all agree on the same key visual):

1. **Spoken hook** — the voiceover
2. **Visual hook** — what's on screen
3. **Text hook** — text overlay
4. **Audio hook** — music / SFX

> "The difference between 500 and 500,000 views is unlocking max alignment between those four things." (Kallaway)

## Hook archetype mapping for TikTok

| Archetype          | TikTok-flavored example                                                                                   |
| ------------------ | --------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------- |
| **Fortune Teller** | "This is the future of [X]" — over a never-before-seen demo                                               |
| **Experimenter**   | "Watch what happens when I [X]" — live demo on screen                                                     |
| **Teacher**        | "Three things you can learn from [Y]" — talking head with cuts                                            |
| **Magician**       | "Yo check this out" — visual stun (signature object, jump-cut, neon) layered on top of one of the other 5 |
| **Investigator**   | "This is a secret [X] you've probably never heard about" — reveal-paced                                   |
| **Contrarian**     | "Everyone says [X] but they're wrong because [Y]" — straight to camera                                    |

For BuildOS-attributed founder content on TikTok, the natural archetypes are **Investigator** (anti-feed observations, model-behavior critiques), **Contrarian** (anti-AI marketing POV pieces), and **Teacher** (context engineering frameworks). Use Experimenter for product demos. Pair Magician (a visual signature — typing in BuildOS, walking, whiteboarding, working on a real project) on top of any of the above to compensate for limited b-roll.

## Killer-script compressed to 60 seconds

The cross-platform killer-script skeleton (`SKILL.md` Step 4) compresses for short-form like this:

```
HOOK (0–3 seconds):     5-part intro compressed to one sentence + key visual
BODY (3–45 seconds):    2-1-3-4 ordering applied to 2–3 quick value beats
                         (skip Value Loops; one beat = context + application + framing in <10s)
                         Insert ONE rehook between beats
OUTRO (45–60 seconds):  Fortune Cookie compressed — one-sentence recap + one-line high note
                         Last line sets up first line on replay
```

For 15-second TikToks: skip the rehook; just hook → 1–2 beats → close.

## Format winners (2025/2026)

**What works:**

- Talking head with on-screen text overlay reinforcing the visual (most universal format)
- Demo videos where the screen IS the proof (works for product, code, design, AI tools)
- Walk-and-talk vertical (Pieter Levels-style, scrappy, founder-voice — high authenticity)
- Storytime with cuts (Kallaway-style — story compressed, jump-cuts, escalating tension)
- Visual pacifier underneath voiceover (cup-stacking, makeup, GTA gameplay split-screen, typing — keeps subconscious eye attention parked)

**What's stale:**

- Pure dance / lip-sync (table stakes, low-signal for value-driven creators)
- Memes without commentary (no audience-match data)
- Trending sound chases without topical fit (poisons the targeting)
- Cinematic shots without multiple cuts (MrBeast: "talking to a single camera for too long is boring and causes viewer loss")

## Retention mechanics

Beyond the hook, retention is the entire game. Three rules:

**1. Cut between angles strategically.** MrBeast: "if people aren't clicking away, it means they're entertained." Cut every 1–3 seconds in the body if you don't have visual variety. Even talking head benefits from B-roll cutaways.

**2. Insert retention beats every 3–5 seconds.** A retention beat = a small surprise (visual cut, audio change, on-screen text reveal, escalation, callback). The brain re-engages on each beat.

**3. Open the next loop before closing the current one.** "I tried [X], but then [unexpected thing happened], so I had to..." — the "but/therefore" structure (South Park / Kallaway _Master Storyteller_) chains loops.

## Sibling platforms

**YouTube Shorts:** ~95% identical mechanics. Shorts ranks completion rate even more aggressively than TikTok. The Shorts shelf surfaces videos to subscribers more than TikTok surfaces to followers, so subscriber-cohort signals matter slightly more on Shorts. Otherwise, treat as a TikTok sibling — Jenny Hoyos's playbook works on both.

**Instagram Reels:** see `references/instagram.md` for the full breakdown. Key divergences:

- Mosseri's Jan 2025 ranking: watch time + likes-per-reach + DM shares (DM shares NOT a primary TikTok signal)
- Trial reels feature (TikTok has no equivalent)
- Carousel + photo posts compete with Reels in the feed (TikTok is video-only)
- December 2025 "raw real human content" memo specifically targeting AI-generated Reels

**Cross-posting penalty:** TikTok and Instagram both penalize content that has the other platform's watermark. Either remove the watermark with editing tools OR shoot a native version per platform. **Do not** copy-paste the same export across platforms — the algorithm reads the watermark as recycled content.

## TikTok-specific anti-patterns

- **Studio polish without retention.** The 47% native-content advantage means founder-shot iPhone footage often outperforms an edited team production.
- **Cinematic single-angle videos.** Talking to one camera for >10 seconds without cuts loses retention.
- **Watermarks from CapCut, Reels, or YouTube Shorts.** Suppressed.
- **Non-vertical aspect ratio.** 16:9 or square content gets reduced FYP distribution.
- **Hooks that need context.** If the viewer can't parse the topic in one second without prior knowledge of you, you lose.
- **Saving the strongest visual for after the hook.** Key visual must be in the first 1 second on TikTok (3 seconds max for bigger creators with established audience).
- **Pure trending-sound chases.** Audience-match collapses; the next 5 videos suffer.
- **Generic CTAs in the outro** ("Like and subscribe for more"). Native CTAs (Kallaway _Killer Script_) embed in the body — pain raised, resource gives the solve.

## When this is the wrong platform

- **B2B enterprise sales.** LinkedIn / X are higher-conversion for that audience.
- **Long-form ideas requiring >3 minutes.** YouTube long-form is the home for those.
- **Older demographics (50+).** Facebook + LinkedIn skew older.
- **Local services.** Local SEO + Google Maps win.
- **Highly text-heavy content.** Carousels on Instagram or LinkedIn beat text-overlay TikToks.
