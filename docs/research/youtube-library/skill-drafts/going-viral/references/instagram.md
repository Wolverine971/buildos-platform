# Instagram — Going Viral Reference

Read this file when working on Instagram specifically. The cross-platform `SKILL.md` covers the universal layer; `references/tiktok.md` covers most short-form vertical mechanics that apply to Reels too. **This file covers what's _different_ on Instagram in 2025/2026** — three new ranking signals confirmed by Mosseri, the trial-reels feature, the December 2025 "raw real human content" priority shift, and the Reels-vs-carousel format trade-off that has no equivalent on TikTok.

## Read me first

In January 2025, Adam Mosseri (Head of Instagram) confirmed the **three top ranking signals** for Reels: **watch time, likes-per-reach, and DM shares.** DM shares are now the most powerful single signal for reaching new audiences — IG interprets a DM share as the strongest possible endorsement because the user is actively recommending content rather than passively liking it.

In December 2025, Mosseri published a year-end memo announcing IG would prioritize **"raw, real human content" over AI-generated material throughout 2026.** Content that smells AI-generated is now actively suppressed, and posts using TikTok or CapCut watermarks are deprioritized as "recycled."

Counterintuitive: **Reels are not always the right format.** Carousels still win for educational and framework-heavy content because the swipe pattern compounds dwell time. The right format is determined by content type, not by platform default.

## Algorithm reality (2025/2026)

**Multiple ranking systems (not one algorithm):**

In 2025, Instagram officially abandoned the singular "algorithm" terminology. The platform uses multiple AI-powered ranking systems — one each for Feed, Stories, Reels, and Explore. Optimizing for one does not necessarily help the others. **A post that wins on Reels can underperform in Feed; the signals are weighted differently.**

**Three top ranking signals for Reels (Mosseri, January 2025):**

1. **Watch time** — #1 ranking factor. How long people watch your Reels matters more than likes or shares for initial distribution. The critical threshold is the first **3 seconds** — IG heavily weighs whether viewers continue past this point.
2. **Likes per reach** — engagement RATE matters more than absolute likes. Niche posts that earn 1k likes from 5k impressions outperform broad posts that earn 5k likes from 100k impressions.
3. **DM shares (sends)** — most powerful signal for reaching new audiences. DM shares are interpreted as the strongest endorsement because users are actively recommending content. **Optimizing for "share-to-friend" is now the highest-leverage activity on Instagram.**

**Connected vs. unconnected reach:**

Mosseri distinguishes between:

- **Connected reach** — followers seeing your content (driven by watch time + likes + saves)
- **Unconnected reach** — non-followers (driven by the same signals + DM shares as the multiplier)

Both surfaces use the same primary signals; DM shares are what tip a post into unconnected (viral) reach.

**Eligibility requirements (December 2025):**

- No TikTok or CapCut watermarks (heavy deprioritization)
- No identifiably AI-generated content (December 2025 raw-human memo)
- 9:16 aspect ratio for Reels (16:9 and square get reduced FYP distribution)
- Captions in feed posts and Reels are weighted more than hashtags

## Trial Reels (2025) — the highest-leverage feature

**Trial reels** is a feature unique to Instagram (no TikTok equivalent). When you post a Reel as a trial:

- It's shown ONLY to non-followers initially
- You can repost top-performing trials to your followers later
- This separates the **discovery test** from the **community signal**

**The Brock Johnson play (cross-referenced from his content + interviews):**

- Post your best-performing existing content as trial reels to reach new audiences without spamming followers
- Post experimental content as trials so failures don't damage your follower-feed performance
- After a trial reel hits a performance threshold (e.g., 2x average), repost to followers as a regular Reel

**Strategic implication:** The Trial Reels feature lets you run **two parallel content cadences** — one for discovery (high-volume, high-experimentation) and one for community (curated wins). Most creators conflate these and damage their follower-feed performance with experimental content. The two-cadence approach is the 2025 Instagram-specific playbook.

## Brock Johnson's three-strategy framework

From _How to grow on Instagram in 2025_ (Creator Science #243), Brock Johnson's three core strategies:

**1. Niche down.**

Specificity turns casual scrollers into loyal, paying super-fans. The "audience matching" discipline (Kallaway _Misunderstood_) is even more decisive on Instagram in 2025 because the multi-algorithm setup punishes off-niche posts in subsequent distribution.

Brock's hook-time math: he reportedly spends **80% of his time, energy, and effort on the hook** — the first sentence or two of the video — because the hook is absolutely everything in short-form content. (Echoes Kallaway: "the hook is the 80/20 of short-form video.")

**2. Hyper-consistency.**

Post frequency directly impacts growth. Brock recommends daily posting (split between Reels, carousels, and Stories) for accounts trying to grow.

The audience-matching loop needs ~30–50 posts to calibrate. Posting weekly takes a year to do that calibration; posting daily takes two months.

**3. Trial Reels.**

See above. The unique 2025 feature; use it.

## Adam Mosseri — what the platform itself says

Mosseri publishes regularly to Instagram and Threads, AND has done direct video interviews with Brock Johnson + others. Pulling the strategic intent in his own words is high-signal.

**January 2025 ranking-signal disclosure:**

Watch time, likes-per-reach, DM shares are the three primary signals. The first 3 seconds determine whether viewers continue. Saves are a weaker signal than shares (saves indicate utility but aren't shared).

**December 2025 year-end memo:**

> "Looking forward to one significant interesting [shift] is that authenticity is becoming..." (Mosseri Threads post, December 2025)

Mosseri's framing: 2026 will prioritize content that reads as "raw, real, human" over polished AI-generated work. The platform is actively retraining its ranking signals to detect AI-generated visuals + scripts and deprioritize them.

**Strategic implication:** if a creator's workflow involves AI scripting or AI image generation, the 2026 algorithm shift may invert their past performance. Lean into founder-voice authenticity, scrappy founder-shot footage, and human imperfection.

## Reels vs. carousels — when to choose what

| Format                                 | Best for                                                    | Hook physics                                      | Engagement weight                              |
| -------------------------------------- | ----------------------------------------------------------- | ------------------------------------------------- | ---------------------------------------------- |
| **Reels (vertical video, 15–60s)**     | Personality, storytelling, demos, humor, motion-heavy ideas | First 3 seconds critical; visual sandwich applies | Watch time + DM shares + likes-per-reach       |
| **Carousels (1:1 multi-slide images)** | Frameworks, education, before/after, deep dives             | Slide 1 is the hook (4–6 word title-card)         | Saves + shares + dwell time per slide          |
| **Single image**                       | Aesthetics, branded statement, photo journalism             | Image as standalone composition                   | Likes + saves; lower reach ceiling             |
| **Stories**                            | Behind-the-scenes, polls, link drops, casual community      | First frame is the hook                           | Replies + DMs (highest signal); 24-hour window |

**Decision rule:**

- Idea has motion, demo, or surprise visual → **Reel**
- Idea is a framework, list, before/after, or deep dive → **Carousel**
- Idea is aesthetic / branded statement → **Single image**
- Idea is real-time, conversational, or community-driven → **Story**

The same underlying idea (Kallaway killer-script structure) can be expressed in any format. The format choice is a strategic decision about which physics serve the content type.

## Carousel structure (Instagram-specific)

Cross-reference `references/linkedin.md` for the 9-slide structure — it works identically on Instagram, with one platform-specific tweak:

**Instagram carousels rely more on visual cohesion** than LinkedIn carousels. LinkedIn audiences will read text-heavy slides; Instagram audiences will swipe past unless the visuals carry the rhythm. Use:

- High-contrast typography
- Consistent color palette across all 9 slides
- 1 idea per slide; if you can't, split
- A title card on Slide 1 that works as a screenshot in isolation

Brand recognition compounds: 5 carousels with the same visual system train your audience to recognize you in 0.3 seconds.

## Hook architecture for Instagram

The cross-platform `SKILL.md` six archetypes apply. Instagram-specific hook patterns:

**For Reels:**

- **Visual stun in frame 1** (Magician layer) — atypical visual, jump-cut, signature object
- **Spoken hook lands in 1 second** (Hoyos doctrine) — but Instagram's 3-second threshold gives slightly more runway than TikTok's 1-second
- **Text overlay in the first frame** matches the visual — comprehension test passes regardless of audio-on/audio-off

**For carousels:**

- **Slide 1 = title card.** 4–6 words, large type, contrarian or specific. If screenshotted, would someone want to swipe?
- **Slides 2–3 = problem + common belief.** The 5-part intro compressed.
- **Slides 4–8 = body.** 2-1-3-4 ordering applied to the carousel framework points.
- **Slide 9 = recap + CTA.** "Save this carousel" is a high-leverage CTA on Instagram because saves are a ranking signal AND DM-shares are amplified by carousels with shareable frameworks.

## Stories — the underrated layer

Stories are not the place for going viral, BUT they're the highest-conversion layer Instagram has.

**Strategic uses:**

- **DM share funnel.** A great Reel + a "send this to someone who needs it" story sticker can dramatically lift DM shares (the #1 unconnected-reach signal).
- **Polls + questions** as audience-research instruments — fastest free user research available.
- **"Recently published" amplification** — share your own Reel to Stories with commentary; followers who missed it surface back.
- **Link drops to owned products / newsletter** — Stories are the only feed-adjacent surface where external links don't tank reach (Instagram's intent: keep links here, not in feed).

## Instagram-specific anti-patterns

- **TikTok / CapCut watermarks.** Heavy deprioritization. Either re-export without watermark OR re-shoot natively.
- **Identifiably AI-generated visuals.** December 2025 raw-human shift suppresses these. Founder-shot iPhone footage > AI-generated Reels.
- **External links in feed posts.** Strong reach penalty. Use Stories for links, or "link in bio" pattern (which loses ~80% of click-through but preserves feed reach).
- **Posting Reels in non-9:16.** Reduced FYP distribution.
- **Treating Reels and Feed as the same algorithm.** They aren't. A great Reel can fail in Feed; optimize per surface.
- **Cross-posting from TikTok identically.** Even without watermark, the platform reads the visual style. Native shoot per platform.
- **Daily Reels with no carousel rhythm.** Audience burnout on a single format. Mix Reels, carousels, and Stories across the week.
- **Generic engagement-bait CTAs.** "Comment 'YES' if you agree" is now actively suppressed by IG's ranking system.
- **Posting Reels at random times.** IG's tier-test starts immediately; if your audience is asleep, the test fails. Check your analytics for peak audience hours.

## When this is the wrong platform

- **Pure B2B enterprise sales.** LinkedIn beats Instagram by an order of magnitude for that conversion path.
- **Real-time news commentary.** X is faster.
- **Long-form ideas needing >3 minutes.** YouTube long-form is the home.
- **Highly text-heavy content with no visual.** Twitter / threads / blog.
- **Audiences over 55.** Facebook still skews older; Instagram's main audience is 18–44.

## Cross-platform note

For BuildOS founder content specifically:

- **Instagram + carousels is the strongest combo.** ADHD-positioning, anti-feed framing, and "thinking environment" content all map cleanly to the carousel format (visual frameworks, contrast slides, framework lists).
- **Founder-shot Reels** with anti-feed visual signature (typing in BuildOS, walking, working on real projects) align with Mosseri's December 2025 raw-human shift.
- **DM-share-driving content** (educational frameworks, contrarian observations) benefits from Instagram's #1 unconnected-reach signal.
- **Avoid the AI-generated content trap** — Mosseri's December 2025 memo explicitly targets this, and BuildOS's anti-AI-marketing positioning aligns naturally with the platform's new direction.
