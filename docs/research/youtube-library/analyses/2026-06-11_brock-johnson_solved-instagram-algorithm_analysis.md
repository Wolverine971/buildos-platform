## Core Thesis

Three different goals — **growth, engagement, sales** — need **three different strategies**, and conflating them is the core mistake. **Stories don't grow you** (no new eyeballs) but are the **trust + sales engine** (DMs are where sales happen). **Feed posts grow you**, and the fastest growth lever is **shares**, engineered via **five categories of shareworthy content**. Growth = **hyper-consistency** (3–4 posts/day) on a **single niche**; quality is learned through volume ("300 bad posts before your first good one"). A 10-point **"start over" checklist** diagnoses dead accounts.

## Operating Lessons

### Three goals, three strategies (the framing that organizes everything)

- **Growth** (more followers): the simplest lever is **more posts** — at bats/reps. Caveat: only if quality holds and you don't burn out. → **hyper-consistency**.
- **Engagement** (active community, no sales push): **quality over quantity** of posts.
- **Sales**: **fewer, higher-quality** posts + **DM automation**; sales close **in the DMs.**

### Stories vs Feed — division of labor

> "Stories... do not help you grow... no new people are seeing them... but stories are really essential for making sales."

- **Stories:** existing-follower-only. Build with the **native stories camera** (raw, fast, documentary). A **"window into your life"** — can be personal/off-niche because the viewer already knows your niche; they're there to **know/like/trust you**. The **reply bar → DM → sale** is the conversion path.
- **Feed:** the growth surface; must be **niche** (≥90%; off-niche 5–10% max). An off-niche viral post is a **liability** (imports wrong-fit followers → lower engagement rate → harder future growth).

### Shareworthy content — why shares win + the 5 categories

> "Shares give the middle finger to the algorithm... I don't care who you think should see this, I'm showing it to my wife."

- Shares are the **most-prioritized engagement** type; they **override** the recommender (a share distributes content the algorithm wouldn't have). A share even to a non-Instagram user still counts — it brings/engages people on IG, which is what IG wants.
- **Multiplier (Brock's figure): 1 share ≈ 400 views.** Snowball: 3 shares → +1,000+; if 3% of those new viewers re-share, it compounds.
- **The five categories** (named by the phrase a viewer would say):
  1. **"I needed this"** — motivational/inspirational; powerful quotes; triumph/recovery-from-failure stories.
  2. **Call to Arms** — "we're all in this together / help spread this" (ALS Ice Bucket Challenge archetype; built-in tag-a-friend).
  3. **"That's me"** — relatable. (Worked example: Seinfeld "every entrepreneur forced to become a content creator" → ~100K likes, **~120K shares**, **7–8M views in days**.)
  4. **Feather-ruffling** — niche-relevant **contrarian** takes (e.g. "hashtags are overrated"); agreement → shares, disagreement → comment-argument engagement. NOT generic political flame-bait.
  5. **"In the know"** — timely pop-culture / breaking news / memes, **related back to your niche**; the speed window is short (a week-late meme is dead).
- **Edutainment rule:** pure education underperforms; **weave educational value INTO the five categories** (e.g. contrarian "hashtags" post teaches while ruffling feathers; a meme with a value caption). Even straight educational posts need a **strong hook + storytelling** to be "captivating."

### Growth mechanics — hyper-consistency + volume-before-quality

- **"The biggest secret of my growth is not consistency, it's hyper-consistency — 3–4 posts/day, every day, for ~3–4 years."**
- **"Make 300 shitty posts before your first good one."** Volume is the learning loop; the audience only shows up around post #301.
- **Consistency floor:** judge results only after **≥6 months** of consistency. (Strong tactical complement to the Mosseri-cluster's "small accounts graduate on rate" mechanic — volume is how you find the content that wins the audition.)

### Account health — the 10-point "start over" checklist

Brock's diagnostic: **5+ checked boxes → consider starting a fresh account.** Items: fake>real followers, completely changed niche, **3–4+ yr-old account with dead followers, engagement rate <1%, declining engagement rate MoM, 6+ months consistent but still flat, low-quality/forced content, inorganic growth used, multiple guideline violations, an off-niche viral that imported wrong-fit followers.** (Cautionary tale: Brock's 300K-follower TikTok built on dance virality was dead weight for his business → he restarted at 8K because the niche-aligned small account converts better.)

## Failure Modes

- **Conflating goals** — running a growth strategy when you need sales, or vice versa.
- **Niche-locking your stories** (kills the trust/personal function) OR **off-niching your feed** (kills growth + imports wrong-fit followers).
- **Pure-education content** with no hook/entertainment layer → underperforms.
- **Judging growth before 6 months** of consistency.
- **Buying followers / giveaways / engagement groups** → tanks engagement rate, digs the account deeper.
- **Chasing off-niche virality** → wrong-fit followers → lower future engagement rate.

## Contradictions / Tensions

1. **Single-story rule (time-evolved):** this 2024 interview leans on stories mechanics that the **2025 companion transcript shows Mosseri later "fixed"** (the single-story reach advantage is largely gone). When T5 writes the stories section, present **2024 → 2025 evolution**, not a flat rule.
2. **"1 share ≈ 400 views"** is **Brock-asserted**, no source. Useful as a directional heuristic, not a published constant. Mosseri (PRIMARY) confirms **sends are a top signal** and **rate beats raw views**, but gives **no share-to-view multiplier.**
3. **"Solved the algorithm" framing** overstates determinism; Mosseri (PRIMARY) stresses the system is many continuously-changing models with **no single gate** to game. Keep Brock's tactics as **practitioner heuristics**, not mechanics.
