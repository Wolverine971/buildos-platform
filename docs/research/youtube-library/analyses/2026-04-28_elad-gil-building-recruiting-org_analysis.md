## Core Thesis

Recruiting changes with company stage. Early startups should not assume recruiting can be delegated to a recruiter. In the 3-15 person stage, the founder and early team are the recruiting function; process support can help, but closing and credibility still come from the people building the company.

For BuildOS, the key insight is that solo-founder fundraising credibility depends on a concrete recruiting operating plan: who the founder needs next, how they will source them, how much founder time is reserved for recruiting, and what changes when hiring volume grows.

## Operating Lessons

### 1. Early recruiting is founder-led by default

At the 3-10 person stage, founder and employee networks are usually more useful than recruiters. Elad notes that many founders and early employees spend a large share of their time on recruiting while scaling from roughly 3 to 15 people.

Agent behavior:

- Treat early hiring as founder work, not HR work.
- Ask what percentage of founder time is reserved for recruiting.
- Turn the founder's network, advisors, investors, customers, and early users into a sourcing map.
- Push against "we will hire a recruiter" as the first answer.

### 2. Use coordination help before outsourcing judgment

An early coordinator or split-role operator can schedule candidates, track referrals, and handle lightweight outbound, but the founder or hiring manager should still carry the relationship and close.

Agent behavior:

- Separate sourcing, scheduling, evaluation, and closing.
- Identify which parts can be delegated without lowering candidate trust.
- Preserve founder involvement in high-leverage moments: warm intro, first serious conversation, offer, and close.

### 3. Candidate trust follows the strongest credible person

The article notes that outreach from a founder or senior title can improve response because candidates want direct exposure to key people.

Agent behavior:

- Use founder-led outreach for priority candidates.
- Draft short, specific founder notes instead of generic recruiter copy.
- Avoid handoffs that make candidates feel like they are entering a funnel.
- Make the candidate feel the mission, not just the role description.

### 4. Hire an in-house recruiter only when hiring volume justifies it

Elad suggests in-house recruiters begin making sense when the company is adding roughly 15-20 people per year or more. Before then, part-time, split-role, referral-heavy, or founder-led structures may fit better.

Agent behavior:

- Estimate hiring volume before recommending recruiting infrastructure.
- Avoid premature recruiting-org complexity.
- Use a part-time/split-role recruiter only when the pipeline volume requires coordination help.
- Keep hiring managers active even after recruiting support exists.

### 5. Recruiting roles specialize as hiring volume scales

At high growth, recruiting fragments into sourcers, recruiters, candidate researchers, recruiting marketing, and university programs. Early recruiters should often have sourcing ability because handoffs create friction.

Agent behavior:

- Map current stage before designing process.
- Keep early recruiting roles broad.
- Specialize only when repeated role families and candidate volume justify it.
- Watch for candidates falling through handoffs.

### 6. Executive search is different

For executive hires, stage and brand determine whether titles are inflated or deflated. Retained recruiters can help when the target candidates sit outside the startup's network or respond better to a known search firm.

Agent behavior:

- Do not treat executive search like IC hiring.
- Ask whether the startup's brand is strong enough to reach the target profile directly.
- Clarify whether the role title is being inflated to attract a senior operator or deflated because the company brand is strong.
- Use retained search selectively for true executive roles.

## Failure Modes This Prevents

- A solo founder has no credible answer for "how will you recruit?"
- A founder tries to outsource early hiring before they have a story candidates believe.
- The company hires a recruiter before hiring volume justifies it.
- Candidate experience breaks through too many handoffs.
- A founder confuses sourcing help with evaluation quality.
- Early hiring plans ignore the founder's own time allocation.
