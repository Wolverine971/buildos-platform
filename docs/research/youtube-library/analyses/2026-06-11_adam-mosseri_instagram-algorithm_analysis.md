## Core Thesis

There is **no single "algorithm."** Instagram runs many ranking systems; "the algorithm" is the colloquial sum. Organic ranking has **two sides**: (1) a **connected side** that ranks your post against everything else a follower could see, with **no gate or minimum threshold to clear**, and (2) an **unconnected side** that works like an **audition / graduation system** — every public post gets a small reserved batch of impressions, and content that beats the field on **interaction RATE** gets promoted to progressively larger unconnected audiences. Ranking is **consumer-oriented, not producer-oriented**: nothing meaningful happens at the moment you post; ranking happens later, when each viewer opens the app. "Shadowban" is real only in the narrow sense of **recommendation-guideline limits you can see and appeal** in Account Status.

Governing correction: _"We almost never make major changes to it. We constantly make small changes to try to improve it over time."_

## Operating Lessons

### 1 — There is no single algorithm, and big swings are not "a big change"

> "There isn't one algorithm. There's lots of things we do to try to understand how interested you are in certain topics... the sum of all of those things."

> "People often think that something major has changed in the algorithm... But the truth is, we almost never make major changes to it. We constantly make small changes to try to improve it over time."

**Decision rule:** when reach drops, **do not** assume a platform change. The default explanation should be **content-side** (lower interaction rate, off-niche subject) or a **recommendation-guideline issue you can see in Account Status** — not "they changed the algorithm."

### 2 — Ranking is consumer-side and happens AFTER you post

> "Nothing happens when you post right away. ... It's almost like a DVD added to a DVD collection, and then the next person who comes into the store, it's an option."

The system asks, per viewer, "who might be interested in this?" — it is **not** primarily reasoning about your account history. Implication: there is no "post and the clock starts" broadcast event; distribution accrues as your audience (and matched non-followers) open the app.

### 3 — The two sides of organic ranking

**Connected (followers):**

- When a follower opens IG, your post is ranked **against everything else they could see**, by relevance.
- **"There's no sort of gate or minimum thing or threshold that you have to cross."** (Directly contradicts the popular "you must hit X early engagement to unlock follower reach" myth.)
- You structurally **cannot reach most followers**: ~half aren't on IG that day; of the rest, ~half don't scroll far enough.

**Unconnected (non-followers) — the audition system:**

- Every public post that's trying to reach people gets a **minimum reserved number of views/impressions** ("we set aside a little bit").
- If it does well, it **graduates** to more unconnected accounts. Well again → graduates again. **"Almost like a competition."**
- This is the explicit mechanism by which **very small accounts go viral**.

### 4 — Interaction RATE is the currency, not view count

> "What matters is not really the number of views. It's the interaction rates."

- The signals named in this transcript: **likes per impression** and **sends to a friend** (DM shares). Comment rate is used as the worked example.
- **Worked delta:** average post ≈ 5–6% like rate, ≈ 1% comment rate. A post at **1.1%** comment rate vs 1.0% "is actually a big delta" and gets "picked up in the ranking system." (Treat these % as **illustrative**, not published thresholds.)
- **Caution:** "It's best not to overreact to the first hour." Early signal is real but noisy.

### 5 — "Shadowban" demystified (PRIMARY, appealable)

> "Your account can have its reach limited, but you can go to Profile → Settings → Account Status and then see what's going on and then act on that information."

Reach can be limited when:

1. You recently had a **bunch of content taken down**, OR
2. You posted content that **doesn't violate community guidelines but violates recommendation guidelines** → it won't be shown to non-followers (you can still see it yourself).

**Decision rule:** before believing you're "shadowbanned," open **Account Status**; appeal if you think it's a mistake, or remove the offending content.

### 6 — The politics myth corrected

> "It's not that... we want to downrank politics. We don't do that."

The reach drop after an off-profile political post is because **your followers didn't follow you for politics**, so they engage less per impression → it looks less interesting to the system → lower reach. The system **doesn't even need to know it's about politics.** Accounts that post politics consistently do fine. Generalizes to a **subject-pivot penalty**: any sharp pivot to or away from your normal topic depresses engagement-per-impression and therefore reach.

### 7 — Format is personal, not a platform rule

Host Ben: a meme reaches **4× a video** for him. Mosseri: "**It's not always the case that memes do better than videos. It depends on the person and the content.**" He hears regularly from creators for whom **carousels (feed photos) or photo-stories beat video**. (Important: this **directly tempers** any "Reels always win" claim.)

### 8 — Translation as a reach lever

Auto-translation supports **English, Spanish, Portuguese, Hindi** — chosen because India and Brazil are massive and Spanish spans many countries. It increases reach because more language-circles can understand the content, and **"it'll help with reach more and more over time"** as the feature matures. Not a ranking boost — a comprehension/addressable-audience expansion.

### 9 — "Tune your algorithm" (the malleable-algorithm test)

New feature in test (US, small %, Reels first): you can **see what IG thinks you're interested in**, **add** missed interests, **remove** wrong ones, or say **"show me none of X."** Reels you asked for get tagged (e.g. "parenting advice") so you can see the cause. Inspired by the Threads **"Dear Algo"** meme. Target rollout: "over the next couple months" from Nov 2025.

## Failure Modes (per Mosseri)

- **Blaming "a big algorithm change"** for normal reach variance — almost always wrong; changes are continuous and small.
- **Believing in a hard early-engagement gate for follower reach** — Mosseri explicitly says there is no minimum threshold to cross on the connected side.
- **Over-reacting to the first hour** of a post's performance.
- **Reading comments in the first hour** — the earliest commenters skew hostile; wait 1–2 hours.
- **Assuming you're shadowbanned** without checking Account Status (where real limits are visible + appealable).
- **Pivoting subjects** (politics or otherwise) and misreading the resulting reach drop as suppression of the topic rather than misalignment with why people followed you.
- **Generalizing one creator's format edge** (memes 4× video) into a universal rule.

He says interaction **rate** matters (likes/impression, sends, comments as the example) and gives **no watch-time figure** and **no "3-second" threshold**. The current `instagram.md` "three top signals + first 3 seconds" claim is **NOT supported by this primary transcript** — label it secondary/asserted until a primary Jan-2025 source is found.
2. **No "minimum early views to unlock reach" gate.** The popular checkpoint myth is contradicted directly for the connected side. The unconnected side has a reserved view **budget**, but that is the platform seeding a test — not a creator-side hurdle.
3. **Format hierarchy.** Mosseri **declines** to rank Reels > carousels > photos universally ("depends on the person and the content"). Any reference asserting a fixed format ranking should be softened.
4. **"December 2025 raw-human / anti-AI memo."** Not present in this transcript (recorded Nov 2025). Mosseri here is *optimistic* about AI ("we should try to get to a place where you can talk to the algorithm"). The anti-AI-suppression claim in `instagram.md` has **no basis in this primary source** — keep flagged as unverified.

## BuildOS Application

1. **Anti-feed / thinking-environment content:** the **interaction-RATE-over-views** mechanic is the strongest argument that a small, sharply-niched BuildOS account can out-distribute large generic ones — every post auditions on rate, and small accounts graduate if rate beats the field.
2. **DM-share funnel:** Mosseri confirms (in the Brock-quoted clip + the DMs-beat-feed-for-sharing point here) that **sends** are a top reach signal. "Send this to someone who's drowning in tabs" CTAs map directly to BuildOS's relief positioning.
3. **Reframe "the algorithm changed":** internal growth diagnostics should default to **content-side and Account-Status** explanations before assuming platform changes — matches Mosseri's own framing.
