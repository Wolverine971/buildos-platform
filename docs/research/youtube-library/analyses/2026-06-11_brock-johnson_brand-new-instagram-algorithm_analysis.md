## Core Thesis

Instagram crossed **3 billion monthly active users** and is reorganizing the app around the **three drivers of that growth: messaging (DMs), Reels, and recommendations** (PRIMARY, Mosseri clip). For creators this means: **shares are the goal** (content moves person-to-person in DMs more than on feed), **niche is now mandatory** (the recommendation engine needs a single category to place you), and four 2025 feature changes alter tactics — a **customizable "tune your algorithm,"** a **story-reach fix** that ends the single-story advantage, **trial-reel caps + anti-duplicate enforcement**, and a **Meta Verified search-ranking boost**.

## Operating Lessons

### The growth drivers (PRIMARY)

> Mosseri clip: "We're going to continue to focus on those products and reorient the app more around DMs, Reels, and recommendations over the next couple months."

- **Messaging is #1.** Brock: more content is shared in DMs daily than is posted to feed → optimize for **sendability**. (Reconciles with Mosseri PRIMARY in the companion transcript that **sends/interaction rate** drive reach.)
- **Reels** reach the most non-followers of any format.
- **Recommendations** = posts shown to non-followers based on inferred interest → why **niche** is now decisive.

### Niche is the placement key (secondary, but reinforced by Mosseri's primary "subject-pivot penalty")

> Brock: "If you post one reel that is a puppy training video, one reel about your hike... you're going to confuse the algorithm. And when you confuse, you lose."

This is Brock's tactical restatement of the same mechanic Mosseri describes primarily as a **subject-pivot / off-profile engagement penalty**. **Decision rule:** one account = one searchable category; keep ≥90% of feed posts on-niche (see sibling analysis: 5%, 10% max off-niche).

### UPDATE 1 — Tune your algorithm (PRIMARY feature)

- Access: Settings, **or** open any reel → settings icon (top-right).
- Top of screen: **3 interests IG thinks you're most into recently.** Below: a fuller predicted-interest list, fully editable (**add / remove**, or "show me none of X").
- Interests are **1–2-word categories** = niches. **Strategic read (Brock):** when users add YOUR niche category, they become more likely to see your content → reinforces niche-down for small accounts.

### UPDATE 2 — Story-reach fix (PRIMARY)

> Mosseri clip: "People were complaining about getting less reach... if they posted lots of stories in the same day. That is not at all the intended behavior... we've addressed the issue. ... no longer will posting more and more stories in the same day decrease the reach... particularly that first story."

- **Before:** one story → high views; second/third → reach dropped sharply (the basis of Brock's "Single" rule).
- **After:** the gap is **balanced**, not inverted. Posting more no longer tanks reach; posting one is **no longer disproportionately rewarded**.
- **Brock's still-recommended STD strategy:** **S**ingle (smaller edge, still real) · **T**ext-heavy · **D**M-automation (reply-keyword → auto-DM link; a top story-view lever for ~5–6 years).

### UPDATE 3 — Trial reels: caps + anti-duplicate (mixed PRIMARY/secondary)

> Mosseri (story quote): "Don't try to use them just to pummel the same content over and over again. ... That's become a bit of a spam vector that we are addressing."

- **Caps (secondary, asserted figures):** non-universal per-day limit. Brock's account = **5/day**; others 20–25/day or uncapped. Exceeding → **24-hour block**; some users → **30-day** restriction. **Treat the specific numbers as Brock-asserted, unsourced.**
- **Anti-duplicate (PRIMARY mechanic, secondary specifics):** identical trial reels are throttled. Each of the 5 needs **non-minimal variation** focused on the **first 6–7 seconds of on-screen visual** (re-edit/re-film the hook). Minor changes (caption, audio swap, text tweak) **don't count**. Comparison is **indefinite** (not daily reset) — IG checks all future trial reels against the first.
- **Normal feed reels are exempt:** exact-duplicate re-upload is fine after **~a week**; no deletion or disclosure required. (Brock: confirmed with IG staff.)

### UPDATE 4 — Meta Verified search boost (secondary)

Meta Verified handles surface **at the top of IG search**. Brock recommends it as a **trust/seriousness signal** plus impersonation protection and 1:1 support; the search-ranking boost is the new functional benefit.

## Failure Modes

- **Mixed-niche feed** → recommender can't place you → poor unconnected reach.
- **Spamming identical trial reels** → throttled as a spam vector (now enforced; PRIMARY).
- **Exceeding your trial-reel cap** → 24-hr (or reported 30-day) block. Cap is account-specific; assume **5/day** if unsure.
- **Minimal trial-reel variation** (caption/audio only) → counted as a duplicate.
- **Re-uploading the same content too soon** even to normal feed (< ~1 week) → IG staff advise against.
- **Assuming the single-story rule still gives a big edge** — the gap is now small post-fix.

## Contradictions / Tensions

1. **Single-story rule:** the **sibling Brock transcript (2024)** treats "Single" as a strong lever; **this 2025 transcript reports Mosseri fixed it** so the edge is now small. T5 should present this as a **time-evolution**, not a flat rule.
2. **"Algorithm speed lag vs TikTok"** (5–10 vs 20–30 videos) is **Brock-asserted illustrative**, not from Mosseri. Mosseri (companion transcript) confirms IG _wants_ to let you "talk to the algorithm" but gives no adaptation-speed numbers.
3. **Trial-reel cap numbers (25/20/5/day, 30-day ban)** are **unsourced anecdotes** — only the existence of caps + the anti-duplicate "spam vector" are PRIMARY.
4. **"Followers don't matter much"** — Brock softens this himself: followers still expand the candidate pool and IG shows followers first. Don't over-read into "followers are irrelevant."

## BuildOS Application

1. **Sendability over polish:** with DMs the #1 driver, BuildOS anti-feed content should be explicitly **DM-shareable** ("send this to the person with 40 open tabs") — aligns with relief positioning.
2. **Trial reels as a two-cadence engine:** run discovery experiments as trial reels (respect the **5/day** + **substantial-variation** rules), then graduate winners to the feed — lets the founder test anti-AI hooks without burning follower-feed.
3. **Niche discipline:** keep the BuildOS account a single searchable category ("thinking environment / messy-thinking-to-structured-work") so the recommender + tune-your-algorithm adds place it cleanly.
