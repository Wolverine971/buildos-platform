# Erika Hall - UX Research At Scale And Right-Sized Learning

## Skill Routing

- Primary child skill: `usability_quick_research`
- Secondary routing: `information_architecture_review` when the research question is about labels, conceptual models, navigation, or wayfinding.
- Runtime impact: upgrade quick research from a methods checklist into a decision-first learning workflow.

## Core Thesis

Hall's practical research model starts with goals and decisions, not methods. Teams fight about methodology when they have not aligned on what they need to know, what success means, and what assumptions are driving the work. Research is the act of becoming informed enough to make a better decision.

This source is especially useful because it adds organizational realism: research objections often sound like schedule or budget concerns, but the deeper issue is usually fear, incentives, unclear decision rights, or a desire to avoid learning something inconvenient.

## Behavior-Changing Principles

### 1. Start with goals before methods

Hall repeatedly returns to the same sequence: define goals, define success, identify what needs to be known, identify assumptions, then choose a method.

Agent rule: never answer "what research method should we use?" until the decision and unknowns are named.

### 2. Research buy-in should be framed as risk reduction

Hall argues that the word research can trigger objections. The useful frame is making better informed decisions, increasing chance of success, and decreasing risk.

Agent rule: when stakeholders resist research, reframe the ask around the bet size and decision risk.

### 3. Bigger bets need more confidence

The amount of research depends on how large the decision is, how much is already known, and how uncertain the team is. Small decisions can use lightweight methods; large bets deserve stronger evidence.

Agent rule: include a bet-size estimate in every research plan.

### 4. Brainstorm questions, not ideas

Hall rejects idea brainstorming as a weak default. A better team exercise is to list unknowns, rank them by risk, and decide what learning would change the next decision.

Agent rule: when facilitating early UI/UX discovery, produce a ranked unknowns list before solution ideas.

### 5. Research is continuous learning

The team is never "done" researching. The correct question is what learning is needed for this decision and this stage.

Agent rule: recommend a cadence, not a one-off study, when the user asks how to institutionalize research.

### 6. Internal research comes first when buy-in or alignment is weak

If decision makers block research, the research target may be the organization itself: who decides, what they value, what incentives exist, and what language will make evidence useful.

Agent rule: include stakeholder interviews or decision-maker mapping when research is blocked.

### 7. Existing research must answer today's question

Hall treats old research as potentially useful but not automatically authoritative. Start with what the team needs to know now, then see whether old evidence answers that question with enough confidence.

Agent rule: when handed old research, audit it against current questions, evidence quality, date, participant fit, and decision relevance.

### 8. Participant quality is the insight quality

If the team cannot talk to current customers, they can use representative proxies or analogous research, but the recruiting logic must be explicit. Generic participant pools can produce low-confidence answers.

Agent rule: every research plan must name who qualifies, why they represent the target, and what proxy limitations remain.

### 9. Interface quality is language quality

Hall calls out language as core interface material. Clear, concise writing can solve many problems that teams try to solve with visual or technical complexity.

Agent rule: pair quick research with copy and label review when users misunderstand a UI.

## Quick Research Output Contract

When this source is loaded, the agent should produce research plans in this shape:

| Field                   | Required answer                                  |
| ----------------------- | ------------------------------------------------ |
| Decision                | What will change based on the learning?          |
| Bet size                | Low, medium, or high risk/cost.                  |
| Current belief          | What does the team think is true?                |
| Unknowns                | What must be learned next?                       |
| Disconfirming evidence  | What would change the team's mind?               |
| Method                  | Smallest credible way to learn it.               |
| Participants or proxies | Who qualifies and why.                           |
| Output                  | Decision, design change, risk, or next question. |
