## Why this draft

- **Led with BuildOS, not Curri.** GovEagle's job description is full of phrases — "long-running LLM workloads," "async processing," "background job orchestration," "AI pipelines" — that map directly to BuildOS's queue + multi-LLM routing layer. That's the most directly transferable thread.
- **Bridge sentence does the work.** "So when I read the role is about optimizing AI pipelines… that felt really close to what I've been heads-down on" — explicitly draws the line from past work to their problem.
- **Government / USMC angle is one line, not a paragraph.** Adds credibility for a govcontractor company without becoming a credential dump. "Scout Sniper Team Leader" is specific and memorable — worth the extra words.
- **Python gap disclosed up front.** Their listing explicitly invites applicants who don't meet every qualification, so this is the right move. The framing "shipped C# and Java at scale and ramp quickly on languages" is honest and signals adaptability without overclaiming.
- **No bulleted resume dump.** Prose only, per voice rules.

## Considerations / open questions

- **In-person NYC.** The listing is explicitly in-person in NYC. If DJ isn't NYC-based or willing to relocate, this is a hard blocker — should probably be addressed in conversation, not in the cold message.
- **Python gap is real.** Their requirement is "deep expertise in Python." Honest disclosure is the right play, but expect this to be the first conversation point.
- **No named contact.** Listing didn't name a hiring manager — opened with "Hey" instead of a name. If a name surfaces (e.g. CTO on LinkedIn), prefer that.
