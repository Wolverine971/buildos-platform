## Why this draft (v2 — rules-aligned)

- **Aligned with `cold-outreach-rules.md`.** Stripped "the mission lands for me" line (anti-pattern) and the "A bit about me:" transition (anti-pattern).
- **Real connection now leads: government contracting + USMC.** This is concrete lived experience — the kind of factual overlap the rules require — not an emotional mission statement.
- **Two-thread structure.** First thread: govcontract background as the audience-fit. Second thread: BuildOS as the directly-transferable backend work for their specific role responsibilities. Each thread has its own bridge sentence.
- **Python gap stays.** Honest disclosure remains the right move; their listing explicitly invites applicants who don't meet every qualification.
- **~210 words.** Slightly longer than the GoGo draft because two distinct threads do real work — the government background and the LLM-workload work each justify themselves.

## Considerations / open questions

- **In-person NYC.** The listing is explicitly in-person in NYC. If DJ isn't NYC-based or willing to relocate, this is a hard blocker — should probably be addressed in conversation, not in the cold message.
- **Python gap is real.** Their requirement is "deep expertise in Python." Honest disclosure is the right play, but expect this to be the first conversation point.
- **No named contact.** Listing didn't name a hiring manager — opened with "Hey" instead of a name. If a name surfaces (e.g. CTO on LinkedIn), prefer that.
