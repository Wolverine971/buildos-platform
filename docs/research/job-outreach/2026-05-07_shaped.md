## Why this draft

- **Aligned with `cold-outreach-rules.md`.** No mission-affirmation line, no "A bit about me:" transition, single explicit bridge sentence ("So when I read that the role involves shaping ShapedQL — semantics, validation, query planning — that felt really close to what I worked on").
- **Real connection leads: SPARQL query-builder → ShapedQL.** The Webworld work is the closest factual overlap in DJ's background to a declarative query language with semantics, validation, and query planning. It's a concrete prior shipped project, not an aspirational statement.
- **Two threads, but tightly bounded.** First thread: query-language / retrieval credentials (Webworld + Elasticsearch). Second thread: BuildOS for the "agents" side of Shaped's pitch (real-time retrieval engine for search, feeds, **and agents**). Each thread carries its own bridge.
- **Curri integrations layer left out on purpose.** It's the strongest credential for ops/integrations roles but doesn't map to a query-language / retrieval engine company. Pulling the most relevant thread, not the most prestigious one.
- **Multi-LLM router left out on purpose.** Shaped is a retrieval engine, not an LLM gateway. The agentic chat / tool calling / context engineering is the more direct overlap with their "agents" framing.
- **Python and Rust gaps disclosed honestly.** Their listing requires both. Honest disclosure is the right move and also signals the stack was actually read.
- **~195 words.** Same range as GoGo v3 (140) and Clad v2 (150) — slightly longer because two distinct threads (query language + agents) each justify themselves.
