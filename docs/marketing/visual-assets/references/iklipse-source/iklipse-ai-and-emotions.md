# Source Analysis — @iklipse\_ "AI and Emotions (Look Closer)"

**URL:** https://www.instagram.com/p/DX2XN1XDXYS/ · **Slides:** 9 · **Likes:** 129 · Posted 2026-05-02

## Concept (important: this one breaks the house template)

Unlike the other decks, this is **not** a text-driven teaching carousel. It's a **wordless gallery of emotionally-charged AI scenes** — every slide is just a photograph (chrome only, no overlay copy), and the audience is asked to _feel_ before they're told anything. The entire lesson lives in the **caption**, and the payoff is a single text closer. The hook isn't "here's a framework," it's "**look closer — you can't tell these are fake, because the emotion is real.**"

## Beat skeleton

Hook ("look closer") → **gallery of single-emotion scenes, no text** → text closer that names the principle + asks "which felt most real?" comment CTA.

The engagement engine is **identification + choice**: the viewer scans the emotions, finds the one that hits them, and the CTA ("which felt most real?") converts that private recognition into a public comment. No formula, no checklist — pure emotional resonance + a pick-one prompt.

## Highest-value extractables

- **The "pick the one that's most you" CTA** over a gallery is a powerful, low-effort comment driver — and it maps _perfectly_ onto 9takes.
- **"It's the driver, not the car"** — a quotable reframe (the tool isn't the differentiator, the human behind it is).
- **Caption-carries-the-lesson** model: when the images are strong enough, you don't need text on them. Worth keeping in the toolkit for visually-driven posts.

## 9takes translation — strong fit

This is arguably the most natural 9takes format in the set. Two direct re-skins:

1. **"One moment, nine reactions."** Nine wordless scenes of the same situation (e.g. a breakup text) read nine ways — one per type. Closer: _"Which reaction was most you? Comment your number."_ This IS the 9takes product (one situation, nine ways to see it) as a gallery.
2. **"AI and Emotions" literal re-skin:** a gallery of familiar emotional micro-scenes, closer "which felt the most real?" — then in replies, tie each emotion to the type that runs on it.
   The "pick-one → comment" mechanic is the single best engagement device for our give-first funnel: recognition is the first step toward contributing. Amber gradient on the closer slide.
