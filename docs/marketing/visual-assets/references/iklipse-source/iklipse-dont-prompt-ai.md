## Why this one over-performed (model the hook + CTA)

- **Hook = a命令 reframe:** "DON'T [the obvious verb everyone uses]. [Better verb] instead." Negation + correction in 4 words. This is the single most engaging hook pattern in the account.

## Beat skeleton

Hook (Don't X / do Y) → promise ("here's how") → **6× [named Step + option-bank menu + a one-line maxim]** → "still want the experts?" service CTA.

Each step ships an **option menu** (Mood: dark/soft/aggressive…) — these menus are the save-able payload, like the word-banks in Weird AI and the angle cards in the cheat sheet. The per-step maxims ("Camera = 50% of the result," "Same subject ≠ Same result," "Style = Identity") are quotable.

## 9takes translation

"**Don't judge people. Read them instead.**" Then a 6-step process, each with a menu:

1. **Lock the situation** (what actually happened)
2. **Name the reaction** (menu: defends / withdraws / attacks / pleases / fixes…)
3. **Find the driver** (menu: fear / control / approval / security / autonomy…)
4. **Match the type** (the Nine)
5. **Check the arrow** (stress vs growth behavior)
6. **Drop your assumption** (keep only what they actually showed you)
   CTA: provocative opener + "Comment NINE and we'll send the type-by-type read." This deck's hook+CTA pattern is the highest-EV thing to copy in the whole set.
