## Story decision

**Recognizable moment:** a writer finally gets a short writing window and spends it reconstructing the book.
**One pain:** the re-entry tax.
**One claim:** a manuscript's surrounding project context can go cold even when the draft was saved.
**Emotional turn:** the invisible burden becomes a visible, resumable project state.
**CTA:** “Where does your book's context live?”

## Receipt

**Real source:** a live BuildOS run captured in the Author Workflow Teardown assets.
**What happened:** one dense revision brain dump created a real `Novel Revision Project` with a goal, scene-level tasks, continuity work, and a suggested next move.
**What was manual:** the manuscript scenario and brain-dump text were prepared as an internal example; the product run itself was real. The composition, sequencing, and annotations are editorial.
**What remains imperfect:** the two continuity items appear primarily as tasks rather than two fully surfaced risk objects, and this is not a timed return-after-delay test.
**Proof status:** internal BuildOS example, not a customer story or testimonial.
**Permission:** owned internal product capture; no customer or third-party footage.
**Privacy:** no private customer data is shown.

## Product boundary

**BuildOS does:** hold the project state around the manuscript—current state, open threads, decisions, tasks, and next moves.
**BuildOS does not claim:** to replace the drafting tool, write the manuscript, automatically maintain a complete story bible, provide a special `Memory Mode`, or guarantee a resume time.
**Required caption label:** “real BuildOS run using an internal manuscript example.”
