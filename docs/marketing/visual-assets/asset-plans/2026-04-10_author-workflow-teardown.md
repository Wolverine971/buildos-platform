## The idea (what this says)

Your manuscript is not the only thing you're holding — every scattered decision about it is a weight too, and that pile can finally live in one place that remembers it _with_ you instead of demanding you remember it.

## Emotional truth

**The relief of setting down an invisible pile you didn't know you were carrying.** Not "look at this productivity tool" — the bodily exhale of the muddy middle turning out to be a memory problem, and the memory problem being solved. Every choice below serves that exhale.

## Story test

- **Character:** the novelist mid-revision — alive draft, dead system. Holding four places' worth of decisions about a manuscript they love. The reader asks _"that's me."_
- **Conflict:** the revision notes live in a Google Doc, a phone note, a monitor sticky, and a voice memo never transcribed. The first 20 minutes of every session is reloading your own brain. The continuity bug with Marcus in chapter 12 and the warehouse-scene change that might break chapters 3–4 are floating, unheld. The reader asks _"why is this so hard?"_
- **Curiosity:** what does it actually look like when you dump the whole mess into one place and two minutes later have a revision plan with the continuity issues flagged and the next move obvious? Show the _before_ clearly; make them want to see the _after_ / take the free teardown.

## Reference feel

A field-notes page that finally got organized — ink on warm paper, a writer's desk after the fog lifts. Calm, literary, handled. Passes "would a mid-revision novelist stop scrolling?" because it names their exact invisible weight before it sells anything.

## Vision

**Shared signature (constant across all 3 assets):**

- Single **burnt-orange `#b85416`** accent, one element per asset (never the old amber/gold).
- Inkprint **paper/ink** finish — light "paper studio" mode (`#fbfaf7` bg, `#16161a` ink). Literary serif (IBM Plex Serif) for the headline voice; Inter for labels/UI.
- The **before → after texture shift carries the transformation**: _before_ wears **Static** (overwhelm); _after_ wears **Frame**/**Thread** (structure + memory).
- Light chrome: small BuildOS wordmark, bottom corner; handle/URL stays in the caption.
- Every asset rides a **real receipt** (a real BuildOS screen or real footage).

## Assets (why + caption + brief)

### Asset 1 — The mess → the revision plan (HERO)

**Why this asset:** This is the literal proof the post promises ("Two minutes later I had a revision plan with the continuity issues flagged"). It makes the transformation undeniable and is the strongest receipt — real input, real output, no narration needed.

**Caption / on-card copy** (DJ voice, on a real receipt): _Left is the actual brain dump — Chapter 7, the Marcus continuity bug, the warehouse scene that might break chapters 3 and 4. Right is what BuildOS gave back two minutes later. I didn't organize anything. I just talked through the mess._

**Receipt:** real BuildOS brain-dump screen with the campaign brain-dump text typed in (left) → the real structured revision-plan project: tasks + continuity risks flagged + one visible next move (right). Capture both from the live product (or clearly-labeled staging).

**Production brief** — built on the before/after skeleton:

    BuildOS before/after — 4:5 portrait (also export 1:1 for X).
    Capture BEFORE (real): the BuildOS brain-dump input screen with the messy revision paragraph typed in
      ("Chapter 7 needs a complete rewrite... Marcus... warehouse scene... chapters 3 and 4"). Texture-feel:
      one dense unstructured block, cursor mid-thought, weak hierarchy — mentally expensive.
    Capture AFTER (real): the resulting BuildOS project — the revision plan: scene-level tasks listed,
      the two continuity issues surfaced as flagged risks (Marcus ch12; warehouse vs ch3-4), and ONE clear
      "next move" visible. Texture-feel: calm, one home, strong hierarchy.
    Layout: side-by-side, BEFORE on left; a single burnt-orange (#b85416) arrow/divider between them.
    Labels: eyebrow micro-label "BEFORE / AFTER" (uppercase, 0.15em tracking). Accent = the divider arrow only.
    Treatment: real UI with realistic data; crop tight to the content; light chrome (small BuildOS mark, bottom-right).
    Constraints: screenshots are REAL product (or clearly-labeled staging) — never AI, never mock-as-proof; legible at phone size.

_Vary: also try stacked (before top / after bottom) for the IG scroll-reveal; and a tighter crop that leads on the two flagged continuity risks._ · _Kill rule: keep only the pair where a reader grasps "scattered → structured" in 2 seconds and the flagged risks are legible._ · _Refine in-tool; recapture, don't over-annotate._

**Why this asset:** The scroll-stopper. Draft C's pain-list is a perfect Inkprint card — it names the invisible pile before any pitch, which is exactly the relief move. Works as the LinkedIn post image, an X quote card, or carousel slide 1.

**Caption / on-card copy** (verbatim on the card): eyebrow `THE MUDDY MIDDLE`; headline _Your manuscript is not the only thing you're holding._; body (the pile) —

- the timeline fix you wrote on your phone at midnight

**Receipt:** this is an explanatory Inkprint graphic (allowed), but it should be posted _alongside_ Asset 1 so the thesis card rides a real screen, never standing alone as a vibe.

**Production brief** — built on the Inkprint card skeleton:

    BuildOS Inkprint card — pain-list, 4:5 portrait.
    Mode: light "paper studio". Background: #fbfaf7, texture tx-static tx-weak (overwhelm).
    Eyebrow (micro-label, uppercase): THE MUDDY MIDDLE.
    Dominant (the one thing): headline "Your manuscript is not the only thing you're holding." — IBM Plex Serif, deep ink #16161a.
    Body: the 5-item pile, Inter, muted ink #66666e, set slightly cramped/stacked so the LIST itself feels like a pile

    Closing line: "It's not a craft problem. It's a memory problem." — small, accent color, bottom.
    Chrome: small BuildOS wordmark bottom-right. No URL on card.
    Type: one dominant element, top-weighted, generous breathing room above the headline.

_Vary: also try dark "ink room" mode for a moodier feed; and a version where the closing "memory problem" line is the dominant instead of the pile._ · _Kill rule: keep only the version where the headline lands first and the pile reads as weight, not as a tidy bulleted list._ · _Build in Canva or gemini-imagegen; lock this palette/type and reuse for the set._

### Asset 3 — Talk through the mess (hero storyboard)

**Why this asset:** The "hero video" option from the post. A 20–30s screen recording is the highest-proof version — real cursor, real product, real two-minute turn. Use the exact campaign brain-dump so the post, the card, and the video all tell one story (Marcus, the warehouse, the revision plan).

**Caption / on-card copy** (DJ voice): _My chapter-revision notes lived in four places. Here's me dumping the whole mess into one — and what came back two minutes later._

**Receipt:** real BuildOS screen recording of the brain dump → structured revision plan. No simulated UI animation.

**Production brief** — built on the storyboard skeleton:

    BuildOS hero storyboard — ~25s, 9:16 (also export 1:1).
    The input (verbatim to type or speak): "Chapter 7 needs a complete rewrite. The pacing is off after the
      reveal in chapter 5. Sarah's motivation doesn't connect to chapter 12 anymore. I have notes in my Google
      Doc but I also wrote something on my phone about the timeline. The beta reader said the middle drags. I
      think I need to move the confrontation earlier but that breaks the subplot with Marcus. Also I changed the
      warehouse scene and need to check if it contradicts chapters 3 and 4."
    Beat 1 [0–5s] — Screen: the messy input field, text appearing as DJ talks. Caption: "revision notes in four places".
    Beat 2 [5–10s] — Over-the-shoulder of the screen: hit process; structure begins to form. Caption: "I didn't organize anything".
    Beat 3 [10–18s] — Close-up: the revision plan resolves — scene tasks, the two continuity issues flagged
      (Marcus ch12; warehouse vs ch3-4). Caption: "two minutes later".
    Payoff [18–25s] — The one clear next move highlighted in burnt orange. Caption: "the project remembers it with me".
    Constraints: real product, real cursor, real data; no simulated animation of the UI; no AI footage; burnt-orange = only the next-move highlight.

_Vary: also cut a 10s version that opens on the close-up of the flagged continuity risks (lead with the payoff)._ · _Kill rule: keep only the take where the plan visibly resolves on screen — if the turn isn't legible, recapture._ · _Refine by re-recording, not by adding motion graphics._
