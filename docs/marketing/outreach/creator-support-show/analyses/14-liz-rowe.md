# Liz Rowe — Notion + web design + books (the niche overlap)

## Profile

- **Channel:** [@lizrowe](https://www.youtube.com/@lizrowe)
- **Subs:** 14,000.
- **Typical views:** 500-5,000.
- **Overperformer:** One video did 100K+ ("I obsessed over becoming a millionaire" after C&S worked it).
- **Transition:** Pivoting from "software + books" to **books only** ("it's really what I love").
- **Audience:** Self-driven entrepreneurs / progress-seekers in their 20s-30s.
- **Connection point:** Brought in via Spotter Studio summit.

## What she's building

Educational book-review channel positioned for Ali Abdaal–style audience. She reads, condenses, and packages the lessons. Was previously split between Notion / web design tutorials and book reviews; now consolidating on books.

## The on-air problem (verbatim)

> "Right now, it's software and books, which I know is a little broad. And I need to niche down. And I'm in a transition period to focus solely on books — because it's really what I love."

> "I need all the support I can get."

## Colin & Samir's diagnosis

1. **Title rework** — "I read every millionaire-titled book" → "I obsessed over becoming a millionaire." (Verb upgrade is the unlock.)
2. **Hook structure (first 30s):** 0-7s confirm click → 7-25s establish video + make it personal → 25-30s open new loop with "but."
3. **Open loop discipline.** Don't answer the question in the hook (don't reveal "it was the 401k" up front).
4. **Mark Rober progress-map / multi-experiment hook** model.

## Verbatim pain quotes

1. "**It's a little broad. I need to niche down.**"
2. "I struggled with do I show all the books? Do I say the books? Do I just have them flash up?"
3. "I need all the support I can get."

## Tool / workflow signal

- **Notion power user** — channel was originally Notion-focused. Likely runs her own creator process in Notion.
- **Book corpus** — reading multiple books per video. The "obsessed over millionaires" video required reading 7+ books on the topic.
- **Spotter Studio** — already using it. Mature tool stack mindset.
- **Web design background** — technical literacy, can integrate / wire things up.

## BuildOS angle

Liz is an under-the-radar perfect fit — Notion-native, book-research-heavy, transitioning her entire channel direction. **Each video is essentially a book project** — read 5–7 books, extract three themes, package the lessons. The next video does the same thing on a different topic. The corpus should compound (a book cited in video #4 should be available for video #11) but currently doesn't. She's also the audience BuildOS naturally appeals to — Notion power-users who realize Notion graveyards their work over time (per the persona doc).

**Pitch shape:** "You're niching down to books only. Each video is a research project — 5-7 books read, three themes extracted, the open-loop hook built. Right now your Notion holds it, and Notion graveyards work over a year. BuildOS holds one project per video, with the book corpus underneath — so the next millionaire / mindset / productivity video reads from everything you've already annotated."
