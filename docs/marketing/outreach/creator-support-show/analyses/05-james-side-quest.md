# James — Side Quest James / "More of James"

## Profile

- **Short-form channel:** [@sidequestjames](https://www.youtube.com/@sidequestjames) — $1 vs mystery gift man-on-the-street format. Big Instagram presence.
- **Long-form channel:** "More of James" — started fresh from zero. One video posted (94K views, fueled by a 9.4M-view IG short with a call-to-action).
- **Recent life:** Just exited a 2-year relationship 2 months ago. Going through a midlife reset moment.
- **Inspirations:** Vintage David Dobrik, 2016-19 vloggers, Yes Theory (older). Doesn't want to be "optimized."
- **Income:** From short-form. Long-form = creative outlet.

## What he's building

A long-form vlog channel for personal storytelling. Allergic to MrBeast-style optimization. Wants to make personal, vulnerable, real long-form content but doesn't know the engine format.

## The on-air problem (verbatim)

> "I want to move from short form to long form and I don't know how."

> "I think this is my problem. I love that 2016 to 19 era of vloggers… This seems very like optimized. **It just seems** — I don't know if it's like the artist in me that wants like a thumbnail that's like color graded and looks like a film photo and then the title is just like one word with a period."

> "I do want this to be a consistent thing… I'm trying to make another video which I sent you guys some titles and thumbs."

## Colin & Samir's diagnosis

1. **Content Market Fit** — Three circles: what you want to make × audience exists × right platform.
2. **Tim Ferriss two-reward framework** — Reward 1 = the act itself (must be a reward to last). Reward 2 = views/money (volatile, don't depend on).
3. **Don't tie this to business metrics for 12 months.**
4. **Engine format =** "I did X for internal reward Y." Every episode = action × why.
5. **Live in the frame** — Subway Takes model. Real frames, not composited thumbnails.

## Verbatim pain quotes

1. "**I don't enjoy the optimized YouTube. It feels like the artist in me wants raw.**"
2. "I'm trying to find that engine format that works where I can post multiple times."
3. "I went to it without the right preparation and mainly to serve my creative ego." (parallels Adrien Purr ep 11 exactly)

## Tool / workflow signal

- Two YouTube channels — split between income side and creative side.
- Strong IG-to-YouTube conversion proven (9.4M IG → 94K YouTube).
- Series mockup he sent C&S of an "adult prom in Paris" video (titles + thumbs already drafted).
- Just out of a 2-year relationship — personal life is the raw material.

## BuildOS angle

James is the **short-form to long-form transition** archetype, with the added complexity that he's running two parallel channels (income + creative). Each long-form he wants to make is essentially a vulnerability project — research into himself, the story he's telling, the location, the friends involved. The Tim Ferriss framework (act-itself reward + outcome reward) is something he'll need to revisit per video. C&S's prescription — "every episode is action × internal why" — is a project template.

**Pitch shape:** "You're running two channels and the long-form one is the artist project. The 'did X for internal reward Y' template Colin & Samir gave you is the right shape — and it lives somewhere. Each episode is a real personal project: the breakup, the prom in Paris, the next vulnerability. BuildOS holds one project per long-form so it doesn't decay between the months it takes you to ship one."
