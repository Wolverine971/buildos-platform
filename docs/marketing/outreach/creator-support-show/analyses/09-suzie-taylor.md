# Suzie Taylor — ex-MrBeast contestant repositioning

## Profile

- **Channel:** [@SuzieTaylor](https://www.youtube.com/@SuzieTaylor)
- **Origin:** Main character in 2 MrBeast challenges (chained-to-ex, locked-in-room-100-days). Combined videos = ~500M views.
- **Background:** PA work, became friends with Airrack, did 5 videos for that network, quit job, got MrBeast call.
- **Wants:** Audience who watches her "for me" — for the story, not the MrBeast association.
- **References:** Mel Robbins, Matt D'Avella, Emma Chamberlain.
- **Inspirations / who she watches:** Yes Theory.

## What she's building

Vlog-style philanthropy + challenge content. Wants to inspire post-grads / working-class people / "seekers" who feel stuck. Coined her own framing: **"joy-baiting"** instead of rage-baiting.

## The on-air problem (verbatim)

> "I want to create a meaningful, lasting audience who watches me for me and wants to feel inspired by the content I make, but feels like they just care that I'm chained to my ex or locked in a room for 100 days."

> "**I'm still trying to grow the channel. Plain and simple.**"

## Colin & Samir's diagnosis

1. Audience audit: young working-class seekers + post-grad figuring it out + MrBeast fans + joy-baiters.
2. Identity-Emotion-Action: motivated / confident / connected / joy.
3. Tell her PA-to-MrBeast origin story — explicit homework.
4. Try unconventional stripped-down thumbnail to filter for the "for me" audience.
5. "Hide the vegetables" — broad packaging, deep human story inside.

## Verbatim pain quotes

1. "I want my story to be able to impact them."
2. "I get scared when it's like I'm still trying to grow the channel."
3. "**As soon as I believed in myself was when things happened.**"

## Tool / workflow signal

- Series planning mock-up sent to C&S (presentation file).
- Cross-platform: YouTube + IG short-form ("Monday motivation" series mentioned).
- Comments feedback loop — quoted a viewer saying "thanks, I finally just got up and went on my run" after watching her Monday post.
- Already brainstorms in title-thumbnail mockups (showed C&S a series mockup).

## BuildOS angle

Suzie's pain is positioning, not workflow per se. But the homework she got — "tell your origin story in a video," "build a series plan that speaks to 4 audience identities" — is multi-week project work that needs to live somewhere. Her **Monday motivation** series is an existing recurring format; the new long-form vision is a separate parallel pipeline.

**Pitch shape:** "You're rebuilding your audience identity on camera. The shape Colin & Samir drew with you — 4 audience buckets × emotion × action — is a framework that lives forever, not just for one video. BuildOS holds the audience map + the origin video + the Monday motivation series in one project, so every new video reads from the position you took on camera last week."

## Cold-email opener seed

> Subject: "watches me for me" — the position you're building
>
> Suzie — your Creator Support line — "audience who watches me for me, not because I was chained to my ex" — that's a positioning project, not a one-video problem. The 4 audience buckets you mapped with Colin & Samir (post-grads, seekers, MrBeast fans, joy-baiters) need to live somewhere your future videos can read from. Want a layout for that?

## Risk / fit notes

- Identity-shift narrative is emotionally loaded — be careful with phrasing.
- Strong network (ex-MrBeast, friends with Airrack) — possible amplification node.
- Mel Robbins / Matt D'Avella / Emma Chamberlain audience overlap — these are useful comp anchors.
