# Kelly Wakasa — adventure-vlog lifestyle creator

## Profile

- **Mantra:** "Do what excites."
- **Tenure:** Long enough to be over the YouTube formula ("I'm a YouTuber for so long that I'm like that's so YouTube that I don't do it").
- **Cadence:** Weekly. Considering reducing to bi-weekly.
- **Performance:** Averages 100K+ views/video over a long stretch.
- **Inspirations on-air:** Ryan Trahan (I tried / I tested / I survived formats), Preston ("I built").
- **Top-performing format historically:** "Surprising my [X]" — surprising friend in NY was an early hit.

## What he's building

Adventure / lifestyle vlogs with no engine format. Bus trip experiment, skatepark Airbnbs, date content, day-in-the-life content. Frustrated by lack of a repeatable "show" identity. C&S diagnosed: too many hobbies, no engine. Locked in **"Surprising [X]"** as the engine to test.

## The on-air problem (verbatim)

> "I know a lot of my issues and it's hard for me to narrow down the content I put out. **I just have too many goddamn hobbies and not a passion for something besides making videos.**"

> "Every single video looks like an experiment to me." — Colin's diagnosis of Kelly's channel from a viewer perspective.

## Colin & Samir's diagnosis

1. **Engine vs experimentation.** 3 of 4 videos = engine format; 1 = experiment.
2. **Repeatable framework.** Lock a titling convention you can own (e.g., "Surprising [X]," "8 hours with [X]").
3. **Brainstorm 100 ideas with 'surprising' as the first word.** Explicit homework.
4. **Daily ideation practice.** Quoted: Scott Belsky — "we're no longer in the how-to-make era, we're in the what-to-make era."
5. **50-in-5 framework** (5 days × 10 ideas / day = 50 channel ideas).

## Verbatim pain quotes

1. **"I just have too many goddamn hobbies."**
2. "I don't feel like I have a straight bread and butter."
3. "Brainstorm. **Never.**" (Kelly's literal answer when asked how often he brainstorms.)
4. "I think I keep going back to is like the biggest creators repeat. They just keep doing similar things and slightly off."

## Tool / workflow signal

- **No brainstorming practice.** Zero ideation cadence — admitted on camera.
- C&S sent him the 50-in-5 framework after the episode.
- Posts weekly but every video feels like an experiment to viewers — no project memory or visual-conventions library.
- Already evaluates his own videos in "engine vs experiment" buckets retrospectively but doesn't decide that ahead of time.

## BuildOS angle

Kelly's stated pain is the absence of an ideation system. He **never brainstorms** (literally his on-air answer), yet has been creating weekly for years. He needs a place where the "Surprising [X]" format lives, where each "X" is a project, where past surprises feed future ones (community connections, locations, gift ideas), and where the daily ideation practice C&S prescribed actually happens. The 50-in-5 framework is a BuildOS project waiting to exist.

**Pitch shape:** "Colin & Samir gave you homework: 100 ideas with 'surprising' as the first word + a daily brainstorm habit. Those don't live well in a Notion that decays. BuildOS holds the engine format as a project, every 'surprising X' as a sub-project, and the daily ideation as a habit that compounds — so by video #20 you have a library, not a memory."
