# Adrien Purr (OMG Adrian) — Instagram-native short-form creator pivoting to long-form

## Profile

- **Native platform:** Instagram. Known for "really cool text style and really engaging shots."
- **YouTube state:** 44K subs after a 30-in-30 long-form sprint that ended in burnout. Hasn't posted on YouTube in 6 months.
- **Self-description:** Wants to "be known as a YouTuber." Likes the intentional-click feedback loop.
- **Audience:** Creators and creative professionals. Education-focused.
- **Existing format wins:** "How I edit" / "How I film" — top-performing on the channel.

## What he's building

Wants to be a desk-show YouTuber teaching short-form / creator process. Vision: a long-form show about short-form content. Considers segments like reacts + tutorials, but C&S pushed back: prove the title-thumbnail concept first, segments come later. Currently planning 2026 push with proper lighting setup ("I bought lights for the first time").

## The on-air problem (verbatim)

> "I filmed 30 days consecutively and it was just as raw as I could keep it as to what those 30 days as a full-time content creator looks like. I tried to make it the longer version of my short-form videos, but I've come to realize like I just didn't enjoy that process. It was so hard that I can't even look at it through the perspective of did I enjoy that? No."

> "It was tough… post-production [was the toughest]."

> **"I went to it without the right preparation and mainly to serve my creative ego. And that lack of clarity yielded the burnout."**

## Colin & Samir's diagnosis

1. Lean into "How I edit" engine format.
2. Brainstorm 10–20 variations of "How I X" before making anything.
3. React format as a second engine — react to cinematic visuals.
4. Don't make a multi-segment show until you've proven the simple format.
5. Constraint creates creativity — give yourself a single format.

## Verbatim pain quotes

1. **"That lack of clarity yielded the burnout."**
2. "I went to it without the right preparation."
3. "Damn, that is genius. Like zoom out, show the five steps. Why didn't I think of that?"

## Tool / workflow signal

- Instagram-native, so heavy reliance on CapCut / IG editing tools + Premiere when going long-form.
- 30-in-30 sprint = lots of unfinished long-form content sitting somewhere.
- Self-aware about ego as a creative factor.
- Hadn't done the work of studying titles / thumbnails in his niche before the sprint — C&S explicitly assigned this as homework.

## BuildOS angle

Adrien is the **transition-from-short-form-to-long-form** archetype. He burned out 30 days into a long-form sprint because he didn't have a system holding the format / hook / thumbnail decisions. C&S sent him home with brainstorm-10-variations-of-"How-I-X" homework + study-the-thumbnail-landscape homework. Both are project-shaped, multi-week artifacts that won't survive in a notes app.

**Pitch shape:** "You burned out 30-in-30 because the system was you. Each long-form was its own from-scratch project. C&S's prescription — 20 variations of 'How I X' + a reactionary engine + thumbnail audit — is the kind of work that should compound across videos, not get retyped. BuildOS is the project layer underneath your IG / YouTube split."
