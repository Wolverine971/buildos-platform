# AmandaRachLee — 12-year YouTube veteran + brand owner

## Profile

- **Channel:** [@amandarachlee](https://www.youtube.com/@amandarachlee)
- **Tenure:** **12 years on YouTube.**
- **Format:** Journaling content. Had a 7-year monthly series, stopped this year due to burnout.
- **Business:** Owns a stationary brand — coloring book planner is the flagship.
- **Top videos:** Some hit 10M views. ("There's some videos I have that have like 10 million views.")
- **Current viewership:** "My 2026 planner" did ~80K views.
- **Comparison creators she names:** Cassey Ho (Blogilates / PopFlex), Ashley (Nami), Martina Calvi.

## What she's building

The classic creator-to-brand-owner crossover. Built the audience through journaling content → audience asked for tools → she launched the planner → now runs both. Her current challenge is storytelling around being a business owner. Her audience expects her to be a creator, but her days are now running a stationary company. "I often feel these days I feel more of like a business owner versus a creator."

## The on-air problem (verbatim)

> "I'm having a little bit of difficulty balancing being a business owner and then also being a creator and kind of where that intersects. It's been a little bit of a hard balance lately."

> "**I often feel these days I feel more of like a business owner versus a creator.**"

> "There's just so many things that I feel like so overwhelmed. It's like one the business aspect, the video aspect, figuring out where to put that into the videos, then doing that again for shorts. And then I also have like with short form, you have like the branded page, like my brand's page, what do I put on there versus what do I put on my own socials?"

## Colin & Samir's diagnosis

1. **Move short-form to react/respond format.** Cassey Ho model: "you guys asked for this, let me show you how I'm addressing that."
2. **Monthly long-form anchored to month-specific value** — "10 things to journal about this September" (Epic Gardening / Kevin Espirito model).
3. **Define yourself before you describe yourself.** Don't define yourself by what you're not ("not a CEO bro").
4. **Identity = artist + business owner.** Both.
5. **Fill out the creator vision worksheet.** 16 questions across financial / lifestyle / content.
6. **Hire an assistant for emails** — explicit homework, she had one who left.
7. **Necessary task you don't want to do = emails.**

## Verbatim pain quotes

1. **"I feel like so overwhelmed."**
2. **"I want to be doing some sort of art every day — which I think I've lost a little bit, especially with the business stuff going on."**
3. "I used to have like an assistant who would handle all those like admin things… and then they left me and then we tried to find someone else… ever since then just because it was so much work to find someone I kind of like stopped the search."
4. "I'm such a old school YouTube person."

## Tool / workflow signal

- **Two short-form surfaces** to feed: her personal page + the brand's Instagram. Doesn't know what goes where.
- **Editor relationship** (post-production stage).
- **Team exists** but spread thin since assistant left ("it's hard for me to like get everyone on the same page" + she's "not a manager" by self-description).
- **Comments are the product feedback channel.** "How my business sort of started" was from comments. ("I'll get comments from people like 'oh I start a journal at the beginning of the year and then I don't finish it.'")
- **Branded vs personal** is fragmented across surfaces.

## BuildOS angle

Amanda is doing the work of **two creators** — a journaling channel creator AND a stationary brand owner — and the two halves don't share context. Her best product insight literally comes from her YouTube comments, but those comments live on YouTube and the planner-design decisions live somewhere else. She's lost her daily art habit because business admin eats her time. C&S explicitly told her: short-form should be "react to comment + show product behind-the-scenes" and long-form should be one-a-month with seasonal hooks. Both require a system that ties the customer feedback loop to the brand decisions to the content output. That's BuildOS.

**Pitch shape:** "You're running a journaling channel and a stationary brand off the same brain. Right now the comments live in YouTube, the product roadmap lives in your head, and 'what to post on which Instagram' is a daily decision. BuildOS holds one project for the brand — every customer comment, every product question, every planner page decision, every video pegged to it — so the channel and the company finally read from the same memory."
