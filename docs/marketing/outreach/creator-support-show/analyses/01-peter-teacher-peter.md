# Peter / "Teacher Peter" — bilingual kids music

## Profile

- **Channel:** [@teacherpetersings](https://www.youtube.com/@teacherpetersings) — YouTube Kids (made-for-kids, no comments, low ad revenue).
- **Tenure:** 6+ years performing live kids music at LA parks + birthday parties.
- **Format:** Bilingual (English/Spanish) songs + videos for kids.
- **Demo:** 2-4-year-old preschool kids (real life classes target 0-6).
- **Side life:** Sings in indie-rock band **Arms Akimbo** (10-year band). Doesn't want this to be 100% of his time — 75% feels right.
- **Income now:** Mostly in-person LA classes. YouTube AdSense paid 2 months' rent ever.

## What he's building

The trust-based kids' music channel. Wants to scale beyond LA via YouTube. Currently planning a "Sticky Notes" series (kids draw something, he writes a song about it) but C&S diagnosed it as short-form material, not long-form. Wants live virtual classes.

## The on-air problem (verbatim)

> "For over 6 years, I've been singing to kids at birthday parties and parks, and I've been trying to figure out how can I get the energy of this experience to the screen? And how can I disseminate what I do for children in Los Angeles and do it for kids all over the world."

## Colin & Samir's diagnosis

1. **Concentric circles model.** Core = educational music videos for kids (long-form YouTube). Middle = live/virtual classes (revenue). Outer = entertainment short-form for Instagram.
2. **Thumbnails must show outcome** — Miss Rachel / Yoga With Adriene model.
3. **Tom Sachs quote: "creativity is the enemy. Simplicity is the answer."**
4. **Virtual live music classes** — clearest revenue + community play.

## Verbatim pain quotes

1. "I'm 1,000% squarely in the definition of what I just said — when you're capable of anything, you will make everything."
2. "The truth is anything that is like about engagement or likes or shares just doesn't apply to me."
3. "I haven't learned to edit for this purpose and it's not even one of my strengths. It's just like who else is going to do it?"

## Tool / workflow signal

- YouTube Kids = no comments, no engagement, low ads = forced to make money via classes / merch / virtual events.
- Editing himself ("not one of my strengths").
- Live class business + YouTube channel + Instagram entertainment = three pipelines for one creator.
- Already thinking in series (Sticky Notes) but doesn't have a system to plan them across long-form vs short-form.

## BuildOS angle

Peter has three parallel businesses (live LA classes + YouTube Kids + IG / TikTok entertainment) and is editing his own videos despite not enjoying it. The Concentric Circles structure C&S drew is literally a project hierarchy. Each "Sticky Notes" episode is a project, each Spanish-language series is a sub-project, each virtual class is an event-project. He's also a band member with his own side creative life. BuildOS could hold the kids music life and the Arms Akimbo life in parallel without them blurring.

**Pitch shape:** "Three pipelines (live classes, YouTube Kids long-form, IG entertainment short-form) running off one creator who admits 'when you're capable of anything, you will make everything.' BuildOS holds each pipeline as a project so the entertainment shorts don't bleed into the educational long-form. The Sticky Notes series, the virtual class roster, the bilingual song library — each is a project that compounds."

## Cold-email opener seed

> Subject: "creativity is the enemy" — for what it's worth
>
> Peter — your Creator Support session and the Tom Sachs callout at the end stuck with me. You're running a live-class business, a YouTube Kids channel, and an IG entertainment side at once — three pipelines, one creator who admits he'll "make everything." Building [BuildOS] for that exact problem. Want a project layout that holds the three lanes separately?

## Risk / fit notes

- Kids music is a specialty niche; BuildOS doesn't change anything about YouTube Kids monetization.
- Indie-rock band side is part of his identity — don't pitch as "make this your full focus."
- Likely budget-sensitive — pricing matters.
