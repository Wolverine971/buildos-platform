# Bryce — The Throttle Bros

## Profile

- **Channel:** [@The_Throttle_Bros](https://www.youtube.com/@The_Throttle_Bros)
- **Subs:** ~7,000 (as of episode).
- **Personal stakes:** Sold house, sold land/investment property, downgraded truck and SUV, moved back in with parents in Idaho with wife and **3 kids (twins + 1)**. 27 years old. Used proceeds to fund 3 years of runway.
- **Why:** Inspired by Forrest Frank's similar all-in story. Quit 9-5 office job he hated.
- **Cadence:** Weekly long-form. Considering reducing.
- **Inspirations:** See Boys, Surronster, How Ridiculous, Logan Taylor.

## What he's building

Extreme-sports / challenge content with his brothers + friends. Crown jewel: two-person tandem pit bike built to jump with the boys. Past videos include kayak down a mountain, backflipping a jet ski. Currently transitioning focus from "anything with a throttle" to e-bikes specifically. Goal: 100K subs by end of year (currently 7K).

## The on-air problem (verbatim)

> "I've worked really hard on the titles, the thumbnails, the hooks — all the stuff. And I still can't get people to watch the videos. So, a lot of them are kind of stuck in that 1,000 view jail."

> "**I sold everything to go all in on YouTube. Please bring me on Creator Support.**" (his application subject line, quoted on-camera)

## Colin & Samir's diagnosis

1. **Treat YouTube as a business.** Audience + value + competitive advantage. Stop treating each video as a one-off.
2. **Audience audit.** Outdoorsy / underdog / outsider, age 14–28, into extreme sports + e-bikes.
3. **Unique to me** — your personal story (sold-everything, 3 kids, all-in) is the unique angle no other creator has.
4. **Pursuit of happiness > "100K subs"** as the public-facing why. Self-serving wins don't sell.
5. **Title the origin story** — "I actually sold my house to go all in on YouTube. Here's how it's going."
6. **Quality over weekly cadence.** 12 great videos > 35 sub-par. Don't put product on shelf before it's ready.
7. **Make at least one video over next 3 months telling the personal story.**

## Verbatim pain quotes

1. "Stuck in that 1,000 view jail."
2. "I sold everything to go all in on YouTube."
3. "It's been hard to package some of those competition videos because there's multiple rounds, we're doing different things, and so I usually just think of what's the most visually compelling thing."
4. "I just need to get a a smooth workflow established."

## Tool / workflow signal

- Already does **thumbnail mock-ups** + writes hook before filming on his best-performing videos.
- Inconsistent with that process — defaults back to making first, packaging second when under pressure.
- Has a list of competitor channels he studies (See Boys, Surronster, Logan Taylor) — he's already running an informal "niche analysis."
- C&S explicitly assigned him three pieces of homework: (a) **niche analysis worksheet**, (b) test the "I built X" framework, (c) plan the origin-story video. **All three are project-shaped, multi-week artifacts.**

## BuildOS angle

Bryce is the textbook **founder-creator with everything on the line** persona. Sold his life to fund this. Has 3 years of runway and 3 kids. His pain is not "I need more views" — it's "I need to treat this as a business and I don't have the operational habits yet." C&S explicitly told him: stop reinventing each video, build engine formats, do a niche analysis once, plan the origin video deliberately. That's BuildOS work.

**Pitch shape:** "You sold a house to do this. The next 3 years are about treating the channel like a business — engine format, niche audit, origin-story video, sponsor pipeline. BuildOS holds those projects so you don't lose them in Notion or your head. You came home to your parents' house to give this a real shot — don't bottleneck it on memory."

## Risk / fit notes

- **High emotional stake.** Sold his house. Treat the outreach with respect for that.
- **7K subs** is small — but his runway, family, and pursuit of "go all in" mindset make him a high-fit early user, not a low-fit by-size.
- Wife is in the room ("Yes, she's here. She's in the background.") — she's a stakeholder.
- C&S said "this is your homework" several times. The homework is the wedge. Hand him the system that does it.
- Idaho-based. Eastern-time outreach OK.
