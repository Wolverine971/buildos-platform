# Mitch Boyer — Cycling YouTuber

## Profile

- **Channel:** [@MitchBoyer](https://www.youtube.com/@MitchBoyer)
- **Niche:** Cycling content; flagship series rides the steepest streets in the world. Falls off bike often.
- **Tenure:** 5 years all-in on cycling content as a "full-time job" (training, nutrition, coach).
- **Cadence:** ~1 long-form per quarter (Steep Streets), plus short-form (some hit 3M views).
- **Team shape:** Solo creator. Has a rep named Connor who takes 15% of brand deals. Wife Val watching.
- **Manager:** Connor (handles negotiation + payment, mostly inbound).
- **Sponsor mix:** Yearly cycling sponsors (bike + clothing) + integrated 60-90s reads. Cycling-adjacent brands pay better than endemic.

## What he's building

A scarcity-driven, high-production solo cycling series. Each Steep Streets video requires international travel, months of training, on-the-ground production. Quality is high; visual storytelling is praised on-air by C&S as "really respecting our eyes." Currently exploring expansion to e-bikes-adjacent audiences and finding sponsors who care about reach not just niche.

## The on-air problem (verbatim from transcript)

> "These videos take a lot of time to train for. They cost a lot of money to go like travel and do. And so I don't produce a ton of them as a solo creator. So I guess like part of my problem is figuring out how do I expand my output uh with these like high-production as a solo creator projects."

> **"I've always been good at creating things, not so good at the operational side… or the business side of my young career."**

This is one of the cleanest BuildOS pain quotes captured from the entire Creator Support archive.

## Colin & Samir's diagnosis

1. **Positioning, not output.** "I don't love the endemic brands. The biking brands aren't going to have enough money."
2. **Inventory + rate card.** Map quarter into 1 long-form + 12 shorts + 1 sponsored ride. Set $50K/quarter floor.
3. **Sell in portfolios.** Three shorts for $5K, not one for $2K. Reduces volatility.
4. **Mission statement.** Sponsors buy "mission alignment." Currently no public-facing mission to align to.
5. **Audience identity exercise.** Identities = midlife-crisis purpose seekers, cycling-curious. Emotion = understood. Action = community connection.
6. **Community product** (Circle group, ride-along events, affiliate gear list).

## Verbatim pain quotes (gold for outreach copy)

1. **"I've always been good at creating things, not so good at the operational side."**
2. "I'm a bottleneck. I'm the one limiting the amount of videos I can actually make."
3. "I think there's only one product that I should look into. Okay. Which is a community product."

## Tool / workflow signal

The transcript doesn't expose specific tools, but reveals the shape:

- Manages sponsors through Connor (no CRM mentioned).
- Editor relationship (post-production handoff exists).
- Annual sponsors + per-video integrations (two parallel pipelines per quarter).
- "Brand deck" homework — he doesn't have one yet.
- Inventory is fragmented across surfaces: long-form on YouTube, shorts on YT + IG, ride is offline event.

## BuildOS angle

Mitch's literal phrasing — _"good at creating things, not so good at the operational side"_ — is BuildOS's value prop verbatim. He's running two parallel pipelines (Steep Streets quarterly + weekly shorts + a planned community/ride product) off one person's brain. C&S sent him homework: build a deck, build a niche analysis, build an inventory map, build a mission statement, plan his next ride for sponsors. **That's a BuildOS project.**

**Pitch shape:** "BuildOS holds the operational side so the creating side doesn't shrink. One project for Steep Streets (research per location, sponsor placements, B-roll list, editor handoff). One project for the community/ride product. The deck and inventory map live where the videos do, not in a separate Notion that decays."

## Risk / fit notes

- **Strong fit.** Workflow is project-shaped (Steep Streets = one project across months of research + travel + sponsor coordination). Mitch already accepts he's the bottleneck and wants out of being the bottleneck without growing a team.
- Wife Val mentioned on-air — she's in the room. Could be a household decision; treat as the second buyer.
- Connor (rep) is a stakeholder for the rate card / brand deal side. Don't pitch as "replace Connor."
- He's mid-career, not new — already has habits. Pitch the underneath layer, not a new system.
