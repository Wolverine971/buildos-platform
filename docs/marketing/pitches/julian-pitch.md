You’re **very close**, but right now the pitch is framed around **you** (your skills, AI, tech). For someone like Julian Dorey—who already has **1.17M subscribers and 500M+ views**—the pitch needs to frame around **three things he cares about**:

1. **Credibility / legitimacy**
2. **Influence / citations**
3. **Long-term media power**

Your idea is actually **very strong** because YouTube research podcasts are **undervalued academically and journalistically**. Turning episodes into **citation-ready research hubs** is genuinely compelling.

The key is to **reframe the pitch as strategic leverage**, not web development.

---

# The Core Reframe

Instead of:

> “I build websites and use AI to gather data”

Position it as:

**“You already do the research. I turn it into a permanent intelligence archive.”**

Or even stronger:

**“Right now your research disappears into YouTube. I turn it into something the internet can cite.”**

---

# What Julian Actually Wants (Whether He Knows It Or Not)

For creators like him, the biggest problems are:

**1. Discoverability decay**

YouTube videos die after a few weeks.

**2. Lack of citation**

Researchers, journalists, and bloggers **cannot cite YouTube easily**.

**3. Information fragmentation**

All the sources and research that went into an episode **disappear.**

**4. Credibility ceiling**

YouTube personalities are often treated as entertainers instead of researchers.

Your website idea solves **all four.**

---

# Refined Pitch (DM Version)

This version works for **Instagram DM**.

---

**Message**

Hey Julian — I’ve been watching your podcast for a while and I love the depth you go into on some of these topics.

One thing I’ve noticed though:

You clearly do a _ton_ of research for your episodes, but once the episode is published, most of that work basically disappears into YouTube.

YouTube videos are surprisingly hard to **cite, reference, or build on**, which means a lot of the research you’re doing doesn’t compound the way it should.

I’m a tech guy and I’ve been working on something interesting around this.

Instead of just show notes, I build **research pages for podcast episodes** that organize:

• sources
• timelines
• evidence
• claims
• supporting data

Basically turning episodes into **citation-ready research hubs.**

I actually built a quick example for one of your recent episodes so you can see what I mean:

[https://build-os.com/p/julian-doc](https://build-os.com/p/julian-doc)

The idea is simple:

Your videos stay the main content, but the site becomes a **credibility engine** where people can dig into the actual research.

This does a few interesting things:

• journalists can cite your work
• other researchers can reference it
• episodes stay discoverable for years via search
• your content becomes a **source of record**, not just a podcast

Not trying to sell you anything here — I just thought this could genuinely amplify what you’re already doing.

If it looks interesting I'd love to explore building this out with you.
