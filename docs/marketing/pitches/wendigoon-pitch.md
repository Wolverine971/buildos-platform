# Wendigoon (Isaiah Nichols)

## Profile

- **Channel:** Wendigoon
- **Subscribers:** ~4.5M+
- **Content:** Deep dives into conspiracy theories, mysteries, horror, iceberg charts, historical events, religious texts
- **Style:** Long-form (often 1hr+), conversational, academic depth delivered in an approachable way. Former teacher — explains complex topics clearly.
- **Contact:** Management through Booking Agent Info; Instagram @daily.wendigoon
- **Notable:** Known for his "Conspiracy Theory Iceberg" series which systematically works through layers of increasingly obscure topics

## Why He's a Fit

Wendigoon's videos are essentially _lectures_ — dense, well-structured deep dives that cover massive amounts of material. His iceberg series alone references hundreds of distinct topics, theories, and historical events. But all of that information is locked inside hour-long videos with no way to search, reference, or build on it. For a creator whose whole brand is _going deep_, his content has no depth layer outside YouTube.

## Suggested Subject Line

**Your iceberg videos deserve a research layer**

## Pitch (Email)

Hey Isaiah — I've been watching your deep dives for a while and what stands out is how much ground you cover. Your iceberg series alone touches on hundreds of topics, events, and theories — with real context behind each one.

Here's something that's been bugging me though:

All of that work is locked inside hour-long videos. If someone watches your conspiracy iceberg and wants to dig deeper into one specific topic, they have to scrub through a video trying to find the right timestamp. There's no way to search it, reference it, or build on it.

For someone who goes as deep as you do, your content deserves a deeper layer.

I've been building something around this — **research companion pages** for long-form episodes that organize:

- topic breakdowns with timestamps
- source links and references
- key claims and evidence
- entity connections (people, organizations, events)
- related reading for each subtopic

Think of it as an **interactive index** for your deep dives — turning each video into something people can actually explore and cite.

I built a quick example for one of your episodes so you can see what I mean:

[LINK TO DEMO PAGE]

For your iceberg videos specifically, imagine each tier linking out to its own research page — giving your audience a way to _keep going_ after the video ends.

This does a few things:

- your deep dives become searchable and referenceable
- your audience gets a way to explore individual topics further
- other creators and researchers can cite your work properly
- episodes stay discoverable via search engines for years

You've basically built an encyclopedia of fringe knowledge — this just gives it a proper home outside YouTube.

Not selling anything here. I built this because your content is genuinely the kind that should compound over time instead of disappearing into the algorithm.

If it looks interesting, I'd love to talk about building this out.
