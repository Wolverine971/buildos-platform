# Johnny Harris

## Profile

- **Channel:** Johnny Harris
- **Subscribers:** ~7.5M
- **Content:** Investigative journalism, geopolitics, social issues, maps/data visualization, explainers
- **Style:** High production value, strong visual storytelling, data-driven. Former Vox Borders creator. Blends personal narrative with investigative reporting.
- **Contact:** Through management/business inquiries (likely on website or YouTube about page)
- **Website:** johnnyharris.co
- **Notable:** Built one of the most successful independent journalism channels after leaving Vox. Known for using maps, data viz, and FOIA documents in his reporting.

## Why He's a Fit

Johnny Harris is doing _real journalism_ on YouTube — FOIA requests, data analysis, on-the-ground reporting. He's already positioned as a serious journalist, not just a creator. But his investigative work still lives inside YouTube videos. When he publishes a deep investigation into, say, US military bases or border policy, all the documents, data, and sources he gathered are invisible to the viewer. He's producing journalism-grade work without journalism-grade infrastructure (searchable archives, source pages, citation-ready references). This is the gap.

## Suggested Subject Line

**Your journalism deserves more than a YouTube upload**

## Pitch (Email)

Hey Johnny — what you've built since leaving Vox is genuinely impressive. You're doing the kind of investigative work that used to require a newsroom — FOIA requests, original data analysis, on-the-ground reporting — and you're doing it independently.

But something's been nagging me:

When you publish an investigation, all the documents, data, and sources behind it disappear into a YouTube video. There's no source page. No document archive. No way for another journalist, researcher, or policymaker to reference your work the way they'd reference a ProPublica investigation.

You're producing journalism-grade content without journalism-grade infrastructure.

I've been building something that bridges that gap — **investigation companion pages** that sit alongside video journalism and expose:

- source documents (FOIA responses, public records)
- data and methodology
- key claims with supporting evidence
- timelines and event sequences
- maps and geographic context
- citations in standard formats

Essentially turning each video investigation into a **citable, searchable, permanent record**.

- your reporting stays discoverable and relevant for years
- it positions you not just as a creator, but as an **independent news organization**

You've already proven you can do the journalism. This just gives it the infrastructure it deserves.

Would love to discuss if this resonates.

## Notes

- Johnny is bigger and more established — he may already have infrastructure or a team thinking about this
- Frame as "journalism infrastructure" not "research pages" — he sees himself as a journalist, not a researcher
- He uses a lot of maps and data viz — the demo page should incorporate visual/geographic elements if possible
- He left Vox to be independent — lean into the "independent news org" framing
- His audience includes journalists, policy people, educators — the citation angle is very real here
- Higher chance of needing to go through management — professional tone throughout
