# LEMMiNO (David Wangstedt)

## Profile

- **Channel:** LEMMiNO
- **Subscribers:** ~5.88M
- **Content:** High-production documentary-style videos on mysteries, space, unsolved cases, and historical events
- **Style:** Cinematic, meticulously researched, slow and deliberate. Uploads rarely (a few times per year) because each video takes months of research and production.
- **Contact:** Through social media / official website. Has stated he tries to reply to inquiries.
- **Website:** lemmi.no
- **Notable:** Transitioned from Top 10 lists to full documentaries. Each video is essentially a short film with original music, custom graphics, and deep primary source research.

## Why He's a Fit

LEMMiNO is arguably the most research-intensive creator on YouTube. Each video takes months and involves deep primary source work — FOIA requests, academic papers, original analysis. But because he uploads so infrequently, each video has an outsized importance to his channel. The problem: all that months-long research effort lives inside a single 30-minute video. No companion material, no source archives, no way for viewers to verify or extend his work. For someone who puts this much effort into research, the lack of a persistent research layer is a massive missed opportunity.

## Suggested Subject Line

**Months of research shouldn't live in a single video**

## Pitch (Email)

Hey David — I've been following your work for years and I think you make some of the best content on YouTube. Full stop.

What stands out isn't just the production quality — it's the research. I know each video takes months. FOIA requests, academic papers, primary source analysis — you're doing work that most actual documentarians don't bother with.

But here's what I keep thinking about:

All of that research collapses into a single 30-minute video. Once it's published, the sources, the evidence chains, the documents you pulled — all of that is invisible. Viewers see the final product but have no way to access the research underneath it.

For someone who puts this much work into primary sources, that feels like a huge missed opportunity.

I've been building something that addresses this — **research companion pages** that sit alongside documentary-style videos and expose:

- primary source documents
- evidence timelines
- source citations and references
- key claims with supporting data
- maps, entity connections, and visual breakdowns

Essentially turning each video into a **documentary + research archive**.

- LEMMiNO uploads very rarely — pitch the longevity angle hard. Each video should have a long tail.
- He's Swedish, based in Sweden — keep language clean and direct, no American slang
