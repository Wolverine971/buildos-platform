# WS10 — Short-Form Video (TikTok, counter-positioned)

> [← Index](../README.md) · [Conventions](../CONVENTIONS.md) · [WS09 Anti-Feed Cluster](./WS09-anti-feed-cluster.md) · [Topic Map](../../strategy/anti-feed-content-topic-map.md) · [Brand Guide](../../brand/brand-guide-1-pager.md) · [Anti-feed skill](../../../../.claude/skills/anti-feed/SKILL.md)

## One-line goal

Build a founder-led TikTok presence that **demonstrates** the anti-feed thesis from WS09 — two scripts per cluster blog, receipts-first, no clip-farm tactics — so the platform that manufactures virality becomes the place where BuildOS shows the alternative.

## Why this is a work stream

WS09 treats TikTok extraction as a line item in each publish kit. But short-form video has enough of its own operational surface area — counter-positioning rules, script formats, posting cadence, anti-clip-farm guardrails, measurement rubric — that it warrants its own file. WS10 is the execution home; WS09 remains the content source.

**The philosophical bet:** the anti-feed thesis gets stronger, not weaker, when BuildOS is present on the platform it's critiquing. The critique is platform-agnostic — "interest media" isn't TikTok-specific, it's a structural claim about how algorithmic distribution works. Showing up on TikTok to demonstrate the alternative is on-brand; ghosting the platform would be read as fear of the argument.

The counter-positioning is the whole play. If we do TikTok the way clip-farmed streamers do TikTok, we fail the thesis. If we do it founder-led, receipts-first, and vocabulary-disciplined, we become the proof.

**Out of scope:**

- Paid promotion, boosts, or Spark Ads
- Working with clippers, clipping agencies, or Discord campaign servers
- Trend-jacking with no vocabulary connection to the cluster
- Buying equipment beyond what a phone + clip-on mic already provides
- Instagram carousel content (that's WS09 publish kit extraction, not WS10)

## Counter-positioning: what makes this work stream different

Every TikTok produced under WS10 must satisfy the rejection rubric in [T50](#t50--counter-positioning-rubric). The short version:

1. **Founder on camera or founder screen recording.** No AI-voiceover faceless loops.
2. **Receipts in every script.** Every claim maps to a specific blog, stat, or named source.
3. **Vocabulary discipline.** One term from the topic map "terms to own" per script, repeated ≥2×.
4. **No clip-farm tactics.** No buying views, no payola, no bought boosts, no trend audio.
5. **Chosen-input CTA.** "Read the piece," "try a brain dump," "send this to someone." Never "follow for more."
6. **Same pacing as a calm blog.** No fast cuts. The aesthetic is "thinking environment," not "engagement farm."

A script that fails any of these doesn't ship. The point is not to win TikTok on its own terms — it's to demonstrate that the anti-feed alternative works on a platform that punishes it.

### T49 — Posting cadence

See [RECURRING §TikTok cadence](../RECURRING.md#tiktok-cadence-t49).

**Cadence rule:** Within 7 days of a cluster blog going live, both of that blog's TikToks must be posted. If the 7-day window is missed by >3 days twice in a row, re-plan — the compounding effect degrades.

**Done when:** T44 completes (all 10 cluster blogs published) AND every blog has 2 recorded + posted scripts. Once that's done, T49 becomes "standalone cadence" — 1 new script per week from the topic map vocabulary, as bandwidth allows.

### T50 — Counter-positioning rubric

A single doc that every TikTok script must pass before recording. Codifies the six rules in this file's [Counter-positioning](#counter-positioning-what-makes-this-work-stream-different) section, plus edge cases.

**Output:** `docs/marketing/social-media/tiktok/counter-positioning-rubric.md`

**Contents:**

1. The 6 rejection rules (expanded with examples of what passes and fails each)
2. Trend-audio policy: never, with reasoning
3. Posting-cadence policy: no bulk-scheduling, no buffering 20 videos for daily posts — that's clip-farm-shaped behavior
4. Comments policy: respond to any comment that engages the thesis; ignore or block hostile troll patterns; never argue with bots
5. Escalation: if a TikTok goes viral (>100K views), the follow-up should **expand the vocabulary**, not "capitalize on momentum"

**Done when:** rubric doc committed and referenced from the anti-feed skill's `tiktok-scripts.md` reference file.

### T51 — Monthly qualitative review

Vanity metrics are NOT the KPI for this work stream. The question each month is:

> Is TikTok compounding the cluster's vocabulary and credibility, or am I just making content?

**Monthly review checklist** (~30 min, first Monday of the month):

1. List every TikTok posted this month and its blog link + term owned
2. For each: did at least one comment engage the vocabulary? (e.g. someone said "interest media" back to you)
3. Did any TikTok generate a measurable link click / newsletter signup / blog read?
4. Did any TikTok get picked up by a larger creator or journalist with a term-to-own quote?
5. What's the ratio of vocabulary-engaged comments to generic "lol" / emoji comments? (target: >30%)

**Output:** `docs/marketing/measurement/tiktok-review-YYYY-MM.md`

**Kill criteria:** if after 3 months (Q3 2026), vocabulary-engaged comments are still <10% of total comments and zero picked-up quotes, reassess whether TikTok is the right surface for this cluster. **Don't kill early for follower-count reasons.** Counter-positioning is a slow loop.
