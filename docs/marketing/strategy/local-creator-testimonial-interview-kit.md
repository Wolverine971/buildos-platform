# Local Creator Testimonial — Interview Kit

**Companion to:** [`local-creator-testimonial-hunt-2026-05-11.md`](./local-creator-testimonial-hunt-2026-05-11.md)
**Purpose:** Reusable templates for running the 30-min interview, capturing consent, and converting to testimonial.

This kit is the operational layer. The hunt doc is the target list. Both should evolve as we learn.

---

## Step 0 — They said yes, now what?

The flow from "yes" to "testimonial" runs:

1. Send consent + pre-call email (same day)
2. Run the 30-min interview
3. Send post-call email + BuildOS access within 4h
4. Write the workflow piece, send draft to them in week 1
5. 90-day check-in call
6. Capture testimonial during/after that call
7. Publish piece + testimonial

Don't skip steps. Don't publish before step 6.

---

## Recording / Release Consent (send via email after they say yes, before the call)

Plain language, no legalese — anything formal will spook creators.

```
Hey [name] —

For the case study, I'd like to:

1. Record audio of our 30-min call (for quote pulls — the audio stays private with me, not published).
2. Use direct quotes and your name + project in the written piece I produce.
3. Use a 1–2 sentence testimonial from you after the 90-day check-in, only if you're up for it.

In return: you get the finished piece to use however you want — your own blog, newsletter, IG, whatever. You're not signing rights away — you keep full rights to the piece I write about your workflow.

If at any point you want a quote pulled, a section changed, or the whole thing killed, just say so. No questions asked.

Reply "good with this" if you're in.

DJ
```

## Pre-Call Email (24h before)

Subject: `tomorrow at [time] — quick prep`

```
Hey [name] —

Confirming our call tomorrow at [time] [timezone].

One thing to do beforehand: have one specific project in mind — something you're actively shipping. Doesn't have to be polished, doesn't have to be your most important project. Just something real.

Here's what we'll do:
1. Talk through how you're currently organizing things (5 min)
2. Dig into that one project (10 min)
3. Live brain dump into BuildOS — I'll observe, you talk (10 min)
4. Talk through what came out (5 min)

I'll record audio so I can pull quotes for the piece — already covered in the consent email you signed off on.

Call link: [link]

DJ
```

## 30-Minute Interview Script

### Phase 1 — Current Setup (5 min)

Goal: warm them up. Get a real picture of what they're using and where it falls apart.

- "Walk me through what's currently in your stack — Notion, Apple Notes, paper, all of the above?"
- "Where does most of your actual _thinking_ happen — vs. just task-tracking?"
- "What was the last tool you tried that didn't stick? What made you abandon it?"
- "Where do things slip through — emails, ideas, half-finished pieces?"

**Listen for:** tool churn ("again", "every time"), frustration words, shame language ("I keep meaning to..."), the moment they laugh at their own chaos. That laugh is the line.

### Phase 2 — Real Project (10 min)

Goal: identify the specific pain attached to real work. This is the foundation of the case study.

- "What's the biggest thing you're trying to ship this quarter?"
- "What's hard about it specifically?"
- "Walk me through what happens in your head when you sit down Monday morning to work on it."
- "Where is it living right now — Notion doc? Apple Notes? Brain? Several places?"
- "What's the _last_ thing you dropped on this one?"

### Phase 3 — Live Brain Dump (10 min)

Goal: capture the first-time-in-BuildOS moment. **This is the testimonial asset.** This phase is more important than anything else on the call.

- "Cool, let's try this — open BuildOS, brain dump everything you're holding about that project. Don't structure it. Don't make it pretty. Just let it pour for 5 min."
- (Observe. Take notes. Don't interrupt unless they get stuck.)
- After they stop: "Now look at what came out. Anything surprise you?"
- "Is there something in there that's been bugging you that you didn't realize you were carrying?"

**Listen for:** the line they say involuntarily when they see the output. Write it verbatim. That's the case-study headline.

### Phase 4 — Reaction + Booking (5 min)

Goal: capture quote-worthy reactions + book the 90-day check-in before they hang up.

- "First reaction — was that easier or harder than expected?"
- "If you used this for the next 90 days, what would you want to be true at the end?"
- "Can we put a 30-min check-in on the calendar for [90 days out]?"

**Send their 90-day BuildOS access in real time during this phase** — while they're still warm. Don't wait until after the call.

## Post-Call Email (within 4h)

Subject: `thanks + your BuildOS access`

```
Hey [name] —

Thanks for the call. A few things:

1. 90 days of BuildOS: [access code or link]. Onboarding takes ~10 min. If you hit anything weird, text me at [phone].

2. What I'm writing up: a piece on your workflow — specifically [the angle from the call]. I'll send the draft in ~1 week for your edits.

3. 90-day check-in: [date / time / link] on the calendar. We'll talk about what stuck, what didn't, what changed.

If anything comes up in week 1, ping me directly. I want to hear when stuff breaks more than when it works.

DJ
```

## Week 1 Draft Send

Subject: `[name] — workflow piece draft for your eyes`

```
Hey [name] —

Draft of the piece is attached / below.

Changes I want from you:
- Anything that misrepresents how you actually work
- Anything that sounds more polished than your real voice
- Any project / detail you'd rather not name publicly
- Anything you'd want pulled

I'd rather you over-edit than under-edit. Take a week if you need.

DJ
```

## 90-Day Check-In Email

Subject: `90 days in — check-in [date]?`

```
Hey [name] —

90-day mark is hitting next week. Same 30-min format as last time?

Two things I'd want to talk through:
1. What stuck vs. what you stopped using
2. One project you ran through BuildOS that you wouldn't have shipped (or shipped worse) without it — if there is one

If a testimonial feels honest at the end of the call, I'd ask for one. If it doesn't, no pressure — the piece you already have is yours regardless.

[Calendar link]

DJ
```

## The Testimonial Ask (during 90-day call)

Phrase to use at the end of the 90-day call, only if the conversation has been positive:

> "If you'd be up for it, a 1–2 sentence quote I could use on the BuildOS site would mean a lot. Could be about what changed, what surprised you, who you'd recommend it to. Totally cool if you'd rather not — the case study stands either way."

Then **shut up**. Let them think. Don't fill silence.

If they offer a quote in real time, write it down verbatim and confirm: _"Got '[quote verbatim]' — okay if I use that?"_

If they say they'll send one later, schedule a 24h follow-up reminder. Real-time asks are 5× more likely to land than written.

## Asset Inventory (after each successful case study)

For each completed case study, capture:

- [ ] Audio recording (private, my files)
- [ ] Written workflow piece (sent to them + posted on BuildOS site)
- [ ] 1–2 sentence testimonial (with their permission, verbatim)
- [ ] 1 quote-worthy line for IG carousels
- [ ] 1 specific project they shipped with BuildOS (with permission to name it)
- [ ] Photo / screenshot if they're up for it
- [ ] Their post-publish reaction (if any — useful as secondary social proof)

## Anti-Patterns

Things that have tanked similar interview programs in the wild:

- **Don't pitch the product during the interview.** They already said yes. Talking up features now makes them defensive. Observe, don't sell.
- **Don't write the piece before the 90-day check-in is on the calendar.** Write the draft, but don't publish. The 90-day moment is where the real testimonial lands.
- **Don't ask for a testimonial in writing.** Always ask on a call, with silence after. Written asks get blown off; in-person asks land.
- **Don't share the piece on social before they've seen the draft.** Even if it's flattering. Always: send draft → wait for sign-off → publish. One published piece they didn't approve burns 10 future relationships.
- **Don't make them log in to give consent.** No DocuSign, no contracts. Reply-to-email is enough.
- **Don't promise reach.** "We'll feature you" is a debt you may not be able to pay. Promise the artifact (the piece), not the audience.

---

## When to Kill a Case Study

If during or after the interview you realize:

- They're not actually using BuildOS in week 2–4 (no logins)
- The "workflow problem" they described doesn't match what BuildOS solves
- They're going to be unhappy with the piece for reasons you can't fix

…kill it. Send a polite "the piece isn't coming together the way I hoped — keep the 90-day access on me, no obligation." Forced case studies make worse testimonials than no testimonial.
