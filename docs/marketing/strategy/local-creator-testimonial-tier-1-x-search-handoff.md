# Handoff — Tier 1 X Search Pass (BuildOS Testimonial Hunt)

**For:** A human VA or AI agent with browser access (logged into X / Twitter in Chrome).
**From:** DJ Wayne, founder of BuildOS.
**Time budget:** 30–45 min.
**Output:** A filled paste-back block (see [§9](#9-paste-back-format)) returned to DJ.

This document is self-contained. You do not need to read any other file to execute this pass. Companion docs are listed at the end for context only.

---

## 1. Mission (one sentence)

Find 5–15 verified creators in the Baltimore / DC / NYC corridor who have publicly posted about content/workflow pain (Notion, Obsidian, "second brain," "too many projects," overwhelm) on X within the **last 30 days**, so DJ can reach out with a relevant, recent reference instead of a stale one.

## 2. Context (what you need to know)

**BuildOS** is a "thinking environment for people running too many projects at once." Users do stream-of-consciousness brain dumps and the system extracts projects, tasks, and context. **Crucially, BuildOS is _anti-AI_ in marketing posture** — DJ does not lead with AI features. The pitch is _"turn messy thinking into structured work"_, not _"AI agents for your knowledge work."_ This matters for evaluating candidates: someone who's posted AI-skeptical content is **still a fit** as long as the workflow-pain angle is there.

DJ is building **testimonials / case studies** with local creators. The plan: trade a 30-min workflow interview + 90 days of free BuildOS + a written workflow piece (which the creator can use as their own content) → for a testimonial after a 90-day check-in.

**Presuasion principle:** filter the creator universe to "people who have already named the pain publicly." That single filter removes ~90% of the market and gives DJ a natural cold-open hook. Your job is to find those people.

## 3. What we're looking for (Tier 1 signal definition)

A candidate counts as **Tier 1** only if **all four** are true:

1. **Their post is in the last 30 days** (since 2026-04-14).
2. **Their post names workflow / content / tool pain** — not just venting about life. See [§4 evaluation rubric](#4-evaluation-rubric-signal-vs-noise) for what counts.
3. **They are a creator** — author, YouTuber, course creator, podcaster, newsletter writer, or someone visibly _making_ things. Not a marketer of other people's stuff, not a CEO, not a tech blogger covering tools.
4. **They're in the corridor** — X profile location or bio mentions a city in the Wave 1–3 list above.

**Audience size sweet spot:** 5K–50K followers on X (or, if X is small but their main platform — podcast / YouTube / Substack — is in that range, that counts).

- **<5K X followers:** OK if their podcast / Substack is verifiably larger (check the link in their bio).
- **>50K X followers:** Skip. They won't respond, and they're too big for our case-study-trade frame.

## 4. Evaluation rubric — signal vs noise

### Posts that COUNT (real pain signal)

- _"I've redone my Notion 4 times this year and it still doesn't work for me"_
- _"Just lost an idea I had Sunday because it lived in three different apps"_
- _"Second brain failure mode: I have 700 unread notes and still can't find anything"_
- _"This week's overwhelm: book draft + podcast prod + course launch all stacked"_
- _"Switching from Obsidian to \_\_\_ again. This is the fifth time."_
- _"How do you all handle the 14 half-finished essays problem"_
- _"AI tools make me feel like I'm losing my own voice when I draft" (still a fit — DJ's anti-AI angle is built for this)_

### Posts that DO NOT count (noise)

- Productivity tweet-threads ("12 lessons I learned about systems...") — too curated, not pain
- Promotional posts about their own course/book/podcast — not pain
- Generic "I'm so busy" without a workflow specific
- Anything from accounts with the bio "productivity guru" / "AI influencer" / "build-in-public coach" — these are the people _selling_ the cure, not feeling the pain
- Engagement-bait questions ("What's your favorite Notion template?")
- Threads with >10 replies from the OP — usually a content piece, not a real moment
- Anything posted by an account <500 followers — too small to be useful for case study weight

### Tie-breaker

When unsure, ask: **"Would DJ feel weird quoting this line back to them in a DM?"** If yes (because it's a curated thread or a brag), skip it. If no (because it's a real moment they'd nod at), keep it.

## 10. Stop conditions (how to know you're done)

Stop when **any one** of these is true:

- You have **10 verified finds** across Searches 1–4 (you can stop here even if there's more)
- You've spent **45 minutes** and have at least 5 finds
- You've spent **60 minutes** total, regardless of count (rate-limit yourself)
- All four searches return thin results and the bonus pass also produces nothing — **return what you have, however little**, and note the searches were thin

**Bias toward quality over quantity.** Five real Tier 1 finds is far more valuable than fifteen marginal ones.

## 11. Anti-patterns (things that will burn DJ if you include them)

- **Productivity-influencer tweets** — these people are selling the cure, not feeling the pain. Skip even if they technically mention Notion/Obsidian.
- **Tech reporters / dev tool bloggers** — wrong audience, wrong frame.
- **Curated threads** ("here are 10 tools I use...") — performance, not pain.
- **Promotional posts** dressed up as pain ("my Notion broke so I built this product instead!") — the punchline is selling, not the pain.
- **AI generation / agent / LLM hype tweets** — DJ is anti-AI in positioning; these creators will not be a values fit.
- **Posts from accounts with "AI", "agents", or "build in public" in their bio + verified blue checkmark + >100K followers** — the standard influencer signature. Skip.
- **Crypto / web3 / token tweets** — instant skip regardless of pain content.
- **Anyone whose handle suggests they're a brand or company, not a person** — case studies need a human face.

## 12. Edge cases

- **Their location is "NYC + DMV" or similar split bio:** count them for both regions, mark as Wave 2 (DC) since that's the higher-priority one between them.
- **They live in DC suburbs but didn't list a city:** check pinned post and last 5 tweets for clues (sports team, local event, neighborhood). If you find one corridor-confirming signal, keep them.
- **They were recently in the corridor but moved away (e.g., "RIP Brooklyn, now in LA"):** skip.
- **They're a podcast co-host and the X account is shared by 2 people:** still keep them, but note in the paste-back.
- **Their post is _exactly_ on the boundary of 30 days (e.g., 2026-04-13):** include it but flag it as "borderline."
- **You hit an X rate limit:** stop, paste back what you have, note the limit, and DJ will resume later.

## 13. If you're an AI agent

Additional notes:

- Use browser automation (Chrome with logged-in X session) — X blocks unauthenticated scraping, so you cannot use plain `curl` / `WebFetch` against `x.com/search`.
- Do not invent handles, URLs, or quotes. If you cannot verify a result with the browser, omit it.
- Do not run more than **5 follow-up profile-clicks per search** to avoid triggering X's bot detection. Pace yourself.
- If you can capture screenshots of the verification steps (profile bio, follower count, recent post), include them as references in the paste-back.
- Do **not** like, follow, reply, repost, or DM from DJ's account during this pass. Read-only.
- If the X UI returns "this account is rate-limited" or similar, stop immediately and paste back what you have.
