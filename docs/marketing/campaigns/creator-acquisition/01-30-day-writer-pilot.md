# 30-Day Writer Acquisition Pilot

**Campaign:** Cold Manuscript
**Audience:** manuscript writers — novelists, memoirists, and narrative/nonfiction authors managing long-lived drafts
**Primary outcome:** activated Writer users, not generic social reach
**Duration:** four campaign weeks after foundation tasks are complete
**Owner:** DJ Wayne

## Campaign Thesis

Most writers do not return to a blank page. They return to a cold manuscript and spend part of the writing session reconstructing the book.

BuildOS should be shown as the place where the book's surrounding context can persist: current state, open threads, decisions, notes, and next moves. It does not replace the writer's drafting or craft tools.

## Campaign Offer

Primary research-and-acquisition offer:

> Show me where your manuscript lives today. I will set up the surrounding project in BuildOS with you, free, and you tell me what helps and what feels wrong.

Initial capacity: **three setup sessions**. Record only with explicit permission. A participant may use BuildOS without consenting to public content.

## Concept And Claim Guardrails

Use publicly:

- Re-entry tax
- Manuscript Memory
- Cold manuscript / keep the story warm
- The book remembers where you left it

Use carefully:

- "A surprising amount of writer's block is re-entry friction" — not "writer's block is mostly context failure."
- "Resume without reconstructing everything" until a timed demo proves a five-minute claim.
- "BuildOS holds the context around the manuscript" unless a no-prose mode is technically enforced.

Do not claim yet:

- Memory Mode exists.
- The story bible automatically builds itself.
- BuildOS never generates prose under any circumstance.
- A user will resume in five minutes.

## Pilot Commitments

Minimum shippable pilot:

- 4 real weekly receipt assets
- 4 LinkedIn Receipt Posts
- 4 Instagram Reel or carousel adaptations
- 8 X hook/proof tests
- 40 executed audience touches across the month
- 3 Writer setup sessions
- weekly scorecard completion

Optional derivatives are welcome only after the minimum for that week ships.

## Receipt Post Skeleton

```text
HOOK
One specific moment a manuscript writer recognizes.

PROOF
The real artifact: screenshot, screen recording, or observed setup session.

JUDGMENT
One sentence explaining what the product changed and what it did not change.

OFFER
A useful next step: answer a diagnostic question, offer a setup slot, or link to the matching workflow.
```

## Setup Session Script

1. What are you writing, and what stage is it in?
2. When did you last work on it?
3. Show me where the manuscript and surrounding context live today.
4. What do you reread or reconstruct before you can work?
5. What have you tried to maintain and abandoned?
6. Set up the project using current BuildOS capabilities.
7. Ask the writer to identify what feels useful, wrong, missing, or untrustworthy.
8. Agree on one return action within seven days.
9. Ask separately for permission to quote, record, or publish any artifact.

## Success Criteria

The pilot succeeds if:

- at least 3 writers create or complete a real first project state;
- at least 2 return within seven days and recover a useful state/next move;
- at least 1 participant grants permission for a proof story or quote;
- at least 1 campaign concept is repeated back unprompted by a writer;
- attribution identifies which surface produced each activated participant.

Follower growth alone cannot make the pilot pass.

## End-Of-Pilot Decision

Choose one:

- **KEEP:** the language resonates and activated writers return. Continue Writer campaign and build the dedicated entry path.
- **ITERATE:** audience pain resonates but the product or handoff fails. Fix activation before increasing distribution.
- **NARROW:** one Writer subsegment responds materially better. Focus the next campaign on that subsegment.
- **KILL/PAUSE:** neither qualified audience response nor activation appears after the minimum pilot ships. Preserve the research and move to the YouTuber test.
