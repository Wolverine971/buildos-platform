# Creator Watering-Hole Map

**Campaign now:** Writer / Cold Manuscript
**Campaign next:** YouTuber / Channel Memory
**Rule:** an account list is not a relationship system until touches and replies are logged.

## What Counts As A Watering Hole

A useful watering hole has one or more of these properties:

- working creators describe actual workflow problems there;
- the account owner replies to comments;
- smaller peers repeatedly appear in the comments;
- the audience discusses tools, transitions, and failure modes rather than only celebrating finished work;
- DJ can add lived curiosity without forcing BuildOS into the conversation.

Follower count is not the primary criterion. A 5K-account conversation with seven real comments can be more useful than a 500K-account post with a thousand spectators.

## Relationship Stages

| Stage             | Meaning                                         | Required evidence                       | Next action                               |
| ----------------- | ----------------------------------------------- | --------------------------------------- | ----------------------------------------- |
| 0 — discovered    | Possible audience fit                           | Current profile/post verified           | Observe 2–3 posts                         |
| 1 — observed      | Voice and audience fit confirmed                | At least one relevant live conversation | Execute a useful comment                  |
| 2 — touched       | DJ has commented/replied                        | URL and date logged                     | Watch for reply; touch again naturally    |
| 3 — engaged       | They replied, followed, or meaningfully reacted | Two-way signal logged                   | Continue relationship; diagnose workflow  |
| 4 — invited       | A relevant setup/interview offer was sent       | Specific offer and date logged          | Await response; no generic follow-up spam |
| 5 — participant   | They completed a conversation or setup          | Notes and permission state captured     | Support activation and return             |
| 6 — proof partner | They granted explicit publishing permission     | Approved quote/assets/scope             | Produce case study or Collab asset        |

## Research-To-Action Rule

Every watering-hole session must end with at least one of:

- an executed comment or reply;
- a qualified creator captured at Stage 0 with a verified current post;
- a setup/interview invite to an already-warm contact;
- a verbatim phrase added to the language log;
- a content hypothesis tied to a real conversation.

Do not create another dated queue if no action can be executed. Carry at most three unexecuted items into the next session.

## Writer Map — Build This First

Target mix for the first two weeks:

- 5 anchor accounts: tools, educators, institutions, or established creators whose comments contain writers;
- 10 working peers: small/mid-size manuscript writers, indie authors, editors, book coaches, or author-operators who reply;
- 15 recurring commenters: people visibly describing manuscript/re-entry/continuity problems;
- 10 live pain conversations: individual posts or threads, not profiles.

### Writer Watering-Hole Categories

| Category                   | Examples to verify, not assumed targets            | Pain to mine                                   | Best first move                                      |
| -------------------------- | -------------------------------------------------- | ---------------------------------------------- | ---------------------------------------------------- |
| Drafting / planning tools  | Scrivener, Plottr, Reedsy, story-bible tools       | continuity, tool maintenance, version fog      | Mine user comments; do not pitch under a competitor  |
| Craft educators            | Novel, memoir, and narrative nonfiction teachers   | messy middle, revision, returning after gaps   | Add a concrete workflow observation                  |

| Writer communities         | Writing prompts, challenges, accountability groups | comeback, shame, consistency                   | Participate as a curious builder, not a tool account |
| AI trust conversations     | Writers discussing authorship and AI boundaries    | voice, ownership, contamination fear           | Lead with the boundary; never sell generation        |

## Weekly Execution Quota

Minimum:

- 10 executed touches
- 3 working-peer accounts, not only anchors
- 2 verbatim language captures
- 1 creator conversation or invitation after warmth exists
- 1 content hypothesis tied to an actual post/comment

Quality gates:

- no automated likes, replies, follows, or DMs;
- no engagement pods or coordinated reciprocal timing;
- no BuildOS mention unless the conversation makes it genuinely useful;
- no walls of generic advice;
- no daily research report without an execution result.

## Friday Review

- Which accounts produced two-way signals?
- Which comments attracted the right profile visits or replies?
- Which pain appeared more than once?
- Which phrase did creators use without prompting?
- Which accounts should be promoted, held, or removed?
- What real conversation should determine next week's receipt?
