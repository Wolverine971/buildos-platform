# Cast, Settings, And Writing World

## Core Cast

### The Writer-Protagonist

Who:

Novelist, nonfiction author, screenwriter-adjacent but probably not the main focus, indie/hybrid/trad-curious writer with a day job, caretaking load, or multiple WIPs.

Wants:

To finish the book and still recognize their own voice in it.

Fears:

That the unfinished draft proves they were never a real writer.

BuildOS role:

The system gives the writer a place where the book accumulates instead of evaporates.

### The Inner Critic

Who:

The internal voice that uses craft standards, rejection, comparison, and perfectionism against the writer.

Native language:

"This is derivative." "You already failed this book." "Real writers do not need this much help." "The first chapter is bad, so why keep going?"

BuildOS role:

Not silenced by hype. Contained by evidence: last session, open decision, next move, feedback patterns, and progress history.

### The Beta Reader

Who:

Volunteer or semi-formal reader who reacts to a draft before publication/submission.

Evidence:

`r/BetaReaders` structures beta requests by manuscript status, word count, genre, and title/description, and asks authors to specify feedback type/timeline/critique swap availability ([source](https://www.reddit.com/r/BetaReaders/about.json)).

Wants:

Clear context, appropriate expectations, and a writer who can receive feedback without arguing.

BuildOS object:

Critique Packet.

### The Critique Partner

Who:

A peer writer who trades work and feedback over time.

Evidence:

Scribophile and Critique Circle both build critique around reciprocity: give feedback to earn the right to receive feedback ([Scribophile](https://www.scribophile.com/), [Critique Circle](https://www.critiquecircle.com/landing)).

Wants:

Specific asks, useful context, reciprocal labor, and trust.

BuildOS object:

Mutual context packet plus decision board.

### The Writing Group

Who:

Local, online, Zoom, Discord, forum, or workshop group that provides accountability and feedback.

Wants:

Rhythm, trust, clear submissions, and members who do the work.

BuildOS role:

Prepare session packets and preserve decisions after meetings.

### The Developmental Editor

Who:

Professional focused on big-picture structure, character, genre promise, pacing, and manuscript viability.

Wants:

The whole manuscript, author goals, genre/category, comp awareness, known concerns, and a writer ready to revise.

BuildOS object:

Editor Handoff Packet.

### The Line Editor

Who:

Professional focused on prose, flow, voice, clarity, rhythm, and sentence/paragraph level craft.

Wants:

Stable draft, voice notes, style goals, and genre expectations.

BuildOS object:

Voice guardrails and style sheet.

### The Copyeditor / Proofreader

Who:

Professional focused on correctness, consistency, punctuation, grammar, formatting, house style, and final polish.

Wants:

Stable manuscript, style sheet, spelling preferences, character/place name list, timeline/capitalization rules.

BuildOS object:

Continuity Ledger and style sheet.

### The Literary Agent

Who:

Trad-pub representative/gatekeeper/career adviser who evaluates pitch, pages, market fit, genre, and author career potential.

Evidence:

Jane Friedman says agents are experts in publishing, have editor contacts, negotiate deals, ensure authors are paid fairly, and usually earn commission only when they sell work ([source](https://janefriedman.com/start-here-how-to-get-your-book-published/)).

Wants:

Strong query, clear genre/category, compelling premise, polished pages, market fit, and professional communication.

BuildOS object:

Query Packet.

### The Acquiring Editor

Who:

Publishing-house editor who champions a project internally.

Wants:

A publishable manuscript, market fit, internal support, sales confidence, author professionalism.

BuildOS object:

Pitch bridge from manuscript memory to commercial positioning.

### The Cover Designer

Who:

Design professional who creates genre-appropriate cover.

Evidence:

Reedsy treats cover design as a core self-publishing step and warns through professionals that amateur cover/formatting choices can degrade the book's chances ([source](https://reedsy.com/blog/guide/how-to-self-publish-a-book/)).

Wants:

Genre, comps, tone, audience, trim/formats, series context.

BuildOS object:

Cover brief.

### The Formatter

Who:

Professional/tool producing ebook, paperback, hardcover, or platform-ready files.

Wants:

Final manuscript, front/back matter, ISBN/imprint decisions, trim size, image specs, platform requirements.

BuildOS object:

Production checklist.

### The Sensitivity Reader

Who:

Specialized reader reviewing representation, cultural context, harm, accuracy, and blind spots.

Wants:

Scope, context, author's questions, and the ability to give feedback without being asked to fix everything.

BuildOS object:

Scoped sensitivity brief plus decision log.

### The Publicist / Marketer

Who:

Launch support for publicity, reviews, interviews, newsletters, ads, social, events.

Evidence:

Jane Friedman notes both self-publishing and traditional publishing require author involvement in marketing and promotion ([source](https://janefriedman.com/start-here-how-to-get-your-book-published/)).

Wants:

Positioning, audience, hooks, author bio, media list, launch dates, assets.

BuildOS object:

Launch room.

### The ARC Team

Who:

Advance readers expected to read and review around launch.

Wants:

Clear instructions, dates, links, review expectations, reminders.

BuildOS object:

ARC list and response tracker.

### Newsletter Subscribers / Substack Readers

Who:

People who follow the author between books or during serialization.

Wants:

Consistent value, voice, updates, behind-the-scenes without spam.

BuildOS object:

Reader promise board.

### Reviewers

Who:

Readers, bloggers, NetGalley/BookSirens/BookFunnel users, store reviewers, genre reviewers.

Wants:

Correct genre promise, clear metadata, polished book, easy access.

BuildOS object:

Review outreach tracker.

### Other Authors In The Genre

Who:

Peers, competitors, collaborators, blurb sources, newsletter-swap partners.

Wants:

Professionalism, reciprocity, genre respect.

BuildOS object:

Peer map.

### The Author Who Already Finished

Who:

The writing-group friend, Substack peer, or genre author who has the career the writer wants.

Role:

Comparison ghost and proof of possibility.

BuildOS role:

Convert comparison into workflow clues: what did they systematize, delegate, track, or protect?

## Settings

### Kitchen Table After Kids Are Asleep

Meaning:

Stolen-hours writing. The writer has a short window and cannot afford a cold re-entry.

BuildOS surface:

Five-Minute Re-entry Brief.

### Coffee Shop

Meaning:

Ritual, public solitude, draft session, notebook-to-laptop bridge.

BuildOS surface:

Offline-friendly session note and next-scene card.

### Library

Meaning:

Research, quiet, legitimacy, escape from home noise.

BuildOS surface:

Source vault, research queue, citation/notes bridge.

### Commute Notes App

Meaning:

Fragment capture: dialogue, premise, fix, phrase, dream logic, new project.

BuildOS surface:

Idea inbox that asks: current WIP, future WIP, character, scene, lore, discard.

### Scrivener Corkboard

Meaning:

Spatial story planning, index cards, scene order, chapter overview.

BuildOS surface:

Scene Queue and Thread Map.

### Google Docs Sprawl

Meaning:

Drafts, feedback, shared links, beta notes, version confusion.

BuildOS surface:

Version map and critique import.

### Notebook Pile

Meaning:

Analog memory with no search. The writer trusts it emotionally but cannot retrieve it under pressure.

BuildOS surface:

Captured note, tagged by WIP, scene, character, lore, or question.

### Index-Card Wall

Meaning:

Physical manifestation of structure and control.

BuildOS surface:

Digital card wall with stable manuscript memory.

### QueryTracker Dashboard

Meaning:

Hope and dread quantified: sent, waiting, rejected, requested, closed, open.

Evidence:

QueryTracker foregrounds agent search, query tracking, response time, reply rate, and success interviews ([source](https://querytracker.net/)).

BuildOS surface:

Query Packet and response pattern review.

### Email Inbox With Rejections

Meaning:

The gate speaks through silence, form letters, and occasional specific notes.

BuildOS surface:

Rejection log that separates signal from wound.

### Writing Group Zoom

Meaning:

Community, feedback, accountability, and dread of reading aloud.

BuildOS surface:

Session packet and post-meeting decision board.

### Book Launch Dashboard

Meaning:

The author becomes operator: dates, ARCs, reviews, posts, ads, newsletters, vendors.

BuildOS surface:

Launch room.

### Amazon KDP Backend

Meaning:

Metadata, files, pricing, reports, KDP Select/KU, series, content guidelines, proof copies.

Evidence:

KDP's help navigation exposes the complexity of self-publishing operations: series, royalties, KDP reports, KDP Select, ads, proof copies, content guidelines, AI disclosure ([source](https://kdp.amazon.com/en_US/help/topic/G200672390)).

BuildOS surface:

Publishing ops checklist.

### Substack / Newsletter Editor

Meaning:

Writer-audience relationship, serialization, essays, launch list, paid/free audience.

BuildOS surface:

Reader promise board and excerpt/content angle queue.

## Villain Forces

### Stateless Tools

The notes app, chat window, Google Doc, and notebook each hold pieces but do not know the book.

### The Feed

The phone eats the writing hour and trains the attention span away from chapter-length thought.

### The Aspiring Label

The word "aspiring" turns unfinished work into identity debt.

### The Query Courtroom

Every silence becomes a verdict unless the writer has a tracker and a reframe.

### The AI Slop Flood

Writers fear their work will be devalued, contaminated, scraped, or mistaken for generated material.

### The Scam Flattery Machine

Writer Beware shows scammers increasingly impersonate publishers, agents, editors, festivals, and film contacts ([source](https://writerbeware.blog/)).

### The Tool-Tinkering Trap

The writer reorganizes instead of re-entering the manuscript.

## BuildOS Cast Upgrade

The current archetype should expand from "writer plus editor/agent/beta readers" into a richer ecosystem:

- Inner critic.
- Critique partner.
- Beta reader.
- Developmental editor.
- Line editor.
- Copyeditor/proofreader.
- Literary agent.
- Query dashboard.
- Writer Beware / scam sentinel.
- Cover designer.
- Formatter.
- ARC team.
- Newsletter subscribers.
- KDP backend.
- The author who already finished.
