# Lore, Legends, And Authorities

## Writer Lore And Legends

### The Drawer Novel

What it is:

The manuscript that did not sell, did not finish, or had to be set aside.

Why it matters:

It turns failure into rite of passage. Jane Friedman notes that many authors do not sell their first manuscript and that some manuscripts need to be put in the drawer ([source](https://janefriedman.com/start-here-how-to-get-your-book-published/)).

BuildOS use:

Use as comeback lore, not shame bait. "The drawer novel taught you. The next system holds the work."

### The Query Trenches

What it is:

The long, emotionally punishing process of agent querying.

Why it matters:

It has a setting and tools: QueryTracker, MSWL, query letter, synopsis, comps, first pages, partials/fulls, rejections, silence, R&R, offer of representation.

Evidence:

`r/PubTips` is built around query critique and publishing questions ([source](https://www.reddit.com/r/PubTips/about.json)). QueryTracker supports agent search, tracking, response data, and success stories ([source](https://querytracker.net/)). MSWL exists to tell writers what agents/editors want in their inbox ([source](https://manuscriptwishlist.com/)).

BuildOS use:

Query Packet, not "confidence hack."

### The Slush Pile

What it is:

The undifferentiated heap of unsolicited submissions, used as writer folklore for the difficulty of being noticed.

BuildOS use:

Useful as a background institution, but do not overuse. It can sound dated or fatalistic.

### NaNoWriMo

What it is:

The annual 50,000-word November novel sprint and the community/lore around write-ins, badges, word counts, and week-two collapse.

Current status:

The institution suffered major trust damage over forum-safety issues and AI controversy; The Guardian reported in April 2025 that NaNoWriMo would close after 20 years ([source](https://www.theguardian.com/books/2025/apr/02/creative-writing-nanowrimo-to-close-after-20-years)). The Verge and Wired reported the 2024 AI backlash around its statement on AI ([The Verge](https://www.theverge.com/2024/9/3/24234811/nanowrimo-ai-stance-classist-ableist-criticism), [Wired](https://www.wired.com/story/nanowrimo-organizers-classist-and-ableist-to-condemn-ai)).

BuildOS use:

Use "NaNoWriMo week-two collapse" carefully. The lore is still recognizable, but the brand itself is no longer a clean positive reference.

### Shitty First Drafts

What it is:

Anne Lamott's widely cited permission structure for drafting badly before revising.

BuildOS use:

Useful as a principle, but avoid quoting the phrase heavily in public copy without checking rights/context. BuildOS can express the same idea as: "The first pass is raw material."

### You Can't Edit A Blank Page

What it is:

Common drafting folklore.

BuildOS use:

Useful for getting writers out of perfectionism, but incomplete. BuildOS should add: "You also cannot revise a draft you cannot re-enter."

### Kill Your Darlings

What it is:

Common revision folklore about cutting beloved material that does not serve the work.

BuildOS use:

Reframe gently: "Keep a cuttings file. Nothing has to be thrown into the void."

### All Writing Is Rewriting

What it is:

Revision-centered craft lore.

BuildOS use:

Useful for Revision Runway.

### The First Million Words

What it is:

Long-road craft lore: skill accumulates through volume and practice.

BuildOS use:

Useful for de-shaming the drawer novel, but can sound punishing.

### The MFA Vs No-MFA Divide

What it is:

Identity tension between institutional literary validation and self-taught/genre/indie craft routes.

BuildOS use:

Avoid choosing sides. BuildOS is for the manuscript, not credential sorting.

### Trad Pub Vs Self Pub Rivalry

What it is:

Ongoing tension between prestige/gatekeeping and control/entrepreneurship.

Evidence:

Jane Friedman lays out trad, assisted/hybrid, and self-publishing paths and the tradeoffs between them ([source](https://janefriedman.com/start-here-how-to-get-your-book-published/)). Reedsy and ALLi provide self-publishing process and watchdog resources ([Reedsy](https://reedsy.com/blog/guide/how-to-self-publish-a-book/), [ALLi](https://selfpublishingadvice.org/)).

BuildOS use:

Treat publishing path as a decision tree, not a moral identity.

### AI Writing Controversy

What it is:

The current high-voltage trust issue: training data, rights, disclosure, craft, originality, accessibility, class, labor, and slop.

Evidence:

KDP distinguishes AI-generated from AI-assisted content and requires disclosure for generated text/images/translations ([source](https://kdp.amazon.com/en_US/help/topic/G200672390)). NaNoWriMo backlash shows writer communities resist rhetoric that frames AI criticism as ignorance or privilege ([The Verge](https://www.theverge.com/2024/9/3/24234811/nanowrimo-ai-stance-classist-ableist-criticism), [Wired](https://www.wired.com/story/nanowrimo-organizers-classist-and-ableist-to-condemn-ai)).

BuildOS use:

"The words stay yours; the system holds the threads."

## Authorities Worth Tracking

### Jane Friedman

Type:

Publishing business, author education, traditional publishing, self-publishing, platform.

Authority:

Her "Start Here" guide is a comprehensive map of publishing paths, query materials, agents, and submission process ([source](https://janefriedman.com/start-here-how-to-get-your-book-published/)).

Best for:

Trad-pub writers, nonfiction authors, platform-minded writers, hybrid decision-making.

BuildOS borrow:

Practical seriousness. No shortcuts, no magical publishing promises.

### Writer Beware

Type:

Scam warning, publishing bad practice, author protection.

Authority:

Official blog sponsored by SFWA, tracking literary scams, publishing scams, AI impersonation, fake agents/editors, and predatory services ([source](https://writerbeware.blog/)).

Best for:

All writers, especially self-published/aspiring authors who receive unsolicited offers.

BuildOS borrow:

"Protect the author's hope from predatory flattery."

### QueryTracker

Type:

Trad-pub query tracking and agent database.

Authority:

Public homepage foregrounds agent search, query organization, response data, and success stories ([source](https://querytracker.net/)).

Best for:

Trad-pub novelists and memoirists.

BuildOS borrow:

Dashboard language: sent, waiting, response time, request, offer, closed/open.

### Manuscript Wish List

Type:

Agent/editor taste and targeting.

Authority:

The site is built around the submissions question "What do you wish you had in your inbox?" ([source](https://manuscriptwishlist.com/)).

Best for:

Trad-pub writers matching manuscripts to agents/editors.

BuildOS borrow:

Agent fit, not generic agent list.

### Reedsy

Type:

Publishing services marketplace, self-publishing guides, editor/designer/marketer ecosystem.

Authority:

Reedsy's self-publishing guide outlines writing, editing, cover, metadata, distribution, pricing, publishing, and promotion ([source](https://reedsy.com/blog/guide/how-to-self-publish-a-book/)).

Best for:

Indie authors, first-time self-publishers, authors hiring professionals.

BuildOS borrow:

Production pipeline and professional handoff language.

### ALLi / Self-Publishing Advice Center

Type:

Indie author advocacy, self-publishing best practice, watchdog ratings.

Authority:

ALLi's advice center describes itself as author outreach and includes process guides plus service/award ratings ([source](https://selfpublishingadvice.org/)).

Best for:

Self-publishers and hybrid authors.

BuildOS borrow:

Ethics, excellence, scam avoidance, author-as-publisher seriousness.

### KDP

Type:

Platform/backend authority.

Authority:

KDP publishes official content guidelines, AI disclosure requirements, KDP Select/KU, reports, royalties, proof copies, and series support ([source](https://kdp.amazon.com/en_US/help/topic/G200672390)).

Best for:

Self-publishing authors.

BuildOS borrow:

Operational specificity: files, reports, content guidelines, AI generated/assisted distinction.

### The Creative Penn / Joanna Penn

Type:

Author-entrepreneur, indie publishing, marketing, AI-positive but author-centered.

Authority:

The Creative Penn centers writing, self-publishing, book marketing, making a living with writing, and Joanna Penn's own author-business journey ([source](https://www.thecreativepenn.com/)).

Best for:

Indie/hybrid authors, nonfiction authors, author-business builders.

BuildOS borrow:

Author business, not just manuscript.

### Writing Excuses

Type:

Craft podcast.

Authority:

Long-running craft source, useful for genre writers and working-writer conversations ([source](https://writingexcuses.com/)).

Best for:

Genre writers, craft students.

BuildOS borrow:

Short, specific craft concepts and language.

### Brandon Sanderson Lectures

Type:

Public craft lectures, especially fantasy/SF.

Authority:

BYU lecture playlist is widely shared among genre writers ([source](https://www.youtube.com/playlist?list=PLSH_xM-KC3Zv-79sVZTTj-YA6IAqh8qeQ)).

Best for:

Fantasy/SF writers, worldbuilding, plotting, writing career.

BuildOS borrow:

Genre seriousness and worldbuilding/career language.

### K.M. Weiland / Helping Writers Become Authors

Type:

Story structure, outlining, character arcs, plot diagnostics.

Authority:

Her story-structure series lays out hooks, acts, midpoint, climax, resolution, and linked story structure ([source](https://www.helpingwritersbecomeauthors.com/secrets-story-structure-complete-series/)).

Best for:

Plotters, plantsers, novelists seeking diagnostic frameworks.

BuildOS borrow:

Structure as diagnosis, not formula.

### Story Grid

Type:

Editorial/craft framework.

Authority:

Story Grid maps genres, beats, scenes, obligatory moments, and editing diagnostics ([source](https://storygrid.com/)).

Best for:

Writers seeking rigorous structural/editing tools.

BuildOS borrow:

Scene/beat diagnostic language.

### Save The Cat Writes A Novel

Type:

Beat-sheet structure system.

Authority:

Save the Cat's official site sells the novel-writing adaptation and foregrounds beat sheets and story tools ([source](https://savethecat.com/products/books/save-the-cat-writes-a-novel)).

Best for:

Plotters and writers who want simple structural scaffolding.

BuildOS borrow:

Beat sheet support, with optionality to avoid formula resistance.

### Ursula K. Le Guin

Type:

Craft, literary taste, language, imagination, seriousness.

Authority:

Official site page for `Words Are My Matter` anchors her as craft/taste authority ([source](https://www.ursulakleguin.com/words-are-my-matter)).

Best for:

Literary/genre writers suspicious of productivity reductionism.

BuildOS borrow:

Respect for language and imagination.

## What To Borrow Into BuildOS

- From Jane Friedman: publishing patience and process literacy.
- From Writer Beware: scam defense and respect for author vulnerability.
- From QueryTracker/MSWL: query as dashboard plus fit.
- From Reedsy/ALLi/KDP: self-publishing is production operations.
- From critique communities: reciprocity, specificity, and feedback triage.
- From craft teachers: scene/beat/thread/structure language.
- From AI backlash: never frame AI as replacement, shortcut, or moral inevitability.
