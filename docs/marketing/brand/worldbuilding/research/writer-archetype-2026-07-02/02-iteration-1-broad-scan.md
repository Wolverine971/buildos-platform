# Iteration 1 - Broad Scan

## Scan Summary

The broad scan supports the current Writer archetype's direction but sharpens the problem:

Writers are not asking for another blank-page pep talk. They are managing a living manuscript that keeps losing state.

The strongest sources point to four overlapping worlds:

1. Craft: scenes, chapters, arcs, voice, POV, stakes, structure, worldbuilding, revision.
2. Community: beta readers, critique partners, writing groups, forums, Substack readers, Discord peers.
3. Publishing: query letters, agents, editors, self-publishing services, KDP, rights, royalties, ARCs, launches, scams.
4. Trust: AI contamination anxiety, plagiarism fear, scam fear, and skepticism toward tools that claim they can "write for you."

The most useful BuildOS line is: writers do not only need a place to write. They need a place where the book keeps its memory.

## Reddit And Community Signal

### `r/writing` - Craft Discussion With Strong Anti-AI Norms

`r/writing` frames itself as a place for "important matters for writers" and craft discussion, not a place to post work. Its rules route critique and self-promotion into weekly threads and explicitly ban generative AI in creative-writing posts/comments ([source](https://www.reddit.com/r/writing/about.json)).

Source-backed observations:

- "Posting work" is guarded because writers dumping drafts turns a craft forum into a slush pile.
- Weekly thread categories are revealing: writer's block/motivation, brainstorming, first-page feedback, and tools/software/hardware.
- The no-AI rule is a strong trust signal. A BuildOS writer story must never sound like "let the machine produce the prose."

Interpretation:

The main community wound is dilution. Writers are protecting the room from low-effort prompts, spam, AI slop, and drive-by critique requests. BuildOS language must show it understands craft labor and community reciprocity.

### `r/selfpublish` - Indie Author Business Without Promo Spam

`r/selfpublish` describes itself as a place to discuss indie books, not self-promotion. Its sidebar bans self-promo in general posts, "shadow promoting," AI posts, and surveying the crowd ([source](https://www.reddit.com/r/selfpublish/about.json)).

Source-backed observations:

- The community points members to its wiki for blurb writing, genre marketing, technical questions, and companies to avoid.
- Celebratory posts belong in weekly self-promotion/achievement threads.
- Networking posts for Discords, subreddits, websites, or services are treated as self-promotion.

Interpretation:

Self-published writers are not simply "creatives." They are overloaded operators. They have to decide cover, editing, launch, ads, KDP/KU/wide, metadata, reviews, and scam avoidance. BuildOS can speak to them through launch packet, ARC list, metadata board, and author-business memory.

### `r/PubTips` - Query Trenches As Professionalization

`r/PubTips` is a traditional publishing community with tags like `[QCrit]`, `[PubQ]`, `[News]`, `[PubTip]`, `[Discussion]`, and `[AMA]`. Its rules require query critique titles to include book metadata, limit QCrit frequency, discourage recently answered questions, ban solicitation/self-promotion, and warn against harmful publishing misinformation ([source](https://www.reddit.com/r/PubTips/about.json)).

Source-backed observations:

- Query critique is not casual. It is a structured professional ritual.
- The community is for writers preparing to seek or actively seeking representation/publication.
- Verified industry flairs matter because writers need information from people who know the industry.

Interpretation:

The query trenches are a setting, not just a pain point. The dashboard, agent list, query letter, synopsis, comp titles, first pages, rejection email, and full request all have emotional charge. BuildOS can create a "query packet" that feels native if it treats querying as evidence management, not confidence theater.

### `r/DestructiveReaders` - Critique As Reciprocity And Trial

`r/DestructiveReaders` is built around blunt critique, high-effort reciprocity, and a leech-tag economy. Writers are expected to critique before posting, match word counts, avoid rough drafts, and keep critique about the writing. It explicitly bans AI-generated critique and ChatGPT spam accounts ([source](https://www.reddit.com/r/DestructiveReaders/about.json)).

Source-backed observations:

- "Leech" is a moral category: someone who takes critique without giving.
- Google Docs comments do not count as the required high-effort critique.
- The community warns writers not to submit if they react poorly to harsh feedback.

Interpretation:

Critique is both medicine and wound. Writers need feedback, but feedback arrives as a pile of incompatible opinions, line edits, ego hits, and revision obligations. BuildOS can help by turning critique into a sorted decision board instead of an identity verdict.

### `r/fantasywriters` - Worldbuilding Needs Prior Thought

`r/fantasywriters` requires critique and brainstorming posts to include sufficient story context and demonstrate prior thinking/research. It bans self-promotion and AI-generated stories ([source](https://www.reddit.com/r/fantasywriters/about.json)).

Source-backed observations:

- Fantasy writers bring magic systems, worlds, subgenres, excerpts, and concept feedback.
- The community pushes against "do my thinking for me" posts.

Interpretation:

Genre writers will respond better to "canon board," "lore vault," and "continuity ledger" than generic "project management." The task is not to replace worldbuilding; it is to keep worldbuilding from detaching from the story.

### `r/BetaReaders` - Feedback Etiquette And Manuscript Matching

`r/BetaReaders` formats requests by manuscript status, word count, genre, and title/description. It discourages paid-service offers and teaches beta readers to be diplomatic while telling authors not to argue with feedback ([source](https://www.reddit.com/r/BetaReaders/about.json)).

Source-backed observations:

- The request packet itself is structured: blurb, excerpt, content warnings, feedback type, timeline, critique-swap availability.
- Feedback etiquette is explicit because beta reading is emotionally loaded.

Interpretation:

The "critique packet" should include: manuscript snapshot, what kind of feedback is wanted, what to ignore, timeline, content warnings, and post-feedback triage.

## Forum Signal

### Absolute Write - The Full Writing Town

Absolute Write describes itself as a moderated, free, online community of writers, agents, editors, and publishers. It has public forums for goals, rejection, writer's block, novels, poetry, scripts, research, publishing FAQs, agents, editors, self-publishing, cover/design, and bewares ([source](https://absolutewrite.com/forums/)).

Source-backed observations:

- "Rejection and Dejection" exists as a dedicated setting.
- "Outwitting Writer's Block" exists as a dedicated setting, with the forum description saying it discusses coping methods and does not debate whether block exists.
- "Bewares, Recommendations & Background Check" is a major institution for publisher/agent scam defense.
- Absolute Write currently states a strict zero-tolerance policy for AI-generated or AI-assisted material.

Interpretation:

Absolute Write is especially useful for worldbuilding. It reveals the writer's town: the cafe, the rejection room, the agent office, the editor office, the scam watchboard, the block clinic, the genre neighborhoods, and the goals board.

### Scribophile And Critique Circle - Reciprocity Machines

Scribophile positions itself as a large online writing community with a points-based peer critique system. Writers earn karma by giving feedback and spend it to post writing for critique ([source](https://www.scribophile.com/)). Critique Circle similarly describes a credit cycle: critique a couple stories, gain credits, submit your story, rewrite ([source](https://www.critiquecircle.com/landing)).

Source-backed observations:

- Critique communities are designed against drive-by extraction.
- Their strongest promise is not "validation"; it is detailed feedback, beta readers, and a less lonely writing process.

Interpretation:

BuildOS should not try to replace critique partners. It can prepare writers for critique, preserve feedback, help sort contradictions, and keep the manuscript's own decision history visible.

## Publishing Authority Signal

### Jane Friedman - Finish First, Then Enter The Gate

Jane Friedman's "Start Here" guide says most first-time novelists and memoirists need to finish the manuscript before approaching agents or editors; it also outlines genre/category, agents/publishers, query/proposal materials, and submission ([source](https://janefriedman.com/start-here-how-to-get-your-book-published/)).

Source-backed observations:

- Traditional publishing is a staged system, not a single "send book" action.
- Query materials usually include query letter, synopsis, sample chapters, and sometimes proposals.
- Jane explicitly notes some authors face hundreds of rejections and some manuscripts must be put in the drawer.
- She also says self-publishing requires sustained marketing/promotion and entrepreneurial mindset.

Interpretation:

The archetype should not shame drawer novels. They are expected in the path. The stronger story is "the drawer novel taught you, and now your system can hold the next one."

### QueryTracker And MSWL - The Dashboard And The Inbox

QueryTracker says it helps authors find literary agents, organize and track queries, and inspect response-time/reply-rate data across a database of agents ([source](https://querytracker.net/)). Manuscript Wish List frames itself around the submissions-process question, "What do you wish you had in your inbox?" ([source](https://manuscriptwishlist.com/)).

Source-backed observations:

- Querying is database work: agent lists, open/closed status, genres, response rates, dates, and outcomes.
- Agent fit is content fit, not just contact collection.

Interpretation:

The query packet should link premise, genre/category, comps, agent fit, submission requirements, first pages, synopsis, version history, and responses.

### Writer Beware - Scam Defense Is Now Core Author Literacy

Writer Beware's current homepage says writing scams are prevalent and aggressive, with direct solicitation via email/phone/social, fake publishers/agents/film adaptation offers, and AI-driven impersonation ([source](https://writerbeware.blog/)).

Source-backed observations:

- Real agents/publishers/film people rarely cold-contact writers.
- Recent posts include AI scams, fake interviews/festivals, impersonated Big Five editors, and copyright/AI issues.

Interpretation:

Scam defense belongs in the Writer archetype's world. The villain is not only the blank page; it is the flattery machine that targets an author's hope.

### Self-Publishing Sources - The Author Becomes The Publisher

Reedsy's self-publishing guide breaks the process into writing a quality manuscript, editing, cover, metadata, distribution, pricing, publishing, and promotion ([source](https://reedsy.com/blog/guide/how-to-self-publish-a-book/)). ALLi frames its Self-Publishing Advice Center as author outreach with advice across publishing processes and watchdog ratings ([source](https://selfpublishingadvice.org/)).

Source-backed observations:

- Reedsy explicitly notes indie authors must manage the process themselves and often hire professionals.
- ALLi emphasizes ethics, excellence, and service ratings/watchdog support.
- KDP's help center surfaces print, ebook, series, KDP Select/KU, royalties, reports, ads, and content guidelines in one operational backend ([source](https://kdp.amazon.com/en_US/help/topic/G200672390)).

Interpretation:

For indie/hybrid writers, BuildOS can become the "author operations room": manuscript, production, launch, backlist, rights, ads, newsletter, and reports.

## Craft Signal

### Structure And Diagnostics

K.M. Weiland frames story structure as a way to diagnose and remedy plot problems, with hooks, inciting events, plot points, midpoint, climax, and resolution ([source](https://www.helpingwritersbecomeauthors.com/secrets-story-structure-complete-series/)). Story Grid organizes craft around genres, scenes, beats, obligatory moments, diagnostics, and editor questions ([source](https://storygrid.com/)). Save the Cat foregrounds beat sheets and novel-writing structure tools ([source](https://savethecat.com/products/books/save-the-cat-writes-a-novel)).

Interpretation:

BuildOS should not choose one craft religion. It should be framework-flexible: beat sheet, scene list, thread map, revision pass, question log, and decision ledger.

### Craft Over Hacks

Story Grid's homepage pushes against "write a book in 30 days" hacks and instant Amazon fame, arguing writers want to create something lasting and need skill ([source](https://storygrid.com/)).

Interpretation:

This validates a BuildOS avoidance rule: do not promise hacks. Promise a place where craft decisions persist.

### Writer As Author-Entrepreneur

The Creative Penn positions Joanna Penn's work around writing, self-publishing, book marketing, making a living with writing, and the transition from fear of AI to using it to amplify creativity/income ([source](https://www.thecreativepenn.com/)).

Interpretation:

Indie authors are a stronger initial BuildOS wedge than purely aspirational writers because they already think in systems, launches, tools, and business continuity.

## AI Trust Signal

### Platform Disclosure

KDP requires authors to disclose AI-generated text, images, or translations, while distinguishing AI-assisted use such as brainstorming, editing, error-checking, or refinement from AI-generated content ([source](https://kdp.amazon.com/en_US/help/topic/G200672390)).

Interpretation:

The safest BuildOS promise is "assistive, not generative": help remember, find, sort, compare, brief, and check continuity; do not produce the manuscript.

### Community Backlash

The Verge and Wired reported that NaNoWriMo's 2024 AI stance triggered major backlash from writers, including criticism from disabled writers and resignations from prominent authors, because the language framed opposition to AI as classist/ableist and touched authors' fears about exploitation and devaluation ([The Verge](https://www.theverge.com/2024/9/3/24234811/nanowrimo-ai-stance-classist-ableist-criticism), [Wired](https://www.wired.com/story/nanowrimo-organizers-classist-and-ableist-to-condemn-ai)).

Interpretation:

Do not moralize at writers about AI. Give them control boundaries. Let the words remain theirs.

### Research Signal

"Holding the Line" found writers' openness to AI varies by writing stage and mode, and that drawing firm boundaries with AI is key for some writers ([source](https://arxiv.org/abs/2404.13165)). "It was 80% me, 20% AI" found writers and readers reason about authenticity as a process and self-preservation issue, not only a final-text issue ([source](https://arxiv.org/abs/2411.13032)).

Interpretation:

BuildOS should expose boundaries as product language: "never generate prose," "draft-only memory," "summarize my notes," "track continuity," "ask before suggesting text," "source-linked suggestions."

## What Is High Signal

High-signal language:

- WIP, draft, revision pass, query, synopsis, comps, beta reader, critique partner, manuscript, scene, chapter, beat, arc, POV, stakes, midpoint, muddy/sagging middle, trunk/drawer novel, story bible, series bible, launch team, ARC, KDP, KU, wide.

High-signal pain:

- Re-entry after time away.
- Continuity drift.
- Messy middle.
- Shiny new idea.
- Feedback overload.
- Rejection and query silence.
- Self-publishing launch overwhelm.
- AI distrust and rights anxiety.
- Scam targeting.

Low-signal/noisy advice:

- "Just write every day."
- "Use AI to write your novel faster."
- "Self-publishing is easy passive income."
- "Beat writer's block with this one trick."
- "No one needs a system, just discipline."

## First-Pass Worldbuilding Implications

- The writer's setting is not only the desk. It is the desk after three weeks away, the query tracker dashboard, the critique Google Doc, the KDP backend, the rejection inbox, the beta reader spreadsheet, the genre Discord, and the notes app after a commute.
- The recurring enemy is not one villain. It is state loss: the book cools off, the writer returns cold, and the system makes them prove they are a writer again.
- The comeback is not a heroic sprint. It is a warm re-entry into a manuscript that still knows where it was.
