# BuildOS Translation Opportunities

## Core Positioning Upgrade

Current direction:

The Writer archetype correctly names re-entry tax, story stays warm, drawer novel, story bible, writer's block as context failure, and the notes app graveyard.

Recommended upgrade:

Make the central problem "the book does not remember itself."

Better formulation:

Writers do not need another app that promises discipline. They need a manuscript memory: a place where the book's scenes, threads, characters, decisions, research, critique notes, and next moves stay warm between writing sessions.

## What BuildOS Should Call The Writer's Problem

Primary:

- The Re-entry Tax.
- Manuscript Heat Loss.
- The Cold Manuscript.
- The Book That Does Not Remember Itself.

Best external-facing phrase:

"Your book deserves a place that remembers it."

Best product-facing phrase:

"Manuscript Memory."

Best content angle:

"Writer's block is often a context failure."

## Highest-Signal BuildOS Concepts

### 1. Manuscript Memory

Definition:

The persistent state of the book: scenes, characters, threads, decisions, notes, timeline, feedback, next moves.

Why it matters:

Every other feature becomes natural if the book has memory.

Product surface:

Project home showing:

- Current draft state.
- Last session.
- Open threads.
- Active scene.
- Character/location reminders.
- Revision pass.
- Next concrete move.

Messaging:

"BuildOS never writes your book. It remembers it."

### 2. Five-Minute Re-entry Brief

Definition:

A small session brief that gets the writer from cold start to next words.

Product surface:

Generated after each session and shown before the next:

- Where you left off.
- What changed.
- What is unresolved.
- What scene is ready.
- What to ignore today.

Messaging:

"Resume in five minutes, not forty."

### 3. Scene Queue

Definition:

A list of scenes by readiness, not just outline order.

Product surface:

Scene cards with:

- POV.
- Goal/conflict/outcome.
- Characters.
- Open threads.
- Required lore/research.
- Status: ready, blocked, revise, cut, move.

Messaging:

"Know which scene is ripe before you sit down."

### 4. Thread Map

Definition:

The visible map of plot, character, mystery, emotional, thematic, and worldbuilding threads.

Product surface:

Thread pages that link to scenes, notes, questions, and payoffs.

Messaging:

"See what the book has promised, and where it pays off."

### 5. Continuity Ledger

Definition:

Canon tracker for details that need to remain true.

Product surface:

Characters, places, timeline, rules, injuries, promises, terminology, dates, ages, relationships.

Messaging:

"Catch the continuity break before a beta reader does."

### 6. Revision Runway

Definition:

A sequenced revision plan with focused passes.

Product surface:

Passes:

- Structure.
- Character/arc.
- Thread/payoff.
- Continuity.
- Scene purpose.
- Voice/line.
- Copy/proof.

Messaging:

"Revision is a runway, not a wall."

### 7. Critique Packet

Definition:

The prepared context for beta readers, critique partners, writing groups, or editors.

Product surface:

Includes status, word count, genre, blurb, excerpt, feedback asks, content warnings, timeline, and what not to focus on.

Source basis:

`r/BetaReaders` publicly recommends beta request context such as blurb, excerpt, feedback type, timeline, and critique-swap availability ([source](https://www.reddit.com/r/BetaReaders/about.json)).

Messaging:

"Ask for the feedback you actually need."

### 8. Critique Triage Board

Definition:

A board that turns notes into decisions.

Product surface:

Columns:

- Repeated signal.
- One-off taste note.
- Structural issue.
- Continuity issue.
- Line-level suggestion.
- Accept.
- Reject.
- Defer.

Messaging:

"Feedback is signal. You decide what becomes the book."

### 9. Query Packet

Definition:

The full trad-pub submission context.

Product surface:

Query letter, synopsis, comps, first pages, agent list, MSWL fit, submission requirements, QueryTracker status, rejections/responses.

Source basis:

`r/PubTips`, QueryTracker, MSWL, and Jane Friedman all point to querying as a structured professional workflow ([`r/PubTips`](https://www.reddit.com/r/PubTips/about.json), [QueryTracker](https://querytracker.net/), [MSWL](https://manuscriptwishlist.com/), [Jane Friedman](https://janefriedman.com/start-here-how-to-get-your-book-published/)).

Messaging:

"A query packet, not a panic folder."

### 10. Drawer-Novel Comeback Mode

Definition:

A guided process for reopening an abandoned draft.

Product surface:

Revive/mine/trunk workflow:

- Summarize existing draft.
- Identify live heat.
- Identify stall cause.
- Preserve salvageable scenes/characters/lore.
- Create next scene or archive gracefully.

Messaging:

"The drawer novel is not evidence against you."

### 11. Lore Vault / Canon Board

Definition:

Worldbuilding source vault tied to scene use and continuity.

Product surface:

Lore entries with:

- Rule/fact.
- Where established.
- Where used.
- Contradictions.
- Open questions.

Messaging:

"Worldbuilding that stays attached to the story."

### 12. Editor Handoff Packet

Definition:

The context professional collaborators need to help without flattening the book.

Product surface:

Manuscript status, author goals, known issues, genre/category, comps, voice notes, revision history, questions, boundaries.

Messaging:

"Send the editor the book behind the book."

## Terms To Redefine In The Archetype

### Writer's Block

Archetype line:

Writer's block is often the feeling of reopening a manuscript with no memory.

Avoid:

"Writer's block is fake."

Use:

"Sometimes you are not blocked. You are cold-starting a whole book."

### Aspiring Writer

Archetype line:

If you are writing, you are a writer. "Aspiring" is often an unfinished system, not an unfinished identity.

Avoid:

Over-promising validation.

Use:

"The work is in progress. So are you."

### Organized

Archetype line:

Organized means you can resume.

Avoid:

Template fetish or folder hygiene.

Use:

"The only organization metric that matters: can you write the next scene?"

### Story Bible

Archetype line:

A story bible should not become another project. It should accrete from the manuscript.

Avoid:

"Never forget a detail again" if too absolute.

Use:

"Let the story bible grow as the book grows."

### AI

Archetype line:

BuildOS is not your co-author. It is your memory, clerk, librarian, continuity assistant, and handoff system.

Avoid:

"Generate prose," "AI novelist," "write your story for you."

Use:

"Your words stay yours."

## How BuildOS Should Talk About AI For Writers

Safe frame:

- Memory, not authorship.
- Context, not replacement.
- Recall, not generation.
- Search, not stealing voice.
- Continuity, not creative decision.
- Handoff, not outsourcing.

Specific language:

- "Never writes your book."
- "Works from your notes and drafts."
- "Shows sources for what it remembers."
- "Summarizes your own context."
- "Keeps a decision log."
- "Turns feedback into choices."

Evidence:

KDP's AI policy gives a useful distinction between AI-generated and AI-assisted content ([source](https://kdp.amazon.com/en_US/help/topic/G200672390)). Writer communities' anti-AI rules and the NaNoWriMo backlash show that generic AI enthusiasm is culturally risky ([`r/writing`](https://www.reddit.com/r/writing/about.json), [`r/selfpublish`](https://www.reddit.com/r/selfpublish/about.json), [The Verge](https://www.theverge.com/2024/9/3/24234811/nanowrimo-ai-stance-classist-ableist-criticism)).

## Product Surfaces That Would Feel Natural

### Manuscript Home

The first screen for a book:

- Current state.
- Last session.
- Next session brief.
- Open threads.
- Scene queue.
- Active revision pass.
- Upcoming external deadlines.

### Session Closeout

After writing:

- What changed?
- What did you decide?
- What is the next scene?
- What should future you remember?

### Re-entry Brief

Before writing:

- You were here.
- These threads are open.
- This decision matters.
- This scene is ready.
- Ignore these other tasks today.

### Story Bible That Builds Itself

Autofills from notes/drafts but asks for writer confirmation:

- Character fact detected.
- New place detected.
- Timeline event detected.
- Magic/world rule detected.
- Possible contradiction detected.

### Revision Pass Planner

Focused pass creation:

- Pass goal.
- Scope.
- Files/scenes.
- Known issues.
- Done criteria.
- Deferred pile.

### Critique Packet Generator

For beta readers/partners/editors:

- Blurb.
- Draft stage.
- Word count.
- Feedback asks.
- Context.
- Content warnings.
- Timeline.
- What not to worry about.

### Query Packet Builder

For trad-pub:

- Query letter.
- Synopsis.
- Comps.
- First pages.
- Agent list.
- MSWL notes.
- Submission requirements.
- Response log.

### Launch Room

For self-pub/hybrid:

- Editing.
- Cover.
- Formatting.
- Metadata.
- ISBN/imprint.
- KDP/wide decisions.
- ARC team.
- Newsletter.
- Ads.
- Reviews.

### Scam Check

Lightweight author protection:

- Did they cold-contact you?
- Are they asking for money upfront?
- Are they impersonating a known publisher/agent?
- Can you verify the email/domain?
- Cross-check Writer Beware/ALLi/Absolute Write.

Source basis:

Writer Beware's current coverage emphasizes direct solicitation, impersonation, AI-driven scams, and fake publishing/film offers ([source](https://writerbeware.blog/)).

## Things BuildOS Should Avoid Saying

- Anything implying writers are lazy.
- Anything implying AI is inevitable and objections are backward.
- Anything implying a tool can make rejection go away.
- Anything promising bestseller outcomes.
- Anything treating critique partners, beta readers, or editors as obsolete.
- Anything that collapses trad-pub and self-pub into one generic "publish" workflow.
- Anything that sounds like content marketing rather than manuscript craft.

## Recommended Next Archetype Direction

Lead with the emotional image already in the draft:

"The writer's world is a long walk with a heavy book."

Then sharpen the mechanism:

"The weight is not only the word count. It is the mental load of keeping a whole book alive in your head while life keeps interrupting."

Then introduce BuildOS:

"BuildOS is the place where the book remembers itself."

The rewrite should feel less like "a productivity system for writers" and more like "a manuscript memory for the person who has already written more than they admit."
