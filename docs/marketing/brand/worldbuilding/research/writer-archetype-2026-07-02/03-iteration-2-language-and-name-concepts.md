## Strong Name Concept Candidates

### 1. Manuscript Memory

What it names:

The persistent context of the book: characters, scenes, open threads, decisions, unresolved questions, revision notes, and next actions.

Why writers would recognize it:

Writers already use story bible, series bible, character sheet, timeline, and manuscript tracker. Communities like `r/writing` even dedicate weekly space to writing tools/software/hardware ([source](https://www.reddit.com/r/writing/about.json)).

Source/community evidence:

- Scribophile and Critique Circle exist because manuscripts need readers and revision memory, not just private drafting ([Scribophile](https://www.scribophile.com/), [Critique Circle](https://www.critiquecircle.com/landing)).
- K.M. Weiland and Story Grid both frame craft as diagnosable structure, not mystical word count ([K.M. Weiland](https://www.helpingwritersbecomeauthors.com/secrets-story-structure-complete-series/), [Story Grid](https://storygrid.com/)).

BuildOS use:

"Manuscript Memory keeps your book's decisions, open threads, and next scenes warm between sessions."

### 2. The Five-Minute Re-entry

What it names:

The ability to return to a manuscript after a gap and understand where to begin without rereading half the draft.

Why writers would recognize it:

The current archetype's "re-entry tax" maps directly onto day-job/caretaking/stolen-hours writing. Writers have limited sessions and lose momentum when the first 40 minutes is archaeology.

Source/community evidence:

- `r/writing` has repeated weekly "writer's block and motivation" threads, which signals that re-entry and momentum are not one-off problems ([source](https://www.reddit.com/r/writing/about.json)).
- Absolute Write has a dedicated writer's block board that treats block as real and discusses coping methods ([source](https://absolutewrite.com/forums/)).

BuildOS use:

"Open the Five-Minute Re-entry before you draft: where you left off, what changed, what to write next."

### 3. Story Stays Warm / Warm Book

What it names:

The felt state of a project that still feels alive when the writer returns.

Why writers would recognize it:

Writers often describe momentum emotionally: cold draft, alive characters, dead middle, stale idea, fresh page. The phrase is BuildOS-native but writer-friendly.

Source/community evidence:

- The manuscript communities emphasize feedback, rewrite, and relationships across time, not one-session writing ([Scribophile](https://www.scribophile.com/), [Critique Circle](https://www.critiquecircle.com/landing)).

BuildOS use:

"Keep the story warm between writing nights."

### 4. Continuity Ledger

What it names:

A tracked record of canonical facts: names, ages, eye colors, timeline, magic rules, injuries, promises, places, and events.

Why writers would recognize it:

Fantasy and series writers already talk about story bibles, series bibles, timelines, and worldbuilding. `r/fantasywriters` requires context-rich posts around magic systems, worlds, and excerpts ([source](https://www.reddit.com/r/fantasywriters/about.json)).

Source/community evidence:

- KDP has a specific "Start a Book Series" area in its help navigation, showing series management is a first-class publishing operation ([source](https://kdp.amazon.com/en_US/help/topic/G200672390)).
- Story Grid includes scenes, beats, worldbuilding, and diagnostics as separate craft surfaces ([source](https://storygrid.com/)).

BuildOS use:

"Continuity Ledger catches the tiny contradictions before beta readers do."

### 5. Thread Map

What it names:

The visible network of plot threads, emotional arcs, mysteries, promises, themes, and payoffs.

Why writers would recognize it:

Writers already use arcs, threads, foreshadowing, plants/payoffs, and motifs. The pain is seeing the whole network at once.

Source/community evidence:

- Jane Friedman says query/synopsis materials require writers to express the whole story clearly, including the ending in a synopsis ([source](https://janefriedman.com/start-here-how-to-get-your-book-published/)).
- K.M. Weiland's structure series centers on linked plot moments across a full manuscript ([source](https://www.helpingwritersbecomeauthors.com/secrets-story-structure-complete-series/)).

BuildOS use:

"Turn the manuscript into a Thread Map before writing the synopsis."

### 6. Scene Queue

What it names:

The writer's next-scene runway: scenes ready to draft, scenes needing decisions, scenes blocked by research/continuity, scenes to cut or move.

Why writers would recognize it:

Writers already think in scenes, chapters, and beats. Productivity people say "task list"; writers need "which scene is ready?"

Source/community evidence:

- Story Grid breaks story into beats, scenes, sequences, and diagnostics ([source](https://storygrid.com/)).
- Save the Cat supplies beat-sheet language that many novelists use or argue with ([source](https://savethecat.com/products/books/save-the-cat-writes-a-novel)).

BuildOS use:

"Your Scene Queue shows what is ripe to draft, what needs a decision, and what can wait."

### 7. Revision Runway

What it names:

A sequenced set of revision passes: structure, arcs, continuity, POV, line, copy, proof.

Why writers would recognize it:

Writers already know "revision" is where the book becomes real, but the work arrives as an undifferentiated wall.

Source/community evidence:

- Jane Friedman advises writers to seek constructive feedback and revise before submitting ([source](https://janefriedman.com/start-here-how-to-get-your-book-published/)).
- Reedsy's self-publishing guide treats editing as a core production stage, not optional polish ([source](https://reedsy.com/blog/guide/how-to-self-publish-a-book/)).

BuildOS use:

"Revision Runway turns 'fix the book' into the next pass, the next decision, and the next handoff."

### 8. Critique Packet

What it names:

The context a beta reader, critique partner, or editor needs: draft status, genre, word count, blurb, questions, sensitivity notes, content warnings, feedback boundaries.

Why writers would recognize it:

`r/BetaReaders` requests use manuscript status, word count, genre, title/description, and recommended body details such as blurb, excerpt, feedback type, timeline, and critique-swap availability ([source](https://www.reddit.com/r/BetaReaders/about.json)).

Source/community evidence:

- DestructiveReaders requires submission titles with word count and critiques linked for context ([source](https://www.reddit.com/r/DestructiveReaders/about.json)).

BuildOS use:

"Generate a Critique Packet before asking someone to read. Tell them what kind of feedback you need."

### 9. Query Packet

What it names:

The trad-pub submission bundle: query, synopsis, comps, first pages, agent list, submission requirements, version history, and response log.

Why writers would recognize it:

`r/PubTips` is organized around QCrits and publishing questions, and QueryTracker exists to help writers find agents, track queries, and inspect response data ([`r/PubTips`](https://www.reddit.com/r/PubTips/about.json), [QueryTracker](https://querytracker.net/)).

BuildOS use:

"A Query Packet keeps the book, pitch, comps, and agent notes in one place."

### 10. Drawer-Novel Comeback Mode

What it names:

A guided return to an abandoned manuscript: what still works, what changed, why it stalled, what needs triage, and whether to revive, mine, or trunk.

Why writers would recognize it:

Jane Friedman notes that some manuscripts must be put in the drawer, and many authors do not sell their first manuscript ([source](https://janefriedman.com/start-here-how-to-get-your-book-published/)).

BuildOS use:

"Come back to the drawer novel without reliving the shame spiral."

### 11. Manuscript Weather Report

What it names:

A quick state check: warm/cold, stuck/flowing, scattered/coherent, high-risk threads, last session, next move.

Why writers would recognize it:

Writers describe drafts as alive, dead, muddy, stuck, hot, stale, or flowing. This phrase feels less mechanical than "status dashboard."

Source/community evidence:

- Absolute Write has separate public places for goals, rejection, writer's block, and novels, reflecting emotional/weather-like project states ([source](https://absolutewrite.com/forums/)).

BuildOS use:

"Before you draft, read the Manuscript Weather Report."

### 12. Lore Vault / Canon Board

What it names:

A structured source of truth for worldbuilding, magic rules, history, terminology, places, and series canon.

Why writers would recognize it:

Genre writers already live in lore, canon, and continuity. `r/fantasywriters` is evidence that magic/world/story context is a recurring public need ([source](https://www.reddit.com/r/fantasywriters/about.json)).

BuildOS use:

"Lore Vault remembers the rules so you can write the scene."

## Terms BuildOS Should Redefine

### Writer's Block

Current meaning:

Cannot write; often treated as laziness, lack of discipline, or failure of inspiration.

BuildOS reframe:

"Block is often a context failure or decision failure. The story is not gone; the map is cold."

Use with care:

Some communities defend writer's block as real. Do not dismiss it. Absolute Write explicitly discusses coping with writer's block and says it does not debate its existence ([source](https://absolutewrite.com/forums/)).

### Organized

Current meaning:

Folders, Notion boards, color coding, labels, templates.

BuildOS reframe:

"Organized means you can resume in five minutes."

### Story Bible

Current meaning:

Manual reference doc for characters, places, timeline, lore, series continuity.

BuildOS reframe:

"A story bible should build itself from the work, not become a second book you have to maintain."

### Revision

Current meaning:

Fixing everything after the draft.

BuildOS reframe:

"Revision is a runway of passes, not one giant punishment."

### Critique

Current meaning:

Feedback, often emotionally painful or scattered.

BuildOS reframe:

"Critique is signal that needs sorting. You do not have to obey every note."

### Querying

Current meaning:

Hope, rejection, waiting, and agent gatekeeping.

BuildOS reframe:

"Querying is a packet and a tracker. It is not a referendum on whether you are a writer."

### AI

Current meaning:

Threat, shortcut, plagiarism anxiety, slop, or sometimes tool assistance.

BuildOS reframe:

"AI should not write your book. It should remember the book, surface your own context, and help you make decisions."

## Phrases Worth Testing

- "The book remembers where you left it."
- "Resume in five minutes."
- "Keep the story warm."
- "Stop reopening your manuscript cold."
- "A story bible that builds itself."
- "The drawer novel is not evidence against you."
- "Your critique notes need a sorting room."
- "A query packet, not a panic folder."
- "AI that never writes your book."
- "Your words stay yours. The system holds the threads."

## Phrases To Avoid

- "Write your book for you."
- "Beat writer's block with AI."
- "Just write every day."
- "Produce more content."
- "Hack the publishing industry."
- "Automate your creativity."
- "Generate a bestseller."
- "Scale your novel writing."
- "No more rejection."
- "Never need an editor."
- "Your AI co-author."
