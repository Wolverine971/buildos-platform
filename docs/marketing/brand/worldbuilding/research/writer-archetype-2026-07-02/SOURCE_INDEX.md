## Reddit And Community Sources

### `r/writing`

- URL: <https://www.reddit.com/r/writing/about.json>
- Use: Broad writing community rules, daily thread schedule, AI/self-promo/critique norms.
- Key signals: craft discussion; critique and self-promotion routed to weekly threads; weekly writer's block/motivation, brainstorming, tools; explicit no generative AI rule.
- Best for: Native broad-craft language and community boundaries.

### `r/writers`

- URL: <https://www.reddit.com/r/writers/about.json>
- Use: More permissive writer-sharing community rules.
- Key signals: open critique, resource sharing, no spam, keep submissions writing-relevant.
- Best for: Broader writer support language.

### `r/selfpublish`

- URL: <https://www.reddit.com/r/selfpublish/about.json>
- Use: Indie/self-publishing community rules and norms.
- Key signals: no self-promo outside weekly thread; no AI posts; no surveying the crowd; wiki covers blurbs, marketing, technical questions, companies to avoid.
- Best for: Indie author business pain, self-pub workflow, anti-promo norms.

### `r/PubTips`

- URL: <https://www.reddit.com/r/PubTips/about.json>
- Use: Traditional publishing/query community rules and culture.
- Key signals: QCrit, PubQ, PubTip, News, Discussion, AMA; one QCrit per week; titles need genre/age/title/word count; no self-promo; verified flairs; misinformation removal.
- Best for: Query trenches, query packet, professionalization.

### `r/DestructiveReaders`

- URL: <https://www.reddit.com/r/DestructiveReaders/about.json>
- Use: Harsh reciprocal critique community norms.
- Key signals: high-effort critique, leech tags, word-count critique ratio, no rough drafts, no AI critique, Google Docs norms.
- Best for: Critique trauma, feedback overload, reciprocity.

### `r/fantasywriters`

- URL: <https://www.reddit.com/r/fantasywriters/about.json>
- Use: Fantasy-specific writing/worldbuilding community rules.
- Key signals: critique formats, world/magic system feedback, posts must show prior thinking/research, no self-promo, no AI-generated stories.
- Best for: Worldbuilding sprawl, lore vault, canon board.

### `r/Screenwriting`

- URL: <https://www.reddit.com/r/Screenwriting/about.json>
- Use: Screenwriting norms and possible boundary for separate archetype.
- Key signals: logline/premise/feedback formats, script swap, business posts, no unapproved self-promo, no contest/coverage ads, no plagiarism.
- Best for: Screenwriter-specific future archetype, not core Writer rewrite unless expanded.

### `r/BetaReaders`

- URL: <https://www.reddit.com/r/BetaReaders/about.json>
- Use: Beta reader matching and feedback etiquette.
- Key signals: request title format with status/word count/genre; suggested blurb/excerpt/content warnings/feedback type/timeline/swap availability; no paid-service offers; beta diplomacy; authors should not argue.
- Best for: Critique Packet and beta-reader workflows.

## Publishing And Business Sources

### Jane Friedman - Start Here: How to Get Your Book Published

- URL: <https://janefriedman.com/start-here-how-to-get-your-book-published/>
- Use: Trad-pub path, publishing options, query materials, agent role, rejection/drawer-novel context.
- Key signals: finish manuscript first for novels/memoir; query letter/synopsis/proposal/sample chapters; agents negotiate and advise; some manuscripts belong in drawer; self-pub requires sustained marketing and entrepreneurial mindset.
- Best for: Query Packet, drawer novel, publishing decision tree.

### Writer Beware

- URL: <https://writerbeware.blog/>
