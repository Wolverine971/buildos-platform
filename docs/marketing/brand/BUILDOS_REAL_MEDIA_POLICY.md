# BuildOS Real Media Policy

## The Rule

BuildOS marketing uses **zero AI-generated images** and **zero AI-generated videos**.

This is not just an asset preference. It is part of the market position.

BuildOS should feel real because the proof is real:

- real product screenshots
- real screen recordings
- real footage of DJ using BuildOS
- real photos of the workspace where BuildOS is built and used
- real customer or founder workflows when permission is clear

Do not ship:

- AI-generated product screenshots
- AI-generated BuildOS promo videos
- AI-generated founder photos, avatars, or talking heads
- AI-generated B-roll that implies a real BuildOS workflow
- synthetic office, studio, desk, or workspace scenes
- fake UI that pretends to be an actual BuildOS screen

## Why This Matters

Many well-funded AI companies use synthetic images and videos to borrow a sense of reality. BuildOS should do the opposite.

The counterposition is:

**No synthetic spectacle. Show the actual work.**

BuildOS is a thinking environment for real projects, real workflows, and real people trying to hold complex work together. The marketing should feel material, handled, and lived-in. The product should be seen through evidence, not generated atmosphere.

## Approved Visual Sources

Use:

- screenshots from the current product or a clearly labeled staging build
- screen recordings of actual workflows
- camera footage of DJ using BuildOS
- real workspace photography captured by or for BuildOS
- typography-led cards built from the Inkprint design system
- diagrams, charts, and vector explainers that are clearly explanatory, not fake product proof

AI can help with text seeds, outlines, hooks, or editing prompts. It cannot create the image or video asset.

## How To Present BuildOS

Prefer language like:

- "Here is the actual screen."
- "Here is me using BuildOS on real work."
- "Here is the messy input, and here is the product output."
- "Here is what changed when the project remembered."

Avoid language and visuals that imply:

- "Imagine BuildOS..."
- cinematic AI scenes of productivity
- synthetic people using a synthetic product
- futuristic B-roll standing in for proof
- polished fake environments that make BuildOS feel like a concept instead of a tool

## Asset Rules

- Screenshots must show real UI with realistic data.
- Screen recordings should show the product doing the work, not a simulated animation of the product.
- Camera footage should be real footage of DJ, the workspace, or the workflow.
- Cropping, captions, overlays, zooms, callouts, color grading, and basic editing are allowed.
- If a mock panel is needed for an unreleased concept, label it as a concept or internal mock. Do not treat it as product proof.
- Never use AI-generated image or video as a shortcut for missing proof.

## Review Checklist

Before publishing any visual or video asset, ask:

- Is this real BuildOS, real DJ usage, or a clearly marked explanatory graphic?
- Could a viewer mistake this for product proof if it is not?
- Does this make BuildOS feel more material and trustworthy?
- Does it show evidence instead of spectacle?

If the answer is weak, capture the real screen or real footage instead.
