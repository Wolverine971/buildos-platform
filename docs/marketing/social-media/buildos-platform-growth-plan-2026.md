## Shared Growth Principle

Growth on all three platforms should come from the same core loop:

1. publish proof
2. sharpen the frame
3. engage the right people
4. convert interest into profile visits, DMs, and signups
5. reuse the strongest proof again in new forms

Do not run three unrelated strategies.

Run one thesis across three platforms with different packaging.

---

## Shared Positioning

Across all platforms, BuildOS should be understood as:

**a thinking environment for people making complex things**

The promise is:

**turn messy thinking into structured work**

The contrast is:

**notes + docs + tasks + AI chats scattered everywhere is not a system**

The differentiator is:

**the project remembers**

## Instagram Growth Plan

### Primary Goal

Make people feel the mess -> clarity transformation fast enough that they:

- save the post
- share it
- visit the profile
- understand BuildOS without extra explanation

### Best Growth Formats

- 4:5 carousels
- short proof reels
- story sequences
- behind-the-scenes founder clips

### What Wins On Instagram For BuildOS

- before/after visual contrast
- "do this instead of this" cards
- real product screenshots with clear labels
- workflow transformation
- emotionally relieving copy

### Content Mix

- 50% proof carousels
- 20% short reels
- 15% founder/process
- 15% educational or interactive stories

### Weekly Cadence

- 2 feed posts minimum
- 2-4 stories on active days
- 1 reel if energy allows

### Growth Loop

1. publish a strong carousel with a clear first slide
2. make the caption reinforce the transformation
3. add story repost plus poll or question
4. DM or reply to people who engage meaningfully
5. reuse high-save carousels as reels or updated graphics

### What To Measure

- saves
- shares
- profile visits
- follows from non-followers
- DMs started from stories or post replies

### Instagram Rule

If the first slide does not stop the scroll, the rest does not matter.

## LinkedIn Growth Plan

### Primary Goal

Build trust and category clarity with founders, creators, operators, and AI-curious professionals.

### Best Growth Formats

- founder insight posts
- workflow breakdowns
- strategic category posts
- screenshot-supported explainers

### What Wins On LinkedIn For BuildOS

- calm but contrarian observations
- lessons from building
- "this is what I mean by thinking environment"
- product decisions explained through real use
- comments that invite thoughtful replies

### Content Mix

- 40% category explanation
- 30% founder proof
- 20% workflow breakdowns
- 10% product moments

### Weekly Cadence

- 2 posts minimum
- 5-10 meaningful comments on relevant posts during posting weeks

### Growth Loop

1. publish one thoughtful post tied to the weekly thesis
2. comment early on relevant creator, founder, and AI workflow conversations
3. reply to every meaningful comment on your own post
4. turn good comment threads into future posts
5. repeat the same core category language until people start echoing it back

### What To Measure

- profile views
- comments from the right audience
- inbound connection requests
- DMs
- post saves and reposts

### LinkedIn Rule

LinkedIn is not for sounding smart.
It is for sounding credible, specific, and early on something real.

## X Growth Plan

### Primary Goal

Test hooks quickly, sharpen the enemy, and create repetition around BuildOS’s position inside the AI-native builder ecosystem.

### Best Growth Formats

- short text posts
- quote-style contrasts
- before/after screenshots
- short screen recordings
- founder build-in-public posts

### What Wins On X For BuildOS

- sharp opening lines
- anti-bullshit contrast
- proof in one glance
- casual founder honesty
- replies in relevant AI-builder conversations

### Content Mix

- 40% hook and contrast posts
- 30% proof posts
- 20% founder build-in-public posts
- 10% audience resonance posts

### Weekly Cadence

- 4-7 posts per week
- 10-20 relevant replies across the week

### Growth Loop

1. publish short hook-led posts tied to the weekly thesis
2. test 2-3 versions of the same idea
3. engage under posts where your audience is already talking about Claude, ChatGPT, agents, toolchains, AI workflow pain, and tool sprawl
4. convert strong tweets into screenshots, longer posts, or carousel seeds
5. repeat the winning hooks until they stop working

### What To Measure

- profile visits
- follows from non-followers
- replies from qualified people
- bookmarks
- click-throughs or DMs

### X Rule

Do not try to sound polished.
Try to sound obvious in hindsight.
