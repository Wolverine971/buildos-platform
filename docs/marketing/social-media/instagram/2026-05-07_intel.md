## 7. The `Drafted → Posted` gap

This is the single most important finding in this audit. The pipeline upstream of posting is healthy:

- Discovery is producing real candidates (`2026-05-07_discovery.md` queued 5).
- Warmup is sourcing fresh, high-fit posts (today's morning + evening warmups produced 10 strong drafts across 7 accounts).
- Reply drafting is producing 3-option replies in the right voice (multiple files, all DRAFTS READY).

But the system has produced exactly **1 confirmed posted comment** in 6 weeks. The bottleneck is the human/UI step: actually opening Instagram, posting the chosen option, and updating `comment-log.md` from `Drafted` → `Posted`.

**Implications:**

- The comment-log "Reaction Within 7d" column is uniformly `Pending` — not because nothing reacted, but because nothing was posted to react to.
- Profile relationship-history tables show "No posting confirmation" / "Pending Posting" as the dominant status.
- The compound effect of a peer comment-graph cannot start. This is the actual reason there is nobody at stage_4 yet.

**Fix is not in the system. Fix is in the act of posting.** Two suggestions:

1. **Cap drafts-in-flight per account at 2.** If a profile already has 2 unposted drafts, the next warmup should not draft a third — it should retire one stale draft and pick the new fresh post as the replacement.
2. **Add a posting-day rhythm.** Pick 2-3 posts/day from the evening drafts, post them within the optimal IG window (7-9pm EST per `instagram-voice-quick-ref.md`), then update logs in one short reconciliation block. This is ~30 minutes of work and is the only thing that makes everything upstream pay off.
