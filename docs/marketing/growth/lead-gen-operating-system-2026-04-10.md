# BuildOS Lead Gen Operating System — April 2026

> **Date:** 2026-04-10
> **Purpose:** Define the companion path to activation repair: a narrow, manual lead-generation system that can run now without pretending broad acquisition is ready.

---

## Why This Exists

The growth audit and next-steps doc were correct about one thing:

- broad distribution into a broken activation path is wasteful

But that is not the whole operating reality.

BuildOS still needs:

- qualified people visiting the site
- conversations with likely users
- a campaign system that creates attention on purpose
- a tighter bridge between social warmups, outreach, and actual visits

Right now the missing layer is not another warmup command.

The missing layer is:

**campaign orchestration**

You already have:

- warmup commands that surface opportunities
- outreach strategy material
- growth diagnosis agents
- creator distribution strategy

What you do not yet have is a system that answers:

- which segment are we pursuing this week
- what campaign are we running
- what counts as a lead
- when does warmup turn into outreach
- what CTA are we driving toward
- what did we learn from the campaign

---

## Strategic Decision

Run two parallel motions.

### Motion A: Repair Activation

Keep fixing:

- onboarding Step 2
- welcome sequence delivery
- trial/grace/read-only integrity

This is still the highest-leverage product work.

### Motion B: Run Narrow Manual Lead Campaigns

In parallel, run small, founder-led campaigns that generate:

- qualified visits
- DMs and replies
- user-research conversations
- early users in the right wedge
- proof assets for later scale

This does **not** mean broad top-of-funnel scale.

It means:

- narrow segments
- small target lists
- one proof angle
- one CTA
- explicit weekly review

---

## What Counts As A Lead

BuildOS has four useful lead classes right now:

### 1. Direct user leads

People who could plausibly try BuildOS now:

- authors
- YouTubers
- founder-creators
- podcasters
- newsletter operators
- scattered operators with visible workflow pain

### 2. Audience-multiplier leads

People who can expose BuildOS to the right users:

- creators
- podcast hosts
- newsletter writers
- communities
- collaborators

### 3. Research leads

People whose workflow pain can sharpen messaging, onboarding, or proof:

- near-fit users
- power users of adjacent tools
- people who publicly describe the exact mess BuildOS should solve

### 4. Proof leads

People who can create:

- a case study
- a quote
- a screenshot
- a workflow teardown
- a real example of BuildOS working

If a campaign does not state its lead class, it is underdefined.

---

## The Missing System Layer

Your current system is mostly:

1. scan
2. comment
3. maybe post
4. maybe outreach

That creates motion, but not an actual lead machine.

The better system is:

1. choose one segment
2. choose one pain thesis
3. choose one proof asset
4. run one campaign
5. move warm leads into outreach deliberately
6. review the results weekly
7. either continue, narrow, pivot, or kill

That is the operating system.

## Campaign Design Rule

Every campaign must have exactly:

- one primary segment
- one pain thesis
- one lead class
- one hero asset
- one primary CTA
- one primary channel
- one supporting channel
- one weekly scorecard

If a campaign tries to serve:

- authors and YouTubers and ADHD founders and students
- profile visits and DMs and signups and podcast invites
- LinkedIn and Instagram and X and email and SEO

it is not a campaign.
It is a hope cloud.

## Recommended Campaign Types

### 1. Comment-to-DM campaign

Best for:

- warm relationship building
- smaller creators
- adjacent founders
- people who post openly about workflow pain

Pattern:

1. warmup identifies high-fit posts
2. thoughtful public engagement creates recognition
3. second or third touch moves to DM
4. CTA is a conversation, teardown, or quick walkthrough

### 2. Proof-led content campaign

Best for:

- attracting direct users
- building profile visits
- testing wedge language

Pattern:

1. create one hero asset
2. package it for one audience
3. post it across one primary and one supporting channel
4. direct people to one CTA

### 3. Targeted outreach campaign

Best for:

- audience-multiplier leads
- high-value direct prospects
- partner/outreach motions

Pattern:

1. build a small, high-conviction list
2. warm where possible
3. send personalized outreach
4. follow up with added signal

### 4. Research-to-lead campaign

Best for:

- activation learning
- positioning refinement
- early proof collection

Pattern:

1. invite people into a workflow teardown or research conversation
2. learn their language and pain
3. convert the best-fit people into users or future proof leads

## The Weekly Rhythm

### Monday: Choose the campaign

- pick one segment
- pick one pain thesis
- define the CTA
- define the scorecard

### Tuesday: Build the target map

- identify the target accounts, creators, or prospects
- classify them by lead class
- decide who gets warmup only versus direct outreach

### Wednesday: Ship the hero asset or outreach batch

- publish the anchor post, carousel, video, or teardown
- or send the first outreach batch

### Thursday: Run the bridge

- move strong warmup signals into DMs or email
- follow up on outreach
- note objections and repeated questions

### Friday: Check in

- what shipped
- what moved
- what stalled
- what to do next week

## Metrics Stack

The scorecard should be layered.

### Layer 1: Activity

- comments posted
- replies posted
- DMs sent
- emails sent
- follow-ups sent
- hero assets published

### Layer 2: Attention

- profile appearances
- profile views
- post impressions
- saves
- shares
- reel follows

### Layer 3: Response

- replies received
- DMs started
- email replies
- meetings or calls booked

### Layer 4: Qualified traffic

- visits from campaign links
- visits from profile links
- visits from the right segment

### Layer 5: Activation

- signups
- first brain dumps
- first projects
- onboarding completion

Because activation is still weak, a campaign can still be worth running if it produces:

- strong conversations
- repeated profile visits
- qualified replies
- better proof

But record that honestly.
Do not confuse it with activation success.

## First Three Campaigns To Run

These are the highest-leverage starting points.

### Campaign 1: Author Workflow Teardown

**Segment**

- authors writing or revising long-form work

**Pain thesis**

- the manuscript is spread across notes, docs, loose ideas, and revision fragments

**Lead class**

- direct user leads

**Hero asset**

- messy chapter/revision dump -> structured revision plan

**Primary channel**

- LinkedIn

**Supporting channel**

- email / DM outreach to selected writers and writing-adjacent creators

**CTA**

- "reply if you want me to tear down your workflow" or a lightweight conversation CTA

**Why this is first**

- the before/after is legible
- the wedge is already in strategy
- it creates both direct-user learning and proof assets

### Campaign 2: YouTuber Pipeline Rescue

**Segment**

- YouTubers and video essay creators

**Pain thesis**

- video work lives across idea capture, research, scripting, production, and post in disconnected tools

**Lead class**

- direct user leads plus audience-multiplier leads

**Hero asset**

- raw voice dump for a video -> project pipeline in BuildOS

**Primary channel**

- LinkedIn or X, depending on where the best warm network already exists

**Supporting channel**

- targeted outreach to creator operators or smaller creator educators

**CTA**

- workflow teardown or demo conversation

**Why this is second**

- visually strong
- aligns with the current wedge
- easier to turn into short-form demos later

### Campaign 3: ADHD Founder / Operator Tool-Sprawl Campaign

**Segment**

- founders and operators publicly describing scattered work, context loss, or too many tools

**Pain thesis**

- the problem is not motivation; the stack is fragmented and memoryless

**Lead class**

- research leads plus direct user leads

**Hero asset**

- founder workflow breakdown or personal messy-to-structured example

**Primary channel**

- Instagram or LinkedIn comments-to-DM

**Supporting channel**

- warm outreach to ADHD/productivity adjacent accounts

**CTA**

- conversation, not hard sell

**Why this is third**

- strong affinity wedge
- good for relationship building
- useful for language mining

---

## Operating Constraint

Do not run all three campaigns at once.

Pick one 7-14 day campaign.
Run it cleanly.
Review it.
Then continue, narrow, pivot, or kill.
