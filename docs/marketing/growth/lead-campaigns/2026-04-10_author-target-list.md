## Notes for the record

- Every Bucket A candidate was found in a single LinkedIn Posts search session on 2026-04-10 using the handoff brief's exact queries. The queries that worked best were `"writing my book" revision`, `"writing my memoir"`, `"writing my novel"`, `"writing my first book"`, and `"first draft" "messy" OR "chaos"`. Queries that did not work: `"book project" "scattered"`, `"muddy middle" writing`, `"my book" "keeping track"` -- all returned mostly unrelated business posts.
- Academic manuscript searches (`"revising my manuscript"`) return mostly PhD / postdoc scientific manuscript noise and should be skipped. The productive queries specifically name "book" or "novel" or "memoir."
- The highest-signal pain posts came from non-fiction, memoir, and first-time-author writers. Fantasy/fiction authors post about their process more rarely on LinkedIn (they live on Instagram and X). If Day-7 check-in shows LinkedIn fatigue, the next campaign should extend into Instagram author hashtags.
