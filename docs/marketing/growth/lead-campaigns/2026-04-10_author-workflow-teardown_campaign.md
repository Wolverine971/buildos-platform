## Campaign Summary

Run a narrow, founder-led campaign aimed at authors whose manuscript process is scattered across notes, docs, research, and revision fragments.

The campaign centers on one proof asset:

- a messy author brain dump turning into a structured revision plan inside BuildOS

The primary CTA is **conversation-first**, not immediate signup:

- reply or DM if you want a workflow teardown

This keeps the campaign useful even while activation is being repaired.

## Pain Thesis

The manuscript is not the only thing that matters.

The real pain is that the project lives in too many places:

- notes
- documents
- research scraps
- scene ideas
- revision comments
- character or continuity reminders

The author is forced to remember everything the project should already be holding.

This campaign should frame BuildOS as:

**the place where rough thinking turns into working structure, and the project starts remembering with you**

## Hero Asset

**Important:** This asset does not depend on the onboarding rebuild. The brain dump flow works for existing users. Record as yourself (DJ) using BuildOS. A founder demo is more credible than a product tour for this campaign.

### What to Record

**Format:** Screen recording with voice narration (QuickTime, Loom, or OBS).
**Length:** 90 seconds to 3 minutes.

**The scene:**

1. **(5-10 seconds) Show the messy starting state.** Open a blank brain dump in BuildOS. Have a text file, notes doc, or physical notebook nearby representing scattered source material. Say something like: "I have a chapter I am revising. The notes are in three places. The revision feedback is somewhere else. I am going to dump it all here and see what happens."

2. **(30-60 seconds) Do the brain dump.** Type or paste a realistic author-style dump. Example input:

    > "Chapter 7 needs a complete rewrite. The pacing is off after the reveal in chapter 5. Sarah's motivation doesn't connect to what happens in chapter 12 anymore. I have notes about this in my Google Doc but I also wrote something on my phone last week about fixing the timeline. The beta reader said the middle section drags. I think I need to move the confrontation earlier but that breaks the subplot with Marcus. Also I changed the setting of the warehouse scene and I need to check if that contradicts anything in chapters 3 and 4."

    This is messy. That is the point.

3. **(20-40 seconds) Show what BuildOS produces.** Walk through the structured output. Point out how it identified revision tasks, flagged continuity issues, connected dependencies. Say something like: "It pulled out the revision tasks, flagged the continuity issue with Marcus, and connected the timeline problem to the setting change. I did not have to organize any of this. I just talked through the mess."

4. **(10-15 seconds) Close with the frame.** "This is what I mean by a thinking environment. The project starts remembering with you." Stop. Do not pitch. Do not add a signup CTA.

### What Not to Do

- Do not show the onboarding flow or signup
- Do not explain what BuildOS is in abstract terms
- Do not mention AI, models, or technical architecture
- Do not use a polished voiceover tone -- speak like you are showing a friend

### Three Reusable Forms From One Recording

1. **Full screen recording** (90s-3min) -- LinkedIn native video or link from DMs
2. **Still before/after frame** -- screenshot the messy input on the left, structured output on the right. Clean screenshot pair, not over-designed.
3. **Short clip** (15-30 seconds) -- just the transformation moment (dump going in, structure coming out). For X, Instagram stories, or embedding in outreach messages.

## Target List

Start with a list of **20 targets**. Build the list before the hero asset -- looking at how these people describe their pain will sharpen what the asset should show.

### Bucket A: Direct User Leads (10 people)

Working writers who could plausibly use BuildOS within 30 days. Must meet at least 2 of:

- Currently writing or revising a book, series, or long-form project (mentioned in last 30 days)
- Posts publicly about their creative process, not just promotional content
- Shows visible organizational friction (scattered tools, lost threads, revision chaos, continuity problems)
- Audience size under 50K on LinkedIn or under 100K on Instagram/X (big enough to matter, small enough to respond)
- Not already locked into a heavy Scrivener/Notion/Airtable workflow they are publicly satisfied with

### Bucket B: Audience-Multiplier Leads (5 people)

Writing coaches, editors, newsletter operators, or community hosts. Must meet at least 2 of:

- Runs a writing newsletter, community, or coaching practice with a visible audience
- Posts about writing process, not just grammar or publishing mechanics
- Has promoted or reviewed writing tools before (Scrivener, Notion templates, Milanote, etc.)
- Engages with followers in comments or DMs (not a broadcast-only account)
- Audience size between 5K and 100K (reachable, not untouchable)

### Bucket C: Research / Proof Leads (5 people)

People publicly describing the exact pain thesis of this campaign. Must meet at least 1 of:

- Has posted in the last 60 days about manuscript chaos, revision overwhelm, tool frustration, or "too many moving parts"
- Has publicly described switching between 3+ tools for one project
- Has asked for tool recommendations for managing complex writing projects
- Is in the "muddy middle" or post-draft revision stage and said so publicly

### Where to Source the List

**LinkedIn Search (30 minutes, Buckets A and B)**

Search Posts, filter to past month:

- `writing a book`
- `revising my manuscript`
- `writing process`
- `scattered notes writing`
- `book project`

Search People for `writing coach`.

For each result, check: do they post about process (not just promotion)? Posted in last 2 weeks? Follower count in range? Does pain show in their language?

**X Search (20 minutes, Buckets A and C)**

- `writing a book AND (scattered OR notes OR chaos OR overwhelmed)` -- recent
- `manuscript revision AND (tools OR system OR organize)` -- recent
- `#amwriting AND (too many tabs OR lost track OR scattered)` -- recent
- `series bible AND (need OR help OR looking for)` -- recent

**Instagram Search (10 minutes, Buckets A and C)**

Hashtags and explore:

- `#writingprocess`
- `#writersofinstagram` (filter by recency)
- `#amrevising`
- `#writingcommunity`

Look for people posting about organizational struggle, not aesthetic writing desk photos.

**Reddit and Writing Communities (15 minutes, Bucket C)**

Check for recent posts matching the pain thesis:

- r/writing -- search "organize" or "scattered" or "too many tools"
- r/WritingAdvice -- search "manuscript management"
- r/fantasywriters -- search "world building organization"
- r/selfpublish -- search "project management"

Do not DM on Reddit. Find their LinkedIn or X from profile/portfolio links.

**Your Existing Network (5 minutes)**

Scan existing LinkedIn connections and X follows. Anyone who is a working writer, writing coach, or book editor that you already have a connection to goes to the top of the list.

### List Format

| Name | Platform | Profile URL | Bucket | Last Relevant Post | Pain Signal    | Status                                                                                           |
| ---- | -------- | ----------- | ------ | ------------------ | -------------- | ------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------ |
|      |          |             | A/B/C  | URL or description | what they said | identified / warmup started / DM sent / replied / conversation started / no response / not a fit |

Keep the list small enough to run manually. A Google Sheet or plain doc is fine for 20 people.

## Decision Criteria — Apply on April 17

Apply these rules in order.

### CONTINUE if any of these are true:

- You got 3 or more meaningful conversations (DMs, replies, or outreach responses) from people in the target segment
- At least 1 person asked for a workflow teardown, demo, or deeper conversation
- The language in responses confirms the pain thesis (people said things like "that is exactly my problem" or described their own version of manuscript chaos)
- The LinkedIn post got engagement from writers, not just your existing network

### NARROW if:

- Activity happened but responses came from a different sub-segment than expected (e.g., nonfiction but not fiction, or coaches but not working writers). Narrow to the sub-segment that responded and run another 7 days.
- The post got attention but the CTA did not convert (lots of likes, no replies or DMs). Rewrite the CTA and test a different framing for 7 more days.

### PIVOT if:

- The post got no traction with writers specifically (engagement from general network only)
- Outreach got polite responses but no real workflow interest
- The pain thesis felt vague -- people did not recognize the problem as described
- You could not find 10 people in Bucket A who clearly matched the criteria

If pivoting, the next campaign is the **YouTuber Pipeline Rescue** from the operating system doc.

### KILL if:

- You could not ship the hero asset
- You could not find the target list
- Full week of work produced zero qualified responses
- The author wedge feels fundamentally wrong after trying it with real people

## LinkedIn Post Template

**Structure:**

Line 1 -- Hook with a specific writer observation. Not "writers, are you struggling?" Something like:

> "My chapter revision notes live in four places. I fixed that yesterday."

or

> "A writer friend told me her revision process is 'trying to remember what I already figured out.' That is the whole problem."

Lines 2-6 -- The scene. Describe the actual problem: scattered revision notes, continuity issues across chapters, losing track of your own project. Use words like "muddy middle," "scattered ideas," "external brain," "lost track."

Lines 7-10 -- The transformation. Describe what happened, not what BuildOS is. "I dumped the whole mess into one place. Two minutes later I had a revision plan with the continuity issues flagged." Attach the screenshot or video.

Lines 11-12 -- The CTA:

> "If your draft is alive but your system is not, reply or DM me. I will tear down your workflow for free."

**What not to say:**

- Do not say "AI-powered"
- Do not say "productivity tool"
- Do not say "check out BuildOS"
- Do not use the word "launch" or "announcing"
- Do not tag 10 people hoping they will share it
