## TL;DR (read this first)

Riley is the **#1 target** on the list, but the research changed the pitch. He's not a content creator to "build a workspace for" — he's a **founder building infrastructure that overlaps with BuildOS's agent-host play**. Co-founder of **VibeCode** ($9M raised, ~$10M ARR per Anthropic), and just shipped the **Vibecode CLI**, a multi-agent execution layer that orchestrates Claude Code / Codex / OpenClaw / Cursor / Gemini and persists context across sessions.

**So the play is not "I built you a workspace, your agents forget." That pitch would read as pitching him his own product — or as a competitor fishing.** The play is **builder-to-builder peer**, honest about the overlap, positioning BuildOS one layer _up_ from his execution layer (durable project / goal / human context, not app build-and-deploy). And — good news — **this un-gates him from the MCP dependency.** The peer pitch is sendable now; it doesn't need the cross-agent demo.

## The strategic wrinkle (this is the whole thing)

The Faker doc (2026-05-21) predates the Vibecode CLI and pitched Riley as: _"BuildOS holds project state across your agent tools — your three-agent stack could share context."_ **That pitch is now partly obsolete** — Riley shipped a CLI to do cross-agent execution himself. Sending it would land as either "you're pitching me my own product" or "competitor recon."

How to think about him now — three honest possibilities:

1. **Peer / comparing-notes** (recommended first touch). He's solving continuity at the **execution layer** (build/deploy the app). BuildOS solves it at the **project/thinking layer** (goals, plans, docs, decisions, the humans + agents involved — held durably above any single tool). Genuinely adjacent, not the same. Builder-to-builder respect, no ask.
2. **Distribution partner later.** If the layers are genuinely complementary, there's a "Vibecode builds the app, BuildOS holds the project" integration story his audience would find useful. Months away; only after a real relationship.
3. **Case study / competitive intel.** Even if he never uses BuildOS, how VibeCode positions the execution layer is the sharpest available read on where the agent-infra market is going. Worth tracking regardless.

**Reclassify him out of the "build a fake workspace" Segment A motion.** He's a peer-founder, not a workspace recruit.

### Register note (anti-AI rule)

For Riley specifically, leading with the cross-agent / continuity problem in **technical agent language is the correct register** — he builds agent infra; relief-language aimed at end users would read as naive to him. This is consistent with the agent-host doc's point that the _technical_ cross-agent narrative complements the _emotional_ anti-AI positioning. The anti-AI "lead with relief, not AI" rule governs end-user/general-market copy, not a peer who lives in the agent world. Still: no hype, no "revolutionary AI," no urgency.
