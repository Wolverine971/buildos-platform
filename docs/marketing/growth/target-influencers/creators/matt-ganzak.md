# Matt Ganzak — Target Profile + Outreach

> Stage 1 artifact (per the Faker funnel): research dossier + tailored draft outreach.
> Built off the Riley Brown profile template.

## TL;DR (read this first)

Matt Ganzak is an **AI-business educator/operator with real distribution** — 250K+ newsletter subscribers, a paid cohort program (Sprint), a podcast (_Idea to Exit_), and a studio/agency that ships AI apps for clients. He teaches **non-technical operators to build software that "runs itself" with AI**. That makes him a **two-sided target**: (1) himself a textbook BuildOS power user — newsletter + 4-stage curriculum + podcast + 1:1 advisory + client builds is exactly the multi-project sprawl BuildOS holds; and (2) an **amplifier** — his 250K audience of "build real income with AI" operators is the cleanest mainstream BuildOS persona there is.

**The play is operator-to-operator, not pitch-to-influencer.** BuildOS is not an app-builder and does not compete with Sprint — it's the durable **project/context layer underneath** the AI tools he already teaches. Honest layer differentiation (same move as Riley, milder, since he isn't shipping a competing product). **Sendable now** — a peer-operator first touch needs no MCP demo; the deep "agents read your project across sessions" demo is gated on the MCP server like everyone else.

Tagline: _"Stop Running Your Business. Start Building One That Runs Itself."_ [mattganzak.com](https://www.mattganzak.com/)

## Stated pains (the BuildOS-relevant ones)

- **Multi-business / multi-project sprawl is his literal product surface** — he simultaneously runs a newsletter, a 4-stage cohort curriculum, a podcast, 1:1 advisory, and client studio builds. That is the exact "many projects, one brain" problem BuildOS exists for.
- **His whole pitch is "a business that runs itself"** — relief from operational babysitting. BuildOS's "the project remembers so you don't have to hold it in your head" is the same emotional note one layer over.
- ⚠️ No **verbatim** pain quote captured yet beyond marketing copy ("runs itself," "I'm the bottleneck"). **Pull one sharp line from a recent video/newsletter before sending** — the opener must prove you read/watched something specific, not just his homepage.

## The strategic wrinkle

He **sells** "build software with AI," and his studio builds internal tools/SaaS. So BuildOS could mis-read two ways:

1. **As something he'd want to teach/feature** (the good outcome) — a context layer his students should run underneath the AI tools he already teaches.
2. **As overlap with what he teaches people to build themselves** (the risk) — "isn't this just an app I'd have my students build?"

**Defuse it by layer, up front.** BuildOS is not an app-builder and not a Sprint competitor. It's the durable project/context layer — goals, plans, docs, decisions, the people + agents involved — that the AI tools his students already use read _from_. Different surface. If anything, it's complementary curriculum, not competition.

### Register note (anti-AI rule)

The anti-AI "lead with relief, not AI" rule governs **end-user / general-market** copy. Matt is an **AI educator who sells AI** — talking to him in pure relief-language would read as naive. Use **operator-to-operator** register: specific about his stack and his sprawl, no hype, no "revolutionary AI," no urgency. Middle register — not the deep agent-infra jargon you'd use with Riley/Simon, not the soft end-user relief pitch either.
