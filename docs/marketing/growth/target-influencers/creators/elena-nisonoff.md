# Elena Nisonoff — Target Profile + Outreach

> Stage 1 artifact (per the Faker funnel): research dossier + tailored draft outreach.

## TL;DR (read this first)

Elena is a **tech-culture creator AND a connector** — she built a tech TikTok to ~100K followers, is loud on tech Twitter, and **co-founded Halcyon, a boutique comms/PR firm for Bitcoin, stablecoins, and AI**. That dual role makes her higher-leverage than a pure influencer: she has her own audience _and_ she's professionally in the business of getting emerging-tech founders heard. She lives "at the intersection of culture and the markets."

**The play is creator/operator-to-operator — and explicitly NOT a PR pitch.** Halcyon is a paid firm; leading with "would you cover BuildOS" reads as a prospect, not a peer, and is the weakest possible opening. Lead instead on the **thing she's living right now**: she just got **TikTok-banned and is rebuilding / offloading her thinking to tech Twitter** — a live, specific pain about platform dependency and having somewhere durable to think. **Sendable now** (no MCP needed for a peer/creator touch).

## Stated pains (the BuildOS-relevant ones)

- **Platform dependency + "somewhere to offload."** Her own words after the ban: she _"needed somewhere else to offload"_ her tech thinking, has _"dealt with this issue before."_ That's a near-perfect, verbatim hook for BuildOS-as-durable-thinking-home: the platforms can ban you; the place you think shouldn't be one of them.
- **Narrative/context sprawl is literally her job** — Halcyon crafts narratives for multiple emerging-tech founders at once. Per-client context, story arcs, placements, and follow-ups across a roster is exactly the multi-project surface BuildOS holds.

## The strategic wrinkle (this is the whole thing)

She runs a **PR firm**. Two traps:

1. **Do not pitch her as PR for BuildOS.** That makes you a prospect to her firm (paying customer), which is the wrong relationship and the wrong leverage. If a Halcyon engagement ever makes sense, it's a separate, later, explicit conversation.
2. **Do not pitch her firm as a BuildOS distribution channel either.** Same problem — transactional, premature.

Lead as a **peer who builds the thing she just said she needed** (a durable place to think that a platform can't ban), and as a fellow operator drowning in multi-context work. The amplifier upside (her audience) and the connector upside (her founder network) are _downstream of credibility_, not the opening ask.

### Register note (anti-AI rule)

Elena lives in tech culture and the markets — **culture-fluent operator register**, not naive end-user relief copy and not deep agent-infra jargon. She'll clock both hype and condescension. Specific, dry, a little internet-native. No "revolutionary AI."
