## Key Frameworks / Claims

### Why Organic Social over Paid / SEO / Reddit

- **Organic social**: fast feedback, basically free, universal content plays across TikTok/IG/YouTube.
- **Paid ads**: need $1-2K minimum to run a meaningful test; too expensive for early-stage.
- **Reddit**: less creative freedom (text-only), smaller community overlap; anything you'd do on Reddit you can do on short-form.
- **SEO**: slow burn, good long-term but bad for early validation.

### Algorithm Mental Model

1. You post → algo pushes to a small initial viewer cohort.
2. **Watch time** is the single biggest metric.
3. If the cohort likes it, algo pushes to lookalikes; keeps expanding until watch time drops.
4. Followers get prioritized for the first test cohort; weak follower response makes breakout harder.
5. Whole system is just constant A/B testing of your video against others.

### Account Warmup (3-5 days)

- Scroll like a real user: 10-15 min sessions, 3-4x/day.
- Follow + engage accounts in your target niche.
- Signals to platform "real user," trains algo on what you like.
- If early posts get 5-10 views → shadowbanned → delete and restart.

### Hooks = 90% of the Game

- Spend 80-90% of effort on the first 2-3 seconds.
- Examples he cites:
    - "RIP to all creators using ChatGPT in 2026" (120K views)
    - "Which doctor cheats on their partners the most?" (old AI-PDF app)
    - "Money-saving hacks the wedding industry doesn't want you to know" (POV camera app, carousel)
- Tactics: pose a problem / prey on curiosity / headline like a tabloid. Then the rest of the video earns the right to be watched.

### Video Formats (ranked by his view)

1. **Hook + demo** — shocked face hook, then product demo. Best for conversion.
2. **Talking head** — long-form articulate delivery; good if you can carry it.
3. **Skits** — high virality ceiling, poor conversion (he calls his own skit videos "stupid" and "vanity ego-metric").
4. **Carousels** — TikTok claims 5x engagement vs. video; ideal for AI-generated content since static images are harder to detect as AI.

### Posting Strategy

- Minimum 1 post/day, ceiling 2/day (anything more = burnout + trash content).
- **VC play**: 99% flop, 1% breaks out and drives growth.
- Don't reinvent the wheel — copy working formats in your niche until they stop working.
- When you find something that works, "spam the living hell out of it" (he did this with Autumn Luna / hook+demo).

### Brand Accounts vs. Founder Accounts

- Brand account = dedicated, spammy, pure advertising. No dilution, unlimited posts OK.
- Founder account = builds personal brand, helps, but has pros/cons and caps.
- Strong claim: **brand accounts outperform founder accounts for pure marketing ROI.**
- Run 10-15 accounts; not afraid to delete and restart.
- Content mix options:
    - **Pure marketing spam** — higher short-term, lower long-term shelf life.
    - **Niche content + occasional plug** (e.g., 3 niche / 2 plug per week) — longer shelf life, real followers, more durable.
    - Best case: run one of each.

### AI vs. Human Content

- Algo doesn't care. All that matters is "is it good."
- AI works best in formats where the AI "person" doesn't talk (carousels, still images, reaction faces).
- AI talking-head is where quality tells the audience it's AI → avoid.

### UGC Creator Hiring (Andrew's section)

- **Where to find**: directly on TikTok/IG (free, "diamonds in rough"), or marketplaces Side Shift (big, unvetted) and UGC Tank (smaller, vetted, pricier).
- **Targets**: creators with <10K followers (the big ones have brand-deal saturation).
- **Criteria**: has made viral content for another SaaS/brand, OR personal brand with 5K+ followers that has gone viral at least once.
- **Rule**: "What someone makes right now is what they'll make for you" — don't expect creators to learn new formats.
- **Pay structures**:
    - Base ($25-35/video) + view-tier bonuses (e.g., 10K → $40, 500K → $500, 4M → $1,400; 2-4 week window).
    - Or CPM $1-2 per 1K views, tapering.
- **Interview**: two questions — can they do it, do they want to do it.
- **Red flag**: too many other commitments.
- **Cut benchmarks**: 5 videos → 1K views, 10 videos → 10K views, 20 videos → 100K views. Miss them → cut.
- **Manage**: give them example videos to imitate (don't expect original research). "Creators are lazy" / "no one cares about your brand like you do." Don't nitpick; never say "try harder to go viral" — viral is an outlier outcome, not a directive.

### Rapid-Fire Closers

- Make more accounts, don't overthink it.
- Hashtags and sounds don't really matter.
- Treat each account like a TV show — consistent format so followers know what to expect.
- "You make viral-optimized videos; you don't make viral videos."
