# Clay List — Ranked Targets & Segmented Strategy

_Analysis date: 2026-06-17. Source: 6 Clay/LinkedIn pulls (~295 rows, MD/DC/Baltimore corridor)._

## TL;DR — the honest read

This is **not a creator list**. It's a regional LinkedIn keyword pull. Most "authors" wrote a
business book as a credential (consultants, execs, academics, coaches), not working creators with
audiences. Most "writers" are corporate (proposal / grant / technical). Most "designers" are
agency/studio owners.

BuildOS's wedge = **people actively making something complex who are drowning in their own
thinking.** Only ~15–25 of these 295 truly fit. The smart move is **not** a 295-person DM blast.
It is:

1. **Personalize the ~9 Tier-1 indie working creators** (they have the pain, they'll reply, they
   become testimonials).
2. **Run the famous names as a separate slow reach play** — never templated.
3. **Route the Glen Burnie locals into the in-person testimonial hunt** — coffee, not cold DMs
   (this is DJ's geographic moat).
4. **Keep ADHD contained** as a tiny channel-partner test — do not let it pull the brand back into
   "ADHD tool" while we're repositioning to "thinking environment."

Voice = the **creator/peer-to-peer lane** (warm, human, signed "DJ"), NOT the engineering cold-
outreach voice. Lead with relief, not AI.

---

## Data caveat (do this before scaling)

The export has **no audience signal and no email**. Before any real campaign, enrich for the one
thing that separates "LinkedIn list" from "creator wedge list":

- **Does this person have an audience?** (newsletter, YouTube, podcast, active book sales, big
  following.) Clay can enrich this.
- **Email** for the Tier-1 set (so you're not LinkedIn-DM-only).

Until then, ranking below is a judgment call from title/company/location.

## PLAY D — ADHD practitioners as a **quarantined channel-partner test**

⚠️ **Brand risk.** We're repositioning _away_ from ADHD-heavy framing toward "thinking environment."
These contacts are mostly **providers who serve ADHD clients** (therapists, psychologists, coaches),
not ADHD creators. So:

- **Do NOT** run this as a primary or as user outreach.
- **DO** run a tiny, contained test (3–5 people) of "would you point clients to a tool that turns
  scattered thinking into structure?" — i.e., **referrers**, not users.
- Keep messaging in a separate lane so it never touches the main brand voice.
- Highest-signal if you test: **Kathleen Nadeau** (major ADHD author/clinician), **Lily Dagdag**
  (coach for neurodivergent entrepreneurs), **Lisa Richer** (neurodiversity consulting),
  **Lauren McClenney-Rosenstein** (neurodiversity).

  thinking tool.
