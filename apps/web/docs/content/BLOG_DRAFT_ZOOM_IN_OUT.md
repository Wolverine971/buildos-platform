## HOOK

Watch someone who's great with AI work.

They don't just chat. They **navigate**.

They start with the big picture—"What's the status of this project?"—then dive into specifics—"What's blocking this task?"—then pull back—"How does fixing this affect the timeline?"

In and out. High level. Low level. Strategic. Tactical.

They **zoom**.

And that's the difference between people who get transformative value from AI and everyone else.

---

## THE PROBLEM: FLAT THINKING

Most people use AI like a search engine. One question, one answer, move on.

They stay at one level:

- Always high-level ("Give me ideas for...")
- Always tactical ("Write me a...")
- Never moving between them

This is **flat thinking**. And it's why AI feels underwhelming.

Here's what happens with flat thinking:

- You ask strategic questions but can't execute
- You get tactical help but lose sight of goals
- You have ideas but no implementation
- You have tasks but no alignment

The AI gives you what you ask for. But you're not asking for the right things at the right time.

---

## THE SKILL: ZOOMING

The best AI practitioners have developed a different skill. They **zoom**.

**Zooming in:** Focusing on a specific piece—a single task, a particular problem, one document. Going deep. Getting specific. Working at ground level.

**Zooming out:** Pulling back to see connections. Understanding how this piece fits the whole. Seeing ripple effects. Working at 30,000 feet.

**Dynamic zooming:** Moving fluidly between levels. In one conversation, going from strategy to tactics and back again.

This isn't a feature of any particular AI tool. It's a way of thinking.

But the best AI tools support it. And the worst ones fight against it.

---

## WHY ZOOMING MATTERS

### 1. Context Changes at Different Altitudes

When you're zoomed in on a task, you need different information than when you're zoomed out on a project.

**Zoomed in (task level):**

- What are the specific blockers?
- What's the exact deadline?
- Who's responsible?
- What's the next action?

**Zoomed out (project level):**

- What's the overall status?
- How do tasks relate to goals?
- What patterns am I missing?
- What's at risk?

If you stay at one level, you're always missing something. Strategic thinking without tactical execution is daydreaming. Tactical execution without strategic alignment is busyness.

### 2. Problems Look Different at Different Altitudes

Ever been stuck on something, then stepped back and immediately saw the solution?

That's zooming.

**At ground level:** "This task is impossible. I don't have the data I need."

**At project level:** "Wait—Sarah's task produces that data. We're just out of sequence."

**At goal level:** "Actually, this whole approach might be wrong. The goal is user growth, not this specific feature."

The same problem looks different depending on where you're standing. Zooming lets you find the right altitude for the right insight.

### 3. AI Is More Useful at the Right Zoom Level

Here's a secret about AI: it's not uniformly useful.

**AI at the wrong zoom level:**

- Gives generic strategic advice when you need specific help
- Gives tactical minutiae when you need big-picture thinking
- Feels like it doesn't understand your situation

**AI at the right zoom level:**

- Knows exactly what piece you're focused on
- Brings in relevant context automatically
- Gives responses that actually help

The AI hasn't changed. Your zoom level has.

## THE ZOOM WORKFLOW

Here's how the best AI practitioners actually work:

### Step 1: Start Zoomed Out

Begin with orientation. What's the big picture?

- "What's the status of my project?"
- "How are my Q2 goals tracking?"
- "What should I focus on today?"

This isn't about getting work done yet. It's about understanding the terrain before you dive in.

### Step 2: Identify Where to Zoom In

From the big picture, spot where you need to go deep.

- A blocker that needs attention
- A task that's overdue
- A decision that's pending
- A document that needs work

You're not guessing. You've seen the whole map, and now you know where to focus.

### Step 3: Zoom In and Go Deep

Now focus. Narrow your context to that specific piece.

- What are the exact blockers on this task?
- What decisions are needed for this document?
- What's the next action right now?

At this level, you're doing actual work. Getting specific. Making progress.

### Step 4: Zoom Out to Verify Impact

Before moving on, pull back. Did your work matter?

- How does completing this task affect the project?
- Did solving this blocker unblock other things?
- Are you still aligned with your goals?

This is where you catch misalignment early. Before you've done hours of work in the wrong direction.

### Step 5: Repeat

Find the next zoom-in point. Dive in. Come up. Navigate.

---

## WHAT PREVENTS ZOOMING

### Tool Friction

Most AI tools are zoom-resistant. They give you one level and make it hard to move.

**ChatGPT:** No zoom. Every conversation is flat. You have to manually explain context at every level.

**Document-based AI:** Stuck at document level. Can't see across documents or zoom into sections.

**Task tools:** Stuck at task level. Can't easily see goals or zoom into subtasks.

When your tools resist zooming, you have to fight them. Most people give up and stay at one level.

### Habit

If you've never zoomed, you don't know what you're missing.

You think: "This is just how AI works."

You don't realize: "I could be navigating, not just asking."

Breaking the habit requires intentional practice. Asking yourself: "What zoom level am I at? Should I change?"

### The Illusion of Productivity

Staying zoomed in feels productive. You're doing things!

But without zooming out, you might be doing the wrong things. Efficiently solving the wrong problems. Hitting targets that don't matter.

Strategic thinking feels less productive in the moment. But it prevents enormous waste.

## HOW TO DEVELOP THE ZOOM SKILL

### Practice 1: State Your Altitude

Before asking AI anything, state your zoom level:

- "I'm at project level. Show me status."
- "I'm at task level. Help me with blockers."
- "I'm at goal level. Check my alignment."

This forces you to think about where you are and whether you should move.

### Practice 2: The Three-Level Check

For any significant work session, touch three levels:

1. **Zoom out:** Where does this fit?
2. **Zoom in:** What's the specific work?
3. **Zoom out:** What's the impact?

Don't skip the bookends. They're where strategy happens.

### Practice 3: Set Zoom Triggers

Create triggers that prompt you to zoom:

- **Before starting:** Zoom out. "Is this the right thing to work on?"
- **When stuck:** Zoom out. "Am I seeing this wrong?"
- **When done:** Zoom out. "What did this accomplish?"

Most people only work. The best practitioners navigate THEN work.

### Practice 4: Notice When You're Stuck at One Level

Common patterns:

- Always asking for ideas (stuck zoomed out)
- Always asking for help with tasks (stuck zoomed in)
- Never connecting the two

When you notice, intentionally change altitude. See what's different.

Zooming isn't just about AI. It's about thinking.

The best strategists zoom. They see patterns others miss because they're constantly adjusting altitude.

The best operators zoom. They don't get lost in details because they regularly pull back.

The best decision-makers zoom. They know when to go deep and when to stay high.
