# Instagram Tailoring

Reshape an approved draft into an Instagram **carousel** (best for teaching/argument) or **Reel** (best for story/demo). Instagram is visual-first; the caption supports the visual, not the reverse.

## Pick the format

- **Carousel** — multi-step ideas, frameworks, lists, before/after. High saves and dwell.
- **Reel** — a moment, a demo, a story, a hook that needs motion or voice. Highest reach surface.

---

## Carousel

### Format constraints

- Up to 10 slides (4:5 portrait, 1080×1350). Strongest: **6–9 slides**.
- Each slide is **one beat**: a single idea + a single visual. No paragraphs on a slide.
- Slide 1 (cover) is the entire attention slot — it must stop the scroll and promise the payoff.

### Slide grammar

```
Slide 1  Cover: hook + implied payoff (≤ 6 words on-screen)
Slide 2  The stakes / why this matters
Slides 3–8  One beat each (map the spine's beats 1:1)
Slide 9  Payoff / reframe
Slide 10 CTA: save / follow / comment one word
```

- Carry each `sensory_double_tap` cue onto its own slide (text beat + matching visual).
- Use a consistent visual system (color, type, layout) across slides — the pattern is the brand cue.

### Thresholds

- On-screen text: **3–7 words per slide**; the caption carries depth.
- Cover words: **≤ 6**. One idea.

---

## Reel

### Format constraints

- 9:16 vertical; **15–45s** for hook-driven, up to ~90s for story/demo.
- First **1–2 seconds** are the hook — visual + spoken + on-screen text simultaneously.
- Burn captions; most viewers watch muted.

### Structure

```
0–2s   Hook: gag/image/claim (all three channels at once)
2–S    Body: one idea, fast cuts, on-screen text reinforcing each beat
End    Payoff + single CTA; loop the last frame to the first if possible
```

---

## Caption + amplification (both formats)

- Caption line 1 doubles the cover/hook; it shows in-feed before "…more".
- **Save-bait**: frameworks, checklists, "send this to someone who…".
- **Comment-prompt**: ask for one word or one emoji — low-effort comments lift reach.
- One CTA. Keep hashtags modest (3–5 specific), at the end or first comment.

## Common mistakes

- Paragraph dumped on slide 1 instead of one bold idea.
- Reel hook that starts slow (talking-head intro) instead of in-action.
- Inconsistent slide design that breaks the visual pattern.
- Caption that repeats slides verbatim instead of adding depth.
- More than one CTA.
