# Calm Software Design Review

Use this child when a product should feel quiet, capable, and respectful of attention.

## When to Use

- Reviewing productivity software, internal tools, dashboards, builders, agent workspaces, planning tools, or cognitively loaded workflows.
- The user asks for restraint, calmness, focus, opinionated defaults, fewer distractions, or quality in the Linear/Things/Basecamp sense.
- A delightful or flashy direction would be off-brand or counterproductive.

## Workflow

1. Identify the user's arrival state: loaded, interrupted, uncertain, exploring, or celebrating progress.
2. Remove attention tax: redundant surfaces, noisy states, notification pressure, excessive motion, unnecessary choices, and unclear defaults.
3. Review product posture: opinionated defaults, direct manipulation, predictable navigation, coherent density, and fewer but stronger actions.
4. Check whether quality comes from clarity and reliability before moments of delight.
5. Return subtraction moves first, then additions that make the product calmer or more capable.

## Guardrails

- Do not make calm mean empty, beige, slow, or underpowered.
- Do not add engagement mechanics, streaks, celebratory motion, or novelty unless they serve the user's actual goal.
- Use `delightful_product_review` instead when the context is under-stimulated, consumer-facing, or emotionally payoff-driven.
