# Numbered Fork Library

Load this when silence revival or a fork is the chosen move (workflow steps 2 and 7). One fork per thread, ever.

## Governing Sources

- Steli Efti / Close: the 1-2-3 reply hack, silence recovery, follow-up calendar discipline.
- Connor Murray: strategic cadence and follow-up content rules.
- Austin Schneider / Instantly: volume-mode touch decay.

Number caveat (Steli source analysis): the "457% more replies" and "7%→39%" figures are marketing anecdotes — cite the mechanism, never the numbers.

## The Three Forks

### 1. Qualification fork

First touch when several buyer states are plausible. 3–4 options anchored in buyer pain, not features; promise a tailored artifact per option (1 = teardown, 2 = benchmark, 3 = comparison checklist, 4 = close loop/right owner). No call ask in the same CTA.

### 2. Ghosted-thread fork

After real silence, not as the default first touch. Light tone, dignified exit option, one per thread maximum.

```text
I may be off here, but guessing one of these is true:

1. This is active now.
2. It matters, but not this quarter.
3. You solved it another way.
4. Wrong person / close the loop.

Reply with the number and I will take the right next step.
```

### 3. Objection-discovery / dead-lead fork (Hail Mary)

For churned or long-dead leads. Frame as a feedback request; options = your top known objections + a "no" option. Close's actual subject line: "1, 2, or 3? I can take it." Goal is learning, not revival; every reply is a win.

## Fork Discipline (Steli + Close)

- Options mutually exclusive.
- Real buyer states, not manipulative false choices.
- Always include a no/exit option: "you can work with a no; you can't work with being ignored… If you're open to 'no,' they know you don't plan on being pushy."
- Reply burden = one keystroke.
- Follow through on the promised tailored next step — have a response ready for every number.
- Never two CTAs.
- Don't make the no option embarrassing.
- Do not insult or guilt the recipient.

## Silence and Revival Cadence by Mode

Do-not-import note: list-email cadence advice (Hormozi newsletter material — reward loops, 3x/week rhythm) never applies here; these are cold and post-engagement cadences only.

- **Volume** (Schneider): stop after touch 2; recycle non-responders into a new campaign.
- **Strategic** (Murray): 4 touches over ~7–8 days, then stop.
- **Post-interaction** (they engaged once; Close follow-up guide): 2-7-14-30-day rhythm then monthly, indefinitely until yes/no. Cold = 6 touches max ever; engaged = "keep following up as long as it takes."

Content rules:

- Follow-ups redirect to the original email rather than pitching fresh (Murray: 70–80% of meetings come from follow-ups; "Please give me your thoughts on this" = his highest-reply line).
- Breakup email leverages loss aversion; optional 4th touch only (Close follow-up plan).
- After verbal interest: follow up until booked or declined — assets: promised artifact, short proof, concrete agenda, two specific times.
