# Newsletter Tailoring

Reshape an approved draft into an email newsletter. Email is permissioned and 1:1-feeling: the **subject line + preview text** decide the open, and one clear CTA decides the click. Carries: text + media + links (no link penalty, unlike social).

## Format constraints

- **Subject line**: ~**30–50 characters** (mobile truncates beyond ~40). One idea, curiosity or payoff.
- **Preview/preheader text**: ~**40–90 characters**; it continues the subject — never let it default to the first line of boilerplate.
- Body width is narrow on mobile; short paragraphs and clear sections.
- Links are fine inline; one **primary** CTA button/link plus optional secondary text links.

## Structure template

```
Subject       curiosity gap or payoff, ≤ ~50 chars
Preview text  the second half of the subject's promise
Opening       1–3 sentences, personal, get to the point fast (no "hope you're well")
Body          the spine; short sections, one idea each; carry images/cues inline
Payoff        the reframe or the useful thing they came for
One CTA        a single button/link with a verb ("Read the full piece", "Try it")
Sign-off       a short reply-prompt encourages replies (deliverability + signal)
```

## Hook placement

- The subject line is the hook. Spend real effort here — nothing else matters if it is not opened.
- The first sentence must reward the open immediately; do not bury the lede under pleasantries.

## Thresholds

- Subject ≤ ~50 chars; preview ~40–90 chars and intentional.
- One primary CTA; secondary links allowed but visually subordinate.
- Sections short; respect mobile reading.

## Amplification levers

- **Reply-prompt**: ask a question and invite a direct reply — replies boost deliverability and surface superfans.
- **Forward-bait**: "forward this to someone who…" grows the list organically.
- **One CTA** so the click is unambiguous.
- A/B the subject line when the platform allows.

## CTA norms

- Exactly one primary action. Multiple competing buttons tank click-through.

## Common mistakes

- Subject line written last and phoned in.
- Preview text left as default boilerplate ("View in browser…").
- "Hope you've been well" wind-up before any value.
- Several CTAs competing for the one click.
- A wall of text with no scannable sections on mobile.
