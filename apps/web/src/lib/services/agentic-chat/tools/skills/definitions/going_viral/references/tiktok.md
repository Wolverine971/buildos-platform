# TikTok / Short-Form Vertical — Going Viral Reference

Use this reference when working on TikTok specifically. Most short-form vertical guidance applies to **YouTube Shorts and Instagram Reels** as well — the underlying mechanics (sample-200 test, watch time + completion as #1 signal, the visual → audio → visual sandwich) are identical. Sibling platform-specific divergences are noted at the end. The skill body covers the universal layer; granular hook, script, and storytelling craft lives in the sibling skills `hook_craft_short_form`, `viral_video_script_structure`, and `story_driven_content_craft`.

## Read me first

Short-form vertical rewards **retention and rewatch above all else**. The primary-sourced bar (Jenny Hoyos, who averages ~10M views/Short — see Source attribution) is **90% retention as the bare minimum** for a short to blow up, with her team averaging **95%+**; a sub-30s short needs **over 100% retention** (i.e. heavy looping) or it won't take off. Hook fast, retain through beats every 3–5 seconds, payoff before the loop, and design for replay (last frame setting up first frame on rewatch).

The test: a stranger lands on your video mid-scroll. Can they parse the topic **fast** (Hoyos: "I really do think you have one second to hook someone, especially on Shorts") without context? If no, they swipe.

Hoyos's numbers are **her channel's**, reverse-engineered by scraping/labeling thousands of Shorts — they require per-channel re-derivation, not blind copying. Vendor/portal numbers with no primary source are marked **(internal default / unsourced)**.

## Algorithm reality

**Core principle:**

- Watch time + **retention/completion** are the dominant ranking signal (TikTok creator-portal + algorithm-research consensus; the exact weight % is an **internal default**, not a published figure).
- **Retention benchmark (Hoyos, PRIMARY, her channel):** **90% = bare minimum** to blow up; her average ≈ **95%**; **sub-30s shorts need >100% retention** (looping) or they die. This is why she runs slightly longer (34s) rather than ultra-short — the shorter the video, the higher the retention bar.
- **Scroll-through rate (view vs. swipe-away) (Hoyos, PRIMARY):** average creator ≈ **70%**; her average ≈ **80–85%**; one short hit **92%**. (She says MrBeast ≈ 75%, "so I've heard" — **secondhand/asserted**, keep labeled.)
- **Shares + saves > likes** — saves indicate utility, shares indicate share-payoff (algorithm-research consensus; **internal default**).
- The algorithm tests new videos in expanding tiers (followers/matched non-followers first, then wider). The specific **~200 → 2K → 20K → 200K → 2M** ladder is an **internal default / illustrative**, not a TikTok-published figure.

## Hook architecture (TikTok-specific)

The sibling skill `hook_craft_short_form` covers the six hook archetypes in depth. On short-form vertical, the additional constraint is **hook fast** (Hoyos: ~one second).

**Jenny Hoyos's hook doctrine (PRIMARY — her channel averages ~10M views/Short):**

- _"I really do think you have one second to hook someone, especially on Shorts."_
- **Hook = 3 parts in ONE sentence (Hoyos, PRIMARY):** **power word + action (progression, not a stale statement) + foreshadow** (setup + conflict + resolution-to-come, all in one line).
    - **Power word first** — open with "free / $0 / stole" or any "crazy" word. Avoid stale "This is…" openers (tells the viewer nothing → they scroll).
    - **Progress, don't state** — "I'm building a secret room" (an action) beats "This is a secret room" (a bare statement).
    - **Foreshadow the end** — name what the whole video is about. Worked example: _"Movie theaters are overpriced [hook], so I'm going to make a movie theater at home on a budget of only $5 [foreshadow]."_
- **Hook + foreshadow budget (Hoyos, PRIMARY):** **two lines, ~3 seconds or less**, always **spoken** (VO), in every video.
- **Mechanism (Hoyos, PRIMARY):** a visible progress device that pulls the viewer to a known end — a budget ticking down ($2 on an $8 budget → $5 → …), or "there are 3 steps." She names this as possibly the single biggest reason her videos go viral: _"people have a good expectation of what's actually going to be at the end."_

> **CORRECTED.** The prior "shock → intrigue → satisfy" three-step formula and the "600M+ views in 2024" figure were **not** found in the cited Hoyos interviews. Her actual primary hook template is **power word + action + foreshadow in one sentence** (substituted above). The "averages ~10M views/Short" figure IS in the primary; the specific annual-total was unsourced and dropped.

## The visual → audio → visual sandwich (Kallaway)

How viewers actually consume the first 5 seconds of a vertical video:

1. **Eyes process visual + read text overlay** (~0–500ms)
2. **Ears parse the spoken hook** (~500–2000ms)
3. **Eyes return to the visual** seeking confirmation of what they just heard (~2000–3500ms)

> "Our eyes process 10 to 100 times more information per second than our ears — speed of light versus speed of sound." (Kallaway _100 Viral Hooks_)

**Therefore:** the **key visual** is load-bearing, not the words. Words exist to set up and confirm the visual. If your topic doesn't have a strong key visual, **kill the video**. (Kallaway is explicit about this — manufacture the visual or don't make the video.)

The four hook components (must all agree on the same key visual):

1. **Spoken hook** — the voiceover
2. **Visual hook** — what's on screen
3. **Text hook** — text overlay
4. **Audio hook** — music / SFX

> "The difference between 500 and 500,000 views is unlocking max alignment between those four things." (Kallaway)

For BuildOS-attributed founder content on TikTok, the natural archetypes are **Investigator** (anti-feed observations, model-behavior critiques), **Contrarian** (anti-AI marketing POV pieces), and **Teacher** (context engineering frameworks). Use Experimenter for product demos. Pair Magician (a visual signature — typing in BuildOS, walking, whiteboarding, working on a real project) on top of any of the above to compensate for limited b-roll.

## Killer-script compressed to 60 seconds

The killer-script skeleton (sibling skill `viral_video_script_structure`) compresses for short-form like this:

```
HOOK (0–3 seconds):     5-part intro compressed to one sentence + key visual
BODY (3–45 seconds):    2-1-3-4 ordering applied to 2–3 quick value beats
                         (skip Value Loops; one beat = context + application + framing in <10s)
                         Insert ONE rehook between beats
OUTRO (45–60 seconds):  Fortune Cookie compressed — one-sentence recap + one-line high note
                         Last line sets up first line on replay
```

For 15-second TikToks: skip the rehook; just hook → 1–2 beats → close.

## Format winners (2025/2026)

**What works:**

- Talking head with on-screen text overlay reinforcing the visual (most universal format)
- Demo videos where the screen IS the proof (works for product, code, design, AI tools)
- Walk-and-talk vertical (Pieter Levels-style, scrappy, founder-voice — high authenticity)
- Storytime with cuts (Kallaway-style — story compressed, jump-cuts, escalating tension)
- Visual pacifier underneath voiceover (cup-stacking, makeup, GTA gameplay split-screen, typing — keeps subconscious eye attention parked)

**What's stale:**

- Pure dance / lip-sync (table stakes, low-signal for value-driven creators)
- Memes without commentary (no audience-match data)
- Trending sound chases without topical fit (poisons the targeting)
- Cinematic shots without multiple cuts (MrBeast: "talking to a single camera for too long is boring and causes viewer loss")

## Pre-production order + the autoplay hook (MrBeast, PRIMARY)

MrBeast's pre-production sequence is fixed and explicit (Colin & Samir interview):

1. **Thumbnail → video concept → first 5 seconds → first 30 seconds**, in that order.
2. **The first 5 seconds must look like the thumbnail.** Because feeds **autoplay** (muted) before the click, the opening frame now drives CTR, not just retention — _"I have to visually convince you to click on the video."_ Assume muted: the hook must read with **captions**.
3. **First frame = thumbnail** in a vertical swipe feed — there's no separate thumbnail. **Often remove yourself from the frame** to focus on one person's reaction (vertical can't fit many subjects).
4. **Salesman window:** behave like a salesman until **~the 1-minute mark**; after that the viewer "bought with their eyeballs" — stop selling, deliver the payoff. (For sub-60s shorts this compresses to: hook hard, then pay off fast.)
5. **The dual test at every beat:** _"Am I entertained — can I not look away?"_ AND _"Does this make sense — can people follow it?"_ MrBeast says he no longer looks at retention charts; the craft judgment has internalized the metric. (Legacy benchmark: ~70% retention — **asserted, his channel.**)

## Retention mechanics

Beyond the hook, retention is the entire game. Three rules:

**1. Cut between angles strategically.** MrBeast: "if people aren't clicking away, it means they're entertained." Cut every 1–3 seconds in the body if you don't have visual variety. Even talking head benefits from B-roll cutaways.

**2. Insert retention beats every 3–5 seconds.** A retention beat = a small surprise (visual cut, audio change, on-screen text reveal, escalation, callback). The brain re-engages on each beat.

**3. Open the next loop before closing the current one.** "I tried [X], but then [unexpected thing happened], so I had to..." — the "but/therefore" structure (South Park / Hoyos / Kallaway _Master Storyteller_) chains loops.

**4. Clips are distribution, not theft (MrBeast, PRIMARY).** Encourage reuploads/reactions/clips — a viral clip is "a funnel to your content" / "free billboards." A single craft mastered once can grow you on **Shorts, TikTok, Reels, Facebook, Reddit, and Twitter** simultaneously.

## Sibling platforms

**YouTube Shorts:** largely identical mechanics. Jenny Hoyos's playbook is **YouTube-Shorts-native** (her thresholds above are derived on Shorts). Platform-length divergence (Hoyos, PRIMARY): **YouTube wants slower pace + more story, ~30–34s**; **TikTok wants 10–20s, dense, fewer jokes, dislikes >30s**; **Reels want very visual + subtitles every second.** Same cut can do 1000× differently across platforms — **do not cross-post identical cuts.**

**Instagram Reels:** see `references/instagram.md` for the full breakdown. Key divergences:

- IG ranks on **interaction RATE** (likes-per-impression + **sends/DM shares**), confirmed by Mosseri (PRIMARY). DM shares/sends are a top IG reach driver but **NOT** a primary TikTok signal. (Note: there is no Mosseri-confirmed "watch time #1 / 3-second" rule — that was a removed unsourced claim.)
- IG's **audition/graduation** reach loop: every public post gets a reserved view budget and graduates on rate (Mosseri, PRIMARY).
- Reels default to **muted autoplay** → subtitle every second; high sharability (Hoyos, PRIMARY).
- Trial reels feature (TikTok has no equivalent)
- Carousel + photo posts compete with Reels in the feed (TikTok is video-only); IG format is per-account, not a fixed hierarchy (Mosseri, PRIMARY).

**Cross-posting penalty:** TikTok and Instagram are both widely observed to deprioritize content carrying the other platform's watermark (practitioner heuristic). Either remove the watermark with editing tools OR shoot a native version per platform. Beyond watermarks, Hoyos (PRIMARY) shows the bigger reason not to copy-paste: the **same cut performs 1000× differently across platforms** because length/density/pacing differ — retune per platform.

## TikTok-specific anti-patterns

- **Studio polish without retention.** Native-looking founder-shot footage often outperforms an edited team production. (Direction is sound; the specific "47%" figure was an unsourced vendor number — removed.)
- **Cinematic single-angle videos.** MrBeast (PRIMARY): "talking to a single camera for too long is boring and causes viewer loss." Cut for variety.
- **Watermarks from CapCut, Reels, or YouTube Shorts.** Widely observed to be suppressed as recycled content (practitioner heuristic).
- **Non-vertical aspect ratio.** 16:9 or square content gets reduced FYP distribution.
- **Hooks that need context.** If the viewer can't parse the topic fast (Hoyos: ~1 second) without prior knowledge of you, they swipe.
- **Saving the strongest visual for after the hook.** Lead with the key visual; conceive the hook **visual-first** (Hoyos sketches it like a long-form thumbnail before writing the VO).
- **Ending on a retention cliff (Hoyos, PRIMARY).** The final second is disproportionate — a 70%→45% drop on the last second tanked an otherwise-fine 83% short until she trimmed it. Never end on a drop.
- **Jargon in the script (Hoyos, PRIMARY).** One word ("business/finance/profit") spikes readability past the 5th-grade target.
- **Pure trending-sound chases.** Audience-match collapses; the next several videos suffer.
- **Generic CTAs in the outro** ("Like and subscribe for more"). Native CTAs (Kallaway _Killer Script_) embed in the body — pain raised, resource gives the solve.

## When this is the wrong platform

- **B2B enterprise sales.** LinkedIn / X are higher-conversion for that audience.
- **Long-form ideas requiring >3 minutes.** YouTube long-form is the home for those.
- **Older demographics (50+).** Facebook + LinkedIn skew older.
- **Local services.** Local SEO + Google Maps win.
- **Highly text-heavy content.** Carousels on Instagram or LinkedIn beat text-overlay TikToks.
